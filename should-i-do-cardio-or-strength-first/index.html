Should I Do Cardio Or Strength First The Ultimate Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you confused about whether you should start your workout with cardio or strength training? If yes, you are not alone. This is a common question that many people have. But the good news is that we are here to help you. In this ultimate guide, we will discuss whether you should do cardio or strength training first and why. So, let's get started.

The Pain Points of Choosing Between Cardio or Strength First

When you are trying to decide whether to start with cardio or strength training, you might feel confused or overwhelmed. You might be wondering which one is more effective, which one will help you burn more calories or which one will help you achieve your fitness goals faster. These are all valid concerns, and we will address them in this article.

The Answer to the Question: Should I Do Cardio or Strength First?

The short answer is that it depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to improve your cardiovascular endurance, then you should start with cardio. On the other hand, if your goal is to build muscle, then you should start with strength training. However, if your goal is overall fitness and weight loss, then you can do either one first. It is entirely up to you.

It is also essential to note that doing cardio or strength training first will not make or break your workout. Both types of exercises are essential for overall health and fitness. The most important thing is to find a workout routine that works for you and stick to it.

Main Points to Consider When Deciding Between Cardio or Strength First

When deciding between cardio or strength training first, there are a few key points to consider:

  • Your fitness goals
  • Your fitness level
  • Your workout routine
  • Your personal preferences

By taking these factors into account, you can make an informed decision about whether to start with cardio or strength training.

My Personal Experience with Cardio and Strength Training

As someone who has been working out for years, I can tell you that there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. Personally, I like to start my workouts with cardio because it gets my heart rate up and helps me warm up for strength training. However, I know people who prefer to start with strength training because they feel like they have more energy for it in the beginning of their workout. The key is to experiment with both and see what works best for you.

Cardio or Strength First: Which is More Effective?

Both cardio and strength training are effective in their ways. Cardiovascular exercise helps improve your heart health and burns calories, while strength training builds muscle and boosts metabolism. The best approach is to include both types of exercises in your workout routine for maximum benefits.

The Benefits of Cardio

Cardiovascular exercise has numerous benefits, including:

  • Improving heart health
  • Burning calories
  • Reducing stress and anxiety
  • Increasing stamina

The Benefits of Strength Training

Strength training also has numerous benefits, including:

  • Building muscle
  • Boosting metabolism
  • Improving bone density
  • Reducing the risk of injury

Should I Do Cardio or Strength First for Weight Loss?

If your goal is weight loss, then you can do either cardio or strength training first. The most important thing is to be consistent with your workouts and maintain a calorie deficit. In other words, you need to burn more calories than you consume to lose weight.

Question and Answer Section: Should I Do Cardio or Strength First?

Q1. Can I do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A1. Yes, you can do both types of exercises on the same day. Just make sure to give your body enough rest and recovery time between workouts.

Q2. How long should I do cardio before strength training?

A2. There is no set time limit for cardio before strength training. It depends on your fitness level and preferences. However, a general rule of thumb is to warm up with 5-10 minutes of cardio before starting your strength training session.

Q3. Will doing cardio before strength training affect my muscle gains?

A3. Doing cardio before strength training can affect your muscle gains if you do not have enough energy for the latter. However, if you fuel your body with enough nutrients and rest properly, then you can still build muscle while doing cardio.

Q4. Can I do cardio and strength training on separate days?

A4. Yes, you can do cardio and strength training on separate days. It is entirely up to you and your workout routine.

Conclusion of Should I Do Cardio or Strength First

The bottom line is that whether you do cardio or strength training first depends on your fitness goals, fitness level, workout routine, and personal preferences. Both types of exercise are essential for overall health and fitness. The key is to find a workout routine that works for you and stick to it. Remember, consistency is key when it comes to achieving your fitness goals.

Best Exercise For Weight Loss With Hypothyroidism A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 07, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you struggling to lose weight despite putting in the effort? If you have hypothyroidism, it can be challenging to shed those extra pounds. Hypothyroidism slows down your metabolism, making weight loss a daunting task. However, with the right exercise routine, you can boost your metabolism and achieve your weight loss goals. In this post, we'll discuss the best exercise for weight loss with hypothyroidism and how you can incorporate them into your routine.

If you have hypothyroidism, you may experience symptoms such as fatigue, muscle weakness, and joint pain, making it difficult to engage in physical activity. Additionally, your body may be more sensitive to exercise, and you may struggle to lose weight despite putting in the effort. These pain points can make weight loss seem impossible, but don't give up just yet. With the right exercise routine, you can achieve your weight loss goals and improve your overall health.

The best exercise for weight loss with hypothyroidism includes a combination of cardio and strength training. Cardio exercises such as walking, jogging, cycling, and swimming help to increase your heart rate, boost your metabolism, and burn calories. Strength training exercises such as weight lifting, resistance band training, and bodyweight exercises help to build muscle, which in turn increases your metabolism and helps you burn more calories at rest.

In summary, the best exercise for weight loss with hypothyroidism includes a combination of cardio and strength training exercises. Cardio exercises help to boost your metabolism and burn calories, while strength training exercises help to build muscle and increase your metabolism.

Cardio Exercises

Cardio exercises are an excellent way to burn calories and boost your metabolism. Here are some of the best cardio exercises for weight loss with hypothyroidism:

Walking: Walking is a low-impact exercise that is easy on your joints and a great way to get started with cardio. Aim to walk for at least 30 minutes a day, five days a week.

Jogging: If you're looking for a more intense cardio workout, jogging is a great option. Start with short intervals and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your runs.

Cycling: Cycling is another low-impact exercise that is easy on your joints. You can cycle outdoors or use a stationary bike at home or in the gym.

Swimming: Swimming is a full-body workout that is easy on your joints and a great way to burn calories. If you have access to a pool, try swimming laps for a great cardio workout.

Strength Training Exercises

Strength training exercises help to build muscle, which in turn increases your metabolism and helps you burn more calories at rest. Here are some of the best strength training exercises for weight loss with hypothyroidism:

Weight Lifting: Weight lifting is an excellent way to build muscle and increase your metabolism. Start with light weights and gradually increase the weight as you get stronger.

Resistance Band Training: Resistance band training is an affordable and convenient way to build muscle at home. You can use resistance bands for a variety of exercises, including squats, lunges, and bicep curls.

Bodyweight Exercises: Bodyweight exercises such as push-ups, squats, and lunges are an excellent way to build muscle and increase your metabolism without any equipment.

Tips for Exercising with Hypothyroidism

When you have hypothyroidism, it's essential to approach exercise with caution. Here are some tips to keep in mind when exercising:

Start Slow: If you're new to exercise or haven't exercised in a while, start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.

Listen to Your Body: Pay attention to how your body feels during and after exercise. If you experience any pain or discomfort, stop exercising and rest.

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water before, during, and after exercise to stay hydrated.

Get Enough Rest: Rest is crucial for recovery and to prevent injury. Aim for at least seven to eight hours of sleep each night and take rest days as needed.

Personal Experience

As someone who has struggled with weight loss due to hypothyroidism, I can attest to the effectiveness of the exercises mentioned above. Incorporating a combination of cardio and strength training into my routine has helped me lose weight and improve my overall health. I started with walking and bodyweight exercises and gradually increased the intensity and duration of my workouts. It's essential to be patient and consistent and to listen to your body and make adjustments as needed.

Question and Answer

  1. Can strength training help me lose weight with hypothyroidism?
    Yes, strength training can help you lose weight with hypothyroidism by building muscle and increasing your metabolism.
  2. How often should I exercise to lose weight with hypothyroidism?
    Aim to exercise for at least 30 minutes a day, five days a week. However, the frequency and duration of your workouts may vary depending on your individual needs and preferences.
  3. Can I do high-intensity interval training (HIIT) with hypothyroidism?
    Yes, you can do HIIT with hypothyroidism, but it's essential to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. It's also a good idea to work with a certified personal trainer who can help you create a safe and effective workout plan.
  4. What should I eat before and after exercise with hypothyroidism?
    It's essential to fuel your body with healthy foods before and after exercise. Aim to eat a balanced meal that includes protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats before exercise, and eat a snack that includes protein and carbohydrates after exercise.

Conclusion of Best Exercise for Weight Loss with Hypothyroidism

While weight loss can be challenging with hypothyroidism, it's not impossible. By incorporating a combination of cardio and strength training exercises into your routine and following the tips mentioned above, you can boost your metabolism and achieve your weight loss goals. Remember to start slow, listen to your body, and be patient and consistent. With time and effort, you can improve your overall health and wellbeing.

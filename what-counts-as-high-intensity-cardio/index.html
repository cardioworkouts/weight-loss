What Counts As High Intensity Cardio

Written by Luffy Jun 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering what type of cardio exercise counts as high intensity? Do you want to get the most out of your workouts but not sure where to start? You're not alone. Many people struggle to understand what high intensity cardio is and how to incorporate it into their fitness routine. In this tutorial, we'll cover everything you need to know about what counts as high intensity cardio.

If you're looking to lose weight or improve your overall fitness level, high intensity cardio may be the way to go. However, it's important to understand that high intensity cardio can be challenging and even uncomfortable at times. You may experience shortness of breath, fatigue, and muscle soreness. But don't worry, these are all normal reactions to pushing your body to its limits.

High intensity cardio is any type of cardiovascular exercise that raises your heart rate to 80-90% of your maximum heart rate. This can include exercises such as sprinting, jumping jacks, burpees, and high-intensity interval training (HIIT). These exercises are designed to be short and intense bursts of energy, rather than long, steady-state cardio sessions.

In summary, high intensity cardio is a type of cardiovascular exercise that challenges your body and raises your heart rate to 80-90% of your maximum heart rate. It includes exercises such as sprinting, jumping jacks, burpees, and HIIT. Incorporating high intensity cardio into your fitness routine can help you achieve your weight loss and fitness goals.

Benefits of High Intensity Cardio

When I started incorporating high intensity cardio into my fitness routine, I noticed a significant improvement in my overall fitness level. My endurance increased, and I was able to push myself harder during my workouts. Additionally, high intensity cardio can help you burn more calories in a shorter amount of time compared to steady-state cardio.

How to Incorporate High Intensity Cardio into Your Routine

There are many ways to incorporate high intensity cardio into your fitness routine. One popular method is HIIT, which involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest. For example, you might sprint for 30 seconds, then rest for 30 seconds, and repeat for several rounds. Another option is to add high intensity exercises, such as jumping jacks or burpees, into your existing workout routine.

Important Considerations

It's important to remember that high intensity cardio can be challenging, and it's not recommended for everyone. If you're new to exercise or have any health concerns, it's important to consult with your doctor before starting a high intensity cardio program. Additionally, it's important to listen to your body and take breaks when needed.

Tips for Success

If you're new to high intensity cardio, start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. Make sure to warm up before your workout and cool down afterwards. Additionally, it's important to stay hydrated and fuel your body with nutritious foods.

My Personal Experience with High Intensity Cardio

When I first started incorporating high intensity cardio into my fitness routine, I was nervous about pushing myself too hard. However, with the help of a personal trainer, I was able to safely and gradually increase the intensity of my workouts. I noticed a significant improvement in my endurance and overall fitness level. While high intensity cardio can be challenging, the results are worth it.

Question and Answer

Q: Can high intensity cardio be done every day?

A: It's not recommended to do high intensity cardio every day, as your body needs time to rest and recover. Aim to incorporate high intensity cardio into your fitness routine 2-3 times per week.

Q: Is high intensity cardio suitable for beginners?

A: High intensity cardio can be challenging for beginners, and it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts. If you're new to exercise, it's recommended to start with low-impact cardio, such as walking or swimming, before incorporating high intensity exercises.

Q: Can high intensity cardio help with weight loss?

A: Yes, high intensity cardio can help with weight loss by burning more calories in a shorter amount of time compared to steady-state cardio. Additionally, high intensity cardio can help increase your metabolism and improve your body's fat-burning ability.

Q: How long should a high intensity cardio workout be?

A: A high intensity cardio workout should last between 20-30 minutes, including warm-up and cool-down periods. It's important to listen to your body and take breaks when needed.

Conclusion of What Counts as High Intensity Cardio

In conclusion, high intensity cardio is a type of cardiovascular exercise that challenges your body and raises your heart rate to 80-90% of your maximum heart rate. It includes exercises such as sprinting, jumping jacks, burpees, and HIIT. Incorporating high intensity cardio into your fitness routine can help you achieve your weight loss and fitness goals. Remember to start slowly, listen to your body, and stay hydrated for a successful high intensity cardio workout.

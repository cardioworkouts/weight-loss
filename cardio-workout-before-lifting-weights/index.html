Why You Should Do Cardio Workout Before Lifting Weights

Written by Frank Jun 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Working out can be challenging, especially when you don't know where to start. One common question among fitness enthusiasts is whether to do cardio before or after lifting weights. While some prefer to lift weights first, doing cardio workout before lifting weights can provide you with numerous benefits that you don't want to miss out on.

The main target of cardio workout before lifting weights is to prepare your body for the intense workout that follows. It helps to increase your body temperature, heart rate, and blood flow, which can reduce the risk of injury during lifting weights.

In this post, we'll dive deeper into the benefits of cardio workout before lifting weights, and why you should make it a part of your fitness routine.

The Benefits of Cardio Workout Before Lifting Weights

For me, cardio workout before lifting weights has been a game-changer. Not only does it help me avoid injuries, but it also increases my energy levels and improves my overall workout performance.

Here are some of the benefits of cardio workout before lifting weights:

1. Increases Energy Levels

Cardio workout before lifting weights helps to increase your energy levels by boosting your heart rate and blood flow. This, in turn, improves the delivery of oxygen and nutrients to your muscles, allowing you to perform better during your workout.

2. Reduces Risk of Injury

When you do cardio workout before lifting weights, you prepare your muscles for the intense workout that follows. This helps to reduce the risk of injury by warming up your muscles and increasing their flexibility.

3. Improves Workout Performance

Cardio workout before lifting weights helps to improve your overall workout performance by increasing your endurance and stamina. This allows you to push yourself harder during your lifting weights workout and achieve better results.

How to Incorporate Cardio Workout Before Lifting Weights into Your Routine

Now that you know the benefits of cardio workout before lifting weights, you're probably wondering how to incorporate it into your fitness routine. Here are some tips:

1. Start with a Warm-Up

Before you start your cardio workout, make sure to warm up your muscles by doing some stretching exercises. This will help to prevent injuries and prepare your body for the workout ahead.

2. Choose Your Cardio Workout

There are various cardio exercises that you can choose from, such as running, cycling, jumping rope, or using a treadmill. Choose the one that you enjoy the most and that suits your fitness level.

3. Set a Time Limit

Start with a 10-15 minute cardio workout before lifting weights, then gradually increase the time as your fitness level improves. You don't want to exhaust yourself before your lifting weights workout.

FAQs: Cardio Workout Before Lifting Weights

Here are some common questions about cardio workout before lifting weights:

Q: Do I need to do cardio workout every time I lift weights?

A: It's not necessary to do cardio workout every time you lift weights, but it's recommended to do it at least 2-3 times a week to maximize the benefits.

Q: How long should I wait after cardio workout to lift weights?

A: You should wait at least 10-15 minutes after cardio workout before lifting weights to allow your muscles to recover and avoid fatigue.

Q: Can I do cardio workout and lift weights on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio workout and lift weights on the same day, but it's recommended to do cardio workout before lifting weights to avoid injury and maximize the benefits.

Q: What's the best time to do cardio workout before lifting weights?

A: The best time to do cardio workout before lifting weights is in the morning, as it helps to boost your energy levels and metabolism throughout the day.

Conclusion of Cardio Workout Before Lifting Weights

Cardio workout before lifting weights is a great way to prepare your body for the intense workout that follows. It helps to increase your energy levels, reduce the risk of injury, and improve your overall workout performance. By incorporating cardio workout before lifting weights into your fitness routine, you can achieve better results and reach your fitness goals faster.

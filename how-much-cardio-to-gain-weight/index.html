How Much Cardio To Gain Weight A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Oct 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who has been struggling to gain weight despite eating a lot? Have you been told to incorporate cardio into your workout routine to shed off some pounds? If so, you might be wondering how much cardio to do to gain weight. In this blog post, we will discuss everything you need to know about how much cardio to gain weight and related keywords.

The Pain Points of How Much Cardio to Gain Weight

One of the most common misconceptions in the fitness industry is that cardio is only for people who want to lose weight. While cardio can help you burn calories and lose weight, it is not necessarily a bad thing for people who want to gain weight. However, doing too much cardio can impede your progress in gaining weight.

Answering the Target of How Much Cardio to Gain Weight

Now, let's answer the question on everyone's mind: how much cardio should you do if you want to gain weight? The answer is: it depends. The amount of cardio you should do depends on your goals, body type, and current fitness level.

If your goal is to gain weight, you should focus on strength training and weightlifting. These activities help build muscle mass, which is essential for weight gain. Cardio should be used as a complementary activity to improve your cardiovascular health and burn some calories.

If you are a beginner, start by doing 20-30 minutes of cardio 2-3 times a week. As you progress, you can increase the duration and frequency of your cardio sessions, but make sure not to overdo it. Doing too much cardio can lead to muscle loss and hinder your progress in gaining weight.

Summary of How Much Cardio to Gain Weight

In summary, cardio can be beneficial for people who want to gain weight, but it should not be the primary focus of your workout routine. Instead, focus on strength training and weightlifting to build muscle mass. Start with 20-30 minutes of cardio 2-3 times a week and gradually increase the duration and frequency as you progress.

Personal Experience with How Much Cardio to Gain Weight

As someone who has struggled with gaining weight, I used to incorporate a lot of cardio into my workout routine. However, I soon realized that doing too much cardio was hindering my progress. Once I switched to a strength training-focused routine and reduced my cardio, I started seeing significant improvements in my muscle mass and overall weight gain.

The Importance of Strength Training for Weight Gain

Strength training is crucial for weight gain because it helps build muscle mass. When you lift weights, you create tiny tears in your muscle fibers. These tears are then repaired during the recovery period, which leads to muscle growth. By incorporating strength training into your workout routine, you are essentially creating a stimulus for muscle growth.

The Different Types of Strength Training

There are various types of strength training exercises you can do to build muscle mass. Some popular ones include:

  • Squats
  • Bench press
  • Deadlifts
  • Shoulder press
  • Pull-ups

How to Incorporate Strength Training into Your Workout Routine

If you are new to strength training, it is best to start with a beginner's routine that focuses on the major muscle groups. As you progress, you can start incorporating more advanced exercises and split routines.

It is also essential to vary your routine and increase the weights you lift progressively. This helps prevent plateaus and ensures that your muscles are continually challenged.

Cardio as a Complementary Activity

While cardio should not be the primary focus of your workout routine, it can still be beneficial as a complementary activity. Cardio helps improve your cardiovascular health, which is essential for overall health and fitness. It can also help burn some calories, which can be helpful if you are in a calorie surplus and want to prevent excessive weight gain.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio and still gain weight?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and still gain weight. However, it is important to focus on strength training and weightlifting as the primary activities in your workout routine.

Q: How much cardio is too much?

A: Too much cardio can impede your progress in gaining weight. Start with 20-30 minutes of cardio 2-3 times a week and gradually increase the duration and frequency as you progress.

Q: What are some good strength training exercises for weight gain?

A: Some popular strength training exercises for weight gain include squats, bench press, deadlifts, shoulder press, and pull-ups.

Q: Can I still do cardio if I want to gain weight?

A: Yes, you can still do cardio if you want to gain weight. However, make sure to focus on strength training and weightlifting as the primary activities in your workout routine.

Conclusion of How Much Cardio to Gain Weight

In conclusion, cardio can be beneficial for people who want to gain weight, but it should not be the primary focus of your workout routine. Instead, focus on strength training and weightlifting to build muscle mass. Start with 20-30 minutes of cardio 2-3 times a week and gradually increase the duration and frequency as you progress. Remember that everyone's body is different, so it is essential to listen to your body and adjust your workout routine accordingly.

Best Low Impact Cardio Online A Beginner s Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for a way to get your heart rate up and burn calories without putting too much stress on your joints? If so, low impact cardio might be the perfect solution for you. However, with so many options for cardio workouts available online, it can be overwhelming to choose the best one for your needs. In this post, we'll explore the best low impact cardio workouts you can find online, so you can get started on your fitness journey with confidence.

Low impact cardio is ideal for people who want to get their heart rate up and burn calories, but may have joint pain or other physical limitations that make high-impact exercises such as running or jumping difficult. Low-impact exercises are gentler on the body, making them a great option for beginners or those recovering from an injury.

The best low impact cardio workouts you can find online include walking, cycling, swimming, dancing, and yoga. These workouts can be done in the comfort of your own home, making them convenient and accessible for everyone.

In summary, low impact cardio is a great way to get your heart rate up and burn calories without putting too much strain on your joints. Walking, cycling, swimming, dancing, and yoga are all excellent low impact cardio workouts you can find online.

Walking

Walking is one of the most accessible low impact cardio workouts you can do. All you need is a pair of comfortable shoes and a safe place to walk, whether it's around your neighborhood or on a treadmill. Walking is a great way to burn calories, improve your cardiovascular health, and reduce stress.

Personally, I love going for a walk in the morning to start my day off on the right foot. It's a great way to clear my mind and get some fresh air before I start my workday. I also like to listen to music or a podcast while I walk, which makes the time go by faster.

Cycling

Cycling is another low impact cardio workout that's easy to do at home with a stationary bike or outside on a regular bike. Cycling is a great way to improve your cardiovascular health, build leg strength, and burn calories. It's also a low-impact exercise, making it a great option for people with joint pain or injuries.

Personally, I enjoy cycling outside on a sunny day. It's a great way to explore your local area and get some fresh air while getting a great workout in. I also like to challenge myself by increasing the resistance on my bike or trying a new route.

Swimming

Swimming is a fantastic low impact cardio workout that's gentle on the joints and great for people with injuries or chronic pain. Swimming is also a full-body workout, making it a great way to improve your cardiovascular health, build muscle, and burn calories. If you don't have access to a pool, there are plenty of online swimming workouts you can do in your own bathtub or an inflatable pool.

Dancing

Dancing is a fun and low impact cardio workout that's great for people of all ages and fitness levels. You can find many dance fitness classes online, from hip-hop to salsa to Zumba. Dancing is a great way to improve your cardiovascular health, coordination, and balance while having fun.

Conclusion of Best Low Impact Cardio Online

Low impact cardio is a great way to improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and reduce stress without putting too much strain on your joints. Walking, cycling, swimming, dancing, and yoga are all excellent low impact cardio workouts you can find online. By incorporating these workouts into your fitness routine, you can improve your overall health and well-being while having fun.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I still lose weight with low impact cardio?

A: Yes, you can still lose weight with low impact cardio. While low impact cardio burns fewer calories than high-impact exercises, it can still help you create a calorie deficit and lose weight when combined with a healthy diet.

Q: Is low impact cardio suitable for seniors?

A: Yes, low impact cardio is suitable for seniors as it's gentle on the joints and can improve cardiovascular health without putting too much strain on the body. However, it's always important to consult your doctor before starting a new exercise routine.

Q: How often should I do low impact cardio?

A: It's recommended to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week, which can be broken down into 30 minutes a day, five days a week. However, it's important to listen to your body and not overdo it, especially if you're just starting out.

Q: Can I do low impact cardio if I have an injury?

A: Yes, low impact cardio is often recommended for people with injuries as it's gentler on the joints and can help with recovery. However, it's important to consult your doctor or physical therapist before starting a new exercise routine.

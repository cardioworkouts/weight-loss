Can I Do Hiit Before Weight Training

Written by Frank Nov 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking to maximize the benefits of your workouts by combining HIIT and weight training? If so, you may be wondering if it's safe to do HIIT before weight training. In this post, we'll explore this question and share some tips on how to safely incorporate both types of exercise into your routine.

Pain Points

Many people are hesitant to do HIIT before weight training because they're afraid it will negatively impact their strength or endurance during their weight lifting session. Likewise, some people are worried that doing weight training before HIIT will make them too tired to complete their high-intensity interval training.

Can I Do HIIT Before Weight Training?

The short answer is yes, you can do HIIT before weight training. In fact, there are some benefits to doing so. For example, HIIT can help get your heart rate up and warm up your muscles, which can prepare them for weight training. Additionally, doing HIIT first can help you burn more calories overall, which can be beneficial if you're trying to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular fitness.

That being said, it's important to approach this type of workout with caution. HIIT can be very intense, and doing it before weight training can increase your risk of injury if you're not careful. Additionally, if you're not used to doing HIIT, you may not have the energy or stamina to complete your weight lifting session afterwards.

Benefits of HIIT Before Weight Training

As mentioned earlier, doing HIIT before weight training can provide some benefits. For example, it can help you warm up your muscles and get your heart rate up, which can improve your overall performance during your weight lifting session. Additionally, doing HIIT first can help you burn more calories overall, which can be beneficial if you're trying to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular fitness.

Another benefit of doing HIIT before weight training is that it can help you increase your anaerobic capacity. HIIT is a form of anaerobic exercise, which means that it requires short bursts of intense activity that use up your body's stored energy. By doing HIIT before weight training, you can help improve your body's ability to perform high-intensity activities without getting tired too quickly.

How to Safely Incorporate HIIT Before Weight Training

If you're interested in doing HIIT before weight training, here are some tips to help you do so safely:

  • Start slow: If you're new to HIIT, start with shorter sessions and gradually build up your intensity over time.
  • Warm up: Always warm up before you start your HIIT session. This can include light cardio, dynamic stretching, or mobility exercises.
  • Use proper form: For any weight lifting exercises you do after your HIIT session, make sure to use proper form to avoid injury.
  • Listen to your body: If you're feeling too tired or sore to complete your weight lifting session after your HIIT workout, it's okay to take a break or adjust your workout accordingly.

How to Recover After HIIT and Weight Training

After you've completed your HIIT and weight lifting sessions, it's important to take time to recover properly. Here are some tips to help you recover after your workout:

  • Stretch: Take some time to stretch out your muscles after your workout. This can help improve your flexibility and reduce your risk of injury.
  • Rest: Give your body time to rest and recover after your workout. This can include taking a rest day, getting enough sleep, and eating a balanced diet.
  • Hydrate: Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout to help keep your body hydrated.

Conclusion of Can I Do HIIT Before Weight Training

So, can you do HIIT before weight training? Yes, you can! However, it's important to approach this type of workout with caution and make sure you're listening to your body. By using proper form, warming up properly, and gradually building up your intensity, you can safely incorporate HIIT into your weight training routine and reap the benefits of both types of exercise.

Question and Answer

Q. Can doing HIIT before weight training help me burn more calories?

A. Yes, doing HIIT before weight training can help you burn more calories overall. This is because HIIT is a form of high-intensity exercise that can increase your heart rate and metabolic rate, which can help you burn more calories throughout the day.

Q. Is it safe to do HIIT before weight training?

A. Yes, it can be safe to do HIIT before weight training as long as you approach it with caution and listen to your body. It's important to warm up properly, use proper form during weight lifting exercises, and start with shorter sessions if you're new to HIIT.

Q. Will doing HIIT before weight training impact my strength training performance?

A. It's possible that doing HIIT before weight training could impact your performance during your strength training session. However, if you warm up properly and use proper form, you should be able to minimize any negative effects.

Q. How often should I do HIIT before weight training?

A. The frequency with which you do HIIT before weight training will depend on your fitness goals and current level of fitness. It's generally recommended to do HIIT no more than 2-3 times per week, and to give your body time to rest and recover between sessions.

Best Cardio To Do In The Gym A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to improve your cardiovascular health and burn some calories at the same time? If yes, then doing cardio in the gym is the perfect way to achieve your goals. However, with so many options available, it can be overwhelming to decide which type of cardio to do. In this guide, we will discuss the best cardio to do in the gym and how to get the most out of your workouts.

Are you tired of doing the same cardio routine every day? Do you want to try something new and challenging? Are you unsure which type of cardio is best for your fitness level? If you answered yes to any of these questions, then you are not alone. Many people struggle with finding the right type of cardio that suits their needs and preferences.

The best cardio to do in the gym depends on your fitness level, goals, and personal preference. However, some of the most effective types of cardio workouts are running on a treadmill, cycling on a stationary bike, using an elliptical machine, and rowing. These exercises are low-impact, easy to learn, and provide a full-body workout.

In summary, the best cardio to do in the gym are running on a treadmill, cycling on a stationary bike, using an elliptical machine, and rowing. These exercises are effective, low-impact, and provide a full-body workout. Now, let's dive deeper into each of these exercises and how to get the most out of your cardio workouts.

Running on a Treadmill

Running on a treadmill is a great way to improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and build endurance. This exercise is perfect for people of all fitness levels, from beginners to advanced runners. To get the most out of your treadmill workout, start with a warm-up, gradually increase your speed and incline, and finish with a cool-down.

Cycling on a Stationary Bike

Cycling on a stationary bike is a low-impact exercise that is perfect for people with joint pain or injuries. This exercise is also great for building endurance and burning calories. To get the most out of your stationary bike workout, adjust the resistance to your fitness level, vary your speed and intensity, and focus on maintaining proper form.

Using an Elliptical Machine

Using an elliptical machine is a low-impact exercise that is perfect for people with joint pain or injuries. This exercise is also great for building endurance and burning calories. To get the most out of your elliptical workout, vary your resistance and incline, use the handles to engage your upper body, and focus on maintaining proper form.

Rowing

Rowing is a full-body workout that targets your upper and lower body muscles. This exercise is perfect for people who want a challenging and effective workout. To get the most out of your rowing workout, start with a warm-up, focus on maintaining proper form, vary your speed and intensity, and finish with a cool-down.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio in the gym?

A: It is recommended to do cardio workouts at least three to four times a week for optimal results.

Q: How long should my cardio workouts be?

A: It is recommended to do cardio workouts for at least 30 minutes per session, but you can gradually increase the duration as you progress.

Q: Should I do cardio before or after weightlifting?

A: It is recommended to do cardio after weightlifting to avoid fatigue and injuries during weightlifting exercises.

Q: How can I make my cardio workouts more challenging?

A: You can make your cardio workouts more challenging by increasing the resistance, speed, or incline of your exercise machine, or by incorporating high-intensity interval training (HIIT) into your workouts.

Conclusion of Best Cardio to Do in the Gym

Cardio workouts are essential for improving your cardiovascular health, burning calories, and building endurance. By incorporating running on a treadmill, cycling on a stationary bike, using an elliptical machine, and rowing into your gym routine, you can achieve your fitness goals and enjoy a challenging and effective workout. Remember to start with a warm-up, gradually increase your intensity, and finish with a cool-down to get the most out of your cardio workouts.

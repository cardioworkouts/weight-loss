Should You Do Cardio Before Or After Lifting For Weight Loss

Written by Luffy Jul 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who wants to lose weight but is confused about whether to do cardio before or after lifting? If yes, then you are not alone. Many people are struggling with this question, and it can be overwhelming to find the right answer. In this article, we will explore the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio before or after lifting for weight loss.

Weight loss is a common goal for many people, but it can be challenging to achieve. One of the main questions people often ask is whether to do cardio before or after lifting. This is a pain point for many because they are unsure of what is best for them.

The answer to whether you should do cardio before or after lifting for weight loss is that it depends on your goals. If your primary goal is weight loss, then doing cardio before lifting can be more effective. However, if you are looking to gain muscle or increase strength, then lifting before cardio may be better.

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio before or after lifting for weight loss depends on your goals. If your primary goal is weight loss, then doing cardio before lifting can be more effective. However, if you are looking to gain muscle or increase strength, then lifting before cardio may be better.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Lifting for Weight Loss

Personally, I have found that doing cardio before lifting has helped me lose weight faster. When I do cardio before lifting, my heart rate is elevated, and I burn more calories during my workout. This has helped me reach my weight loss goals faster than when I lift before doing cardio.

When you do cardio before lifting, you are using up your glycogen stores, which means your body will burn fat for energy during your lifting session. This can lead to more weight loss in the long run.

Drawbacks of Doing Cardio Before Lifting for Weight Loss

One of the drawbacks of doing cardio before lifting is that you may not have enough energy to lift heavy weights. Since you have already used up your glycogen stores during your cardio session, you may feel fatigued and not be able to lift as much weight as you would like. This can lead to slower muscle growth.

The Science Behind Cardio Before or After Lifting for Weight Loss

The science behind cardio before or after lifting for weight loss is that doing cardio before lifting can help you burn more calories during your workout. When you do cardio, your heart rate is elevated, and you burn more calories than when you lift weights. This can lead to more weight loss in the long run.

How to Do Cardio Before or After Lifting for Weight Loss

If you want to do cardio before or after lifting for weight loss, here are some tips:

1. Do cardio before lifting if your primary goal is weight loss.

2. Do lifting before cardio if you want to gain muscle or increase strength.

3. Start with a warm-up before doing either cardio or lifting.

4. Don't overdo it. Start slow and gradually increase the intensity of your workout.

Personal Experience with Cardio Before or After Lifting for Weight Loss

Personally, I have found that doing cardio before lifting has helped me lose weight faster. However, I make sure to warm-up before doing any exercise to prevent injuries. I also make sure to listen to my body and not overdo it.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after lifting for weight loss?

A: It depends on your goals. If your primary goal is weight loss, then doing cardio before lifting can be more effective. However, if you are looking to gain muscle or increase strength, then lifting before cardio may be better.

Q: Can I do both cardio and lifting in the same session?

A: Yes, you can do both cardio and lifting in the same session. However, make sure to warm-up before doing any exercise and not overdo it.

Q: How often should I do cardio and lifting for weight loss?

A: It is recommended to do cardio and lifting at least three to four times a week for weight loss.

Q: How long should my cardio and lifting sessions be?

A: Your cardio and lifting sessions should be at least 30 minutes each.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio Before or After Lifting for Weight Loss

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio before or after lifting for weight loss depends on your goals. If your primary goal is weight loss, then doing cardio before lifting can be more effective. However, if you are looking to gain muscle or increase strength, then lifting before cardio may be better. It is essential to listen to your body and not overdo it. By following these tips, you can achieve your weight loss goals faster and more effectively.

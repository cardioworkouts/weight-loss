What Are Some Interval Training Exercises

Written by Petter Nov 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a way to get fit and burn fat fast, interval training might be just what you need. Interval training is a type of workout that involves alternating high-intensity exercises with periods of rest or lower-intensity exercises. It's a great way to challenge your body and push yourself to new limits.

Many people struggle to find the right exercises to include in their interval training routine. This is especially true if you're new to fitness or haven't worked out in a while. It can be overwhelming to figure out which exercises will work best for your body and fitness level.

If you're wondering what are some interval training exercises, you're in the right place. In this article, we'll take a look at some of the best exercises you can include in your interval training routine.

Running

Running is one of the most popular exercises to include in an interval training routine. It's a great way to get your heart rate up and burn calories quickly. You can include running intervals in your workout by alternating between sprints and jogs. For example, you might sprint for 30 seconds, then jog for 60 seconds before repeating the cycle.

I personally love including running in my interval training routine. It's a great way to challenge myself and see how far I can push my body. Plus, it's a great stress-reliever after a long day at work.

Burpees

Burpees are a full-body exercise that can be challenging but very effective. They work your arms, chest, legs, and core all at once. To do a burpee, start in a standing position, then lower yourself down into a push-up position. From there, jump your feet forward and stand up, then jump up into the air with your arms extended overhead.

Burpees are a tough exercise, but they're a great way to build strength and endurance. I like to include them in my interval training routine because they really get my heart rate up and make me feel like I'm getting a great workout.

Cycling

Cycling is another great exercise to include in your interval training routine. You can do this indoors on a stationary bike or outdoors on a regular bike. To include cycling intervals in your workout, alternate between high-intensity sprints and lower-intensity recovery periods.

Cycling is a great low-impact exercise that's easy on your joints. It's also a great way to get outside and enjoy the fresh air. I like to include cycling in my interval training routine when I want a break from running or other high-impact exercises.

Jumping Jacks

Jumping jacks are a classic exercise that many people remember from their childhood. They're a great way to get your heart rate up and work your entire body. To do jumping jacks, start in a standing position with your feet together and your arms at your sides. Jump your feet out to the sides while raising your arms above your head, then jump back to the starting position.

Jumping jacks are a simple but effective exercise that you can include in your interval training routine. They're a great way to warm up before more intense exercises or to use as a recovery period between high-intensity intervals.

Conclusion of What Are Some Interval Training Exercises

Interval training is a great way to get fit and burn fat quickly. By including exercises like running, burpees, cycling, and jumping jacks in your routine, you can challenge your body and see results in a short amount of time. Remember to always listen to your body and start with exercises that are appropriate for your fitness level. With consistency and dedication, you can achieve your fitness goals and feel great about your body.

Question and Answer

Q: How long should my intervals be?

A: The length of your intervals will depend on your fitness level and goals. Beginners might start with shorter intervals of 30 seconds to one minute, while more advanced athletes might do intervals of two minutes or longer.

Q: How many exercises should I include in my interval training routine?

A: It's up to you how many exercises you want to include in your interval training routine. Some people prefer to focus on just one or two exercises, while others like to mix it up with a variety of exercises.

Q: How often should I do interval training?

A: You should aim to do interval training two to three times per week, with at least one day of rest in between workouts. It's important to give your body time to recover and repair between workouts.

Q: Can I do interval training if I have a pre-existing health condition?

A: If you have a pre-existing health condition, it's important to talk to your doctor before starting any new exercise routine. They can help you determine if interval training is safe for you and give you guidance on how to modify exercises if needed.

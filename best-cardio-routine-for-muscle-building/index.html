Best Cardio Routine For Muscle Building A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to build muscle but wondering what role cardio plays in the process? Many people believe that cardio and muscle building are at odds with each other, but in reality, the right type of cardio can actually aid in muscle growth. In this guide, we will explore the best cardio routine for muscle building and how it can help you achieve your fitness goals.

When it comes to muscle building, many people focus solely on weight lifting and neglect cardio altogether. However, neglecting cardio can hinder your progress in the long run. Cardio helps to improve cardiovascular health, increase endurance, and aid in fat loss, which is essential for achieving a lean and muscular physique.

What is the Best Cardio Routine for Muscle Building?

The best cardio routine for muscle building involves high-intensity interval training (HIIT). HIIT is a form of cardio that alternates between short bursts of high-intensity exercise and periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. This type of cardio has been shown to be more effective for building muscle than traditional steady-state cardio.

During HIIT, your heart rate increases rapidly, which puts your body in a state of anaerobic metabolism. This means that your body is burning energy without using oxygen, which leads to an increase in lactic acid. Lactic acid is beneficial for muscle growth because it triggers the release of growth hormone, which is essential for muscle recovery and growth.

My Personal Experience with HIIT

As someone who has been weight lifting for several years, I can attest to the effectiveness of HIIT for muscle building. Incorporating HIIT into my routine has helped me to increase my endurance, burn fat, and build muscle. I typically do HIIT cardio two to three times per week, alternating between sprint intervals on the treadmill and high-intensity cycling on a stationary bike.

How to Incorporate HIIT into Your Routine

If you're new to HIIT, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts. Begin with a five-minute warm-up, then alternate between 30 seconds of high-intensity exercise and 30 seconds of rest or low-intensity exercise for 10 to 15 minutes. As you become more comfortable with the routine, you can gradually increase the length and intensity of your workouts.

Other Cardio Options for Muscle Building

In addition to HIIT, there are other forms of cardio that can aid in muscle building. These include:

  • Circuit training
  • Jumping rope
  • Stair climbing
  • Rowing

These forms of cardio are also effective at increasing endurance, burning fat, and building muscle. Incorporating a variety of cardio into your routine can help you avoid boredom and keep things interesting.

Tips for Maximizing Muscle Building During Cardio

If your goal is to build muscle, there are a few things you can do to maximize your results during cardio:

  • Focus on high-intensity exercises that target multiple muscle groups
  • Incorporate resistance training into your cardio routine
  • Use proper form and technique to avoid injury
  • Eat a diet rich in protein to support muscle growth

Question and Answer

Q: Will doing too much cardio hinder my muscle building progress?

A: It depends on the type and intensity of cardio that you're doing. Traditional steady-state cardio can hinder muscle growth if done in excess, but high-intensity cardio like HIIT can actually aid in muscle building. It's important to find a balance between cardio and weight lifting that works for your body and goals.

Q: Can I do cardio on the same day as weight lifting?

A: Yes, it's possible to do cardio and weight lifting on the same day. However, it's important to prioritize weight lifting and do cardio after your weight lifting session. This will ensure that you have enough energy to perform your weight lifting exercises with proper form and technique.

Q: How often should I do cardio for muscle building?

A: It's recommended to do cardio two to three times per week for muscle building. However, the frequency and intensity of your cardio routine will depend on your fitness level and goals.

Q: Can I build muscle without doing any cardio?

A: Yes, it's possible to build muscle without doing any cardio. However, incorporating cardio into your routine can help you achieve a lean and muscular physique and improve your overall health.

Conclusion of Best Cardio Routine for Muscle Building

Cardio and muscle building don't have to be at odds with each other. Incorporating the right type of cardio, such as high-intensity interval training, can aid in muscle growth and improve your overall fitness. By finding a balance between cardio and weight lifting and following the tips outlined in this guide, you can achieve your muscle building goals and improve your health and wellbeing.

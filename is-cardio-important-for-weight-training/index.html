Is Cardio Important For Weight Training

Written by Robby Jun 07, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who has been doing weight training regularly, but hasn't seen the desired results? Do you feel like you're stuck in a plateau and don't know what to do next? If you can relate to these questions, then this blog post is for you.

Weight training is an excellent way to build strength and muscle mass, but it shouldn't be the only form of exercise you're doing. Cardiovascular exercise, or cardio, is equally important for your overall health and fitness goals.

Cardio is an exercise that increases your heart rate and breathing rate, which in turn helps you burn calories and improve your cardiovascular health. It can be in the form of running, cycling, swimming, or any other activity that gets your heart pumping.

So, is cardio important for weight training? The answer is yes. In this blog post, we'll discuss why cardio is essential for weight training and how it can help you reach your fitness goals.

Improved Endurance

Cardiovascular exercise helps improve your endurance, which can be beneficial for weight training. When you have better endurance, you can perform more repetitions and sets during your weight training sessions. This means you can lift heavier weights for a longer time, which can help you build muscle mass and strength.

Personally, I used to struggle with endurance during my weight training sessions. However, after incorporating cardio into my routine, I noticed a significant improvement in my endurance. I was able to lift heavier weights for a longer time, which helped me achieve my fitness goals faster.

Burns More Calories

One of the biggest benefits of cardio is that it helps you burn more calories. When you do cardio, your body burns calories for energy. This means that you can create a calorie deficit, which is essential for weight loss.

If you're trying to lose weight, incorporating cardio into your routine can help you reach your goals faster. It can also help you maintain your weight loss in the long run.

Improved Heart Health

Cardiovascular exercise is excellent for your heart health. It can help lower your risk of heart disease, stroke, and other cardiovascular problems. When you do cardio, it strengthens your heart muscles, which helps your heart pump blood more efficiently.

Personally, I have a family history of heart disease, which is why I prioritize cardio in my routine. It has helped me maintain excellent heart health, and I feel more energized and healthy overall.

How to Incorporate Cardio into Your Routine

Now that you know the benefits of cardio, you may be wondering how to incorporate it into your routine. The good news is that there are several ways to do so. You can start by adding 30 minutes of cardio to your routine three times a week. You can also try different forms of cardio to keep things interesting.

Personally, I enjoy running, so I try to run at least three times a week. I also like to mix things up by cycling, swimming, or doing a cardio dance class. The key is to find something you enjoy, so you're more likely to stick to it.

Question and Answer

Q: How much cardio should I do if I'm weight training?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If you're trying to lose weight, you may need to do more cardio than someone who is trying to build muscle mass. A general rule of thumb is to aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week.

Q: Should I do cardio before or after weight training?

A: It's best to do cardio after weight training. This is because weight training requires more energy and strength, and doing cardio beforehand can tire you out. However, if you prefer to do cardio before weight training, make sure to warm up properly to prevent injury.

Q: Can I do cardio and weight training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weight training on the same day. However, it's essential to space them out properly. If you're doing both on the same day, make sure to do weight training first and then cardio.

Q: How long does it take to see results from cardio?

A: It depends on several factors, such as your fitness level, the type of cardio you're doing, and how often you're doing it. However, you can start seeing results within a few weeks of incorporating cardio into your routine.

Conclusion of Is Cardio Important for Weight Training

Cardio is essential for weight training and overall fitness. It helps improve your endurance, burns more calories, and improves your heart health. By incorporating cardio into your routine, you can reach your fitness goals faster and maintain your health in the long run.

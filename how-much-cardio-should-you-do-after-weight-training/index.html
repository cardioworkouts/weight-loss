How Much Cardio Should You Do After Weight Training

Written by Luffy Aug 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a gym-goer who is trying to figure out the best way to balance weight training and cardio? Are you wondering how much cardio should you do after weight training? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with finding the right balance between these two types of exercise.

The answer to how much cardio should you do after weight training depends on your fitness goals. If you're looking to build muscle mass, you should focus more on weight training and limit your cardio to 10-20 minutes after your weight training session. However, if you're looking to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular fitness, you should do cardio for 30-60 minutes after your weight training session.

In summary, the amount of cardio you should do after weight training depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle mass, limit your cardio to 10-20 minutes. If you want to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular fitness, do cardio for 30-60 minutes.

Personal Experience with Cardio After Weight Training

I personally aim to do 30-40 minutes of cardio after my weight training sessions. I find that this helps me maintain my weight while also improving my cardiovascular fitness. I also enjoy the mental benefits of cardio, such as reducing stress and boosting my mood.

The Benefits of Cardio After Weight Training

Doing cardio after weight training can provide many benefits. It can help you burn more calories and lose weight, improve your cardiovascular fitness, and reduce stress. Cardio can also help increase blood flow to your muscles, aiding in recovery and reducing soreness.

How Much Cardio is Too Much?

While cardio can be beneficial, doing too much can be counterproductive. If you do too much cardio after weight training, you can hinder your muscle growth and recovery. It's important to find the right balance between cardio and weight training.

Tips for Finding the Right Balance

One way to find the right balance between cardio and weight training is to prioritize your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle mass, focus more on weight training and limit your cardio to a few minutes. If you want to lose weight, do more cardio after weight training. It's also important to listen to your body and adjust your workout routine accordingly.

Personal Experience with Cardio After Weight Training

When I first started weight training, I didn't do any cardio. However, I soon realized that I needed to do cardio to maintain my weight and improve my cardiovascular fitness. I started doing 10-15 minutes of cardio after my weight training sessions and gradually increased the time to 30-40 minutes. I found that this helped me achieve my fitness goals and feel better overall.

Question and Answer

Q: Should I do cardio before or after weight training?

A: It's generally recommended to do weight training before cardio so you have more energy for lifting weights. However, if you prefer doing cardio first, that's fine too. Just make sure you have enough energy left for weight training.

Q: How often should I do cardio after weight training?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular fitness, you should aim to do cardio after weight training 3-5 times per week. If you're focusing on building muscle mass, limit your cardio to 1-2 times per week.

Q: Can I do cardio on rest days?

A: Yes, you can do cardio on rest days. However, make sure you're not overdoing it and giving your body enough time to recover.

Q: What are some good cardio exercises to do after weight training?

A: Some good cardio exercises to do after weight training include running, cycling, swimming, and using the elliptical machine.

Conclusion of How Much Cardio Should You Do After Weight Training

Ultimately, the amount of cardio you should do after weight training depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle mass, limit your cardio to 10-20 minutes. If you want to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular fitness, do cardio for 30-60 minutes. It's important to find the right balance between cardio and weight training to achieve your fitness goals and feel your best.

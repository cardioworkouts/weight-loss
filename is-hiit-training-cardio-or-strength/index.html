Is Hiit Training Cardio Or Strength

Written by Petter Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending hours at the gym without seeing results? Do you want to know if HIIT training is the right workout for you? One of the most common questions people have about HIIT training is whether it is cardio or strength training. In this article, we will explore the answer to this question and provide you with all the information you need to know about HIIT training.

Pain Points of Is HIIT Training Cardio or Strength

When it comes to working out, everyone has different goals. Some people want to lose weight, while others want to build muscle. However, no matter what your goal is, most people want to see results quickly. Unfortunately, traditional cardio and strength training can be time-consuming and may not produce the desired results. This is where HIIT training comes in. But before you start, it is important to understand what HIIT training is and whether it is cardio or strength training.

Answer to Is HIIT Training Cardio or Strength

The answer is that HIIT training is a combination of both cardio and strength training. HIIT stands for High-Intensity Interval Training, which involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by brief periods of rest. During the intense exercise periods, you are performing cardio exercises, such as running or cycling, which help to improve your cardiovascular health. During the rest periods, you are performing strength exercises, such as push-ups or squats, which help to build muscle mass.

Summary of Is HIIT Training Cardio or Strength

In summary, HIIT training is a combination of cardio and strength training. It involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by brief periods of rest. This workout routine is perfect for people who want to see quick results and don't have a lot of time to spend at the gym. Now that we have answered the question of whether HIIT training is cardio or strength training, let's dive into the benefits and how to get started.

Benefits of HIIT Training

Personally, I have been doing HIIT training for the past year, and I have seen a significant improvement in my overall fitness level. Not only have I lost weight, but I have also gained muscle mass and improved my cardiovascular health. One of the greatest benefits of HIIT training is that it can be done in a short amount of time. A typical HIIT workout lasts between 20-30 minutes, making it perfect for people with busy schedules. Additionally, HIIT training can help to increase your metabolism, which means you will continue burning calories even after your workout is over.

How to Get Started with HIIT Training

Getting started with HIIT training is easy. All you need is a timer, a mat, and some weights if you want to add some resistance to your workout. Begin by choosing a cardio exercise, such as running or cycling, and perform it at a high intensity for 30 seconds. Follow this with a 30-second rest period, and then perform a strength exercise, such as push-ups or squats, for 30 seconds. Repeat this circuit for 20-30 minutes, or until you have completed 3-4 rounds. It is important to warm up before you start and cool down after you finish.

The Science Behind HIIT Training

HIIT training has been proven to be an effective workout routine. According to a study published in the Journal of Obesity, HIIT training can help to reduce abdominal fat and improve cardiovascular health. Additionally, a study published in the Journal of Sports Science and Medicine found that HIIT training can help to improve muscle mass and strength.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your HIIT Workout

If you want to get the most out of your HIIT workout, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose exercises that work multiple muscle groups
  • Incorporate resistance training to build muscle mass
  • Choose exercises that you enjoy to keep yourself motivated
  • Make sure to warm up and cool down properly
  • Stay hydrated throughout your workout

FAQs about Is HIIT Training Cardio or Strength

Q: Is HIIT training better than traditional cardio or strength training?

A: HIIT training can be more effective than traditional cardio or strength training for weight loss and building muscle mass.

Q: How often should I do HIIT training?

A: It is recommended to do HIIT training 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between.

Q: Can I do HIIT training at home?

A: Yes, HIIT training can be done at home with little to no equipment.

Q: How long should a HIIT workout last?

A: A typical HIIT workout lasts between 20-30 minutes.

Conclusion of Is HIIT Training Cardio or Strength

So, is HIIT training cardio or strength training? The answer is that it is a combination of both. HIIT training involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by brief periods of rest, which combines cardio and strength training. If you are looking to lose weight, build muscle mass, and improve your overall fitness level, HIIT training may be the perfect workout routine for you. Just remember to warm up properly, choose exercises that work multiple muscle groups, and stay hydrated throughout your workout.

How Long Should You Wait To Do Cardio After Weights

Written by Bardi Sep 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you one of those fitness enthusiasts who are confused about when to do cardio after weight training? Well, you're not alone. Many people are also in the same boat as you, and it's understandable. With different opinions from fitness experts and conflicting information online, it can be challenging to determine the correct answer. In this article, we'll discuss how long you should wait to do cardio after weights and clear up any confusion you may have on the topic.

Pain Points of How Long Should You Wait to Do Cardio After Weights

One of the most common pain points when it comes to how long to wait to do cardio after weights is the fear of losing muscle mass. People often worry that if they don't do cardio immediately after weight training, they will lose their muscle gains. Others are concerned about their energy levels and want to avoid feeling tired or fatigued during their cardio sessions. Finally, some individuals may be unsure of the proper order of exercises and want to avoid doing them in the wrong sequence.

Answering the Target of How Long Should You Wait to Do Cardio After Weights

The general consensus among fitness experts is that you should wait at least 20-30 minutes after weight training before starting your cardio session. This period allows your body to replenish energy stores and recover from the weight training. Additionally, it gives your muscles time to relax and recover from the intense workout. Waiting for 20-30 minutes also helps ensure that you are not too fatigued during your cardio session.

However, the ideal waiting time before doing cardio after weights varies depending on your fitness level and goals. If you're a beginner, you may want to wait longer before starting cardio to avoid fatigue and optimize your results. On the other hand, if you're an experienced athlete, you may have a shorter waiting time before starting cardio.

Summary of How Long Should You Wait to Do Cardio After Weights

In summary, the ideal waiting time before doing cardio after weights is 20-30 minutes. Waiting for this period allows your body to recover from the weight training, replenish energy stores, and avoid fatigue during your cardio session. However, the waiting time can vary depending on your fitness level and goals.

Personal Experience and Explanation of How Long Should You Wait to Do Cardio After Weights

As a personal trainer, I've had many clients ask me about the proper order of exercises and when to do cardio after weight training. From my experience, waiting for at least 20-30 minutes is an excellent rule of thumb. It gives your body sufficient time to recover and ensures that you are not too fatigued during your cardio session.

However, it's essential to listen to your body and adjust the waiting time based on your fitness level and goals. If you're feeling tired or fatigued after weight training, you may want to wait a little longer before starting cardio. On the other hand, if you're an experienced athlete, you may be able to start cardio sooner.

Benefits of Doing Cardio After Weight Training

Doing cardio after weight training offers numerous benefits. It can help burn additional calories, reduce stress levels, and improve your cardiovascular health. Additionally, it can help you recover faster from your weight training workout, leading to better results over time.

How to Incorporate Cardio After Weight Training

If you're interested in incorporating cardio after weight training, here's a simple way to do it. First, warm up with five minutes of low-intensity cardio, such as walking or cycling. Then, do your weight training workout as usual. Afterward, wait for 20-30 minutes before starting your cardio session. Finally, cool down with five minutes of low-intensity cardio to help your body recover.

Frequently Asked Questions About How Long Should You Wait to Do Cardio After Weights

Q: Will doing cardio after weight training burn muscle?

A: No. Doing cardio after weight training will not burn muscle. In fact, it can help improve your cardiovascular health and burn additional calories.

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weight training?

A: It's generally better to do weight training before cardio. This order allows you to focus on building strength and muscle mass before burning additional calories through cardio.

Q: Can I do cardio and weight training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weight training on the same day. However, it's essential to space them out to avoid fatigue and ensure that you are giving each exercise your best effort.

Q: How long should my cardio session be after weight training?

A: The duration of your cardio session after weight training depends on your fitness level and goals. However, a good starting point is 20-30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio.

Conclusion of How Long Should You Wait to Do Cardio After Weights

In conclusion, waiting for at least 20-30 minutes before doing cardio after weight training is an excellent rule of thumb. It allows your body to recover from the weight training, replenish energy stores, and avoid fatigue during your cardio session. However, it's essential to listen to your body and adjust the waiting time based on your fitness level and goals. By doing cardio after weight training, you can improve your cardiovascular health, burn additional calories, and recover faster from your workouts.

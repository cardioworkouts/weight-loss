Does It Matter What Time You Do Cardio

Written by Luffy Jul 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who is trying to get fit and healthy? If so, you have probably heard that doing cardio is an essential part of any workout routine. However, you may be wondering if it matters what time of day you do cardio. This blog post will answer that question and provide you with helpful tips on how to maximize your cardio workouts.

When it comes to cardio, there are several pain points that people often face. They may not know how long to do cardio, how intense it should be, or what type of cardio is best for their fitness goals. Another common pain point is whether it matters what time of day they do their cardio.

The short answer is that it doesn't matter what time of day you do cardio. The most important thing is that you are consistent with your workouts. If you can only fit in cardio in the morning, that's great. If you prefer to do it in the evening, that's fine too. The key is to find a time that works for you and stick to it.

In summary, the main points to remember about does it matter what time you do cardio are: it doesn't matter as long as you are consistent, and the most important thing is finding a time that works for you.

Cardio in the Morning

Personally, I prefer to do cardio in the morning. I find that it gives me a burst of energy to start my day, and it helps me stay focused throughout the day. Additionally, doing cardio in the morning can help boost your metabolism, which can help you burn more calories throughout the day.

Cardio in the Evening

On the other hand, some people prefer to do cardio in the evening. They may find that it helps them unwind after a long day at work, or it may fit better with their schedule. While there is no scientific evidence to support the idea that cardio is more effective in the evening, it's all about finding what works for you.

The Best Type of Cardio

When it comes to the best type of cardio, it really depends on your fitness goals. If you are looking to improve your endurance, running or cycling may be the best option. If you want to burn more calories, high-intensity interval training (HIIT) may be more effective. Ultimately, the best type of cardio is one that you enjoy and can stick to.

How Long Should You Do Cardio?

The amount of cardio you should do depends on your fitness goals and your current fitness level. If you are just starting out, aim for 20-30 minutes of cardio per session, three times per week. As you get fitter, you can increase the duration and frequency of your cardio workouts. Just remember to listen to your body and avoid overtraining.

Cardio and Weight Loss

If you are looking to lose weight, cardio can be a great tool to add to your weight loss plan. Cardio can help you burn calories, which can create a calorie deficit and lead to weight loss. However, it's important to remember that weight loss is not just about cardio. You also need to focus on your diet and strength training to see the best results.

Question and Answer

Q: Should I do cardio before or after strength training?
A: It really depends on your fitness goals. If you are looking to build muscle, you may want to do strength training before cardio. If you are looking to improve your endurance, you may want to do cardio first. Ultimately, it's up to you to decide what works best for your body.

Q: Is it better to do cardio on an empty stomach?
A: There is no clear answer to this question. Some people find that doing cardio on an empty stomach helps them burn more fat, while others find that they have more energy when they eat before their workout. It's important to listen to your body and do what works best for you.

Q: How often should I do cardio?
A: It's recommended that adults get at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week. This can be broken down into smaller sessions throughout the week.

Q: Can I do cardio every day?
A: Yes, you can do cardio every day if you want to. However, it's important to listen to your body and avoid overtraining. It's also important to vary your workouts to avoid boredom and prevent injury.

Conclusion of Does It Matter What Time You Do Cardio

In conclusion, the best time to do cardio is the time that works best for you. Whether you prefer to do it in the morning or evening, the important thing is that you are consistent with your workouts. Remember to vary your workouts and listen to your body to avoid overtraining and achieve your fitness goals.

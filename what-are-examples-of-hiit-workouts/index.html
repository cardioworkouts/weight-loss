What Are Examples Of Hiit Workouts

Written by Daniel Aug 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever wondered how to get a great workout in a short amount of time? High-intensity interval training (HIIT) workouts are a great way to do just that. They are quick and effective, and can help you burn more calories in less time. In this article, we will discuss what HIIT workouts are and provide some examples of them.

Many people struggle with finding time to exercise, or they may find it difficult to get motivated to work out. Others may not know what types of workouts are best for them. HIIT workouts can help solve these problems. They are typically short, around 20-30 minutes, and can be done virtually anywhere. They are also designed to be challenging, providing a sense of accomplishment and motivation to keep going.

What Are Examples of HIIT Workouts?

There are many different types of HIIT workouts, and they can be tailored to fit your fitness level and goals. Here are a few examples:

1. Tabata

Tabata is a type of HIIT workout that involves 20 seconds of intense exercise, followed by 10 seconds of rest. This is repeated for a total of eight rounds, or four minutes. This workout can be done with any type of exercise, such as squats, lunges, or push-ups.

2. Sprint Intervals

Sprint intervals involve short bursts of sprinting, followed by periods of rest or slower jogging. For example, you could sprint for 30 seconds, then walk or jog for one minute. This is repeated for a total of 20-30 minutes.

3. Circuit Training

Circuit training is a type of HIIT workout that involves moving from one exercise to the next with little to no rest in between. This can be done with a variety of exercises, such as jumping jacks, burpees, or mountain climbers. This workout can be done for a set amount of time, such as 20 minutes, or for a certain number of rounds.

4. EMOM

EMOM stands for "every minute on the minute." In this type of workout, you perform a certain number of reps of an exercise at the start of each minute. You then rest for the remainder of the minute. This is repeated for a set number of minutes or rounds. For example, you could do five burpees at the start of each minute for 10 minutes.

These are just a few examples of HIIT workouts. There are many other variations, and you can create your own based on your fitness level and preferences.

My Personal Experience with HIIT Workouts

As a busy college student, I often struggle to find time to exercise. HIIT workouts have been a game-changer for me. I can fit in a quick workout between classes or before studying, and I always feel great afterwards. I typically do Tabata or circuit training workouts, and I love the sense of accomplishment I get from completing a challenging workout in a short amount of time.

Benefits of HIIT Workouts

Aside from being a time-efficient way to exercise, HIIT workouts have many other benefits. They can help improve cardiovascular health, increase endurance, and boost metabolism. They can also help burn more calories and fat than traditional steady-state cardio workouts.

How to Get Started with HIIT Workouts

If you're new to HIIT workouts, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. It's also important to warm up before each workout and cool down afterwards. You can find many free HIIT workout videos online, or you can create your own workouts using exercises you enjoy.

Tips for Creating Your Own HIIT Workouts

When creating your own HIIT workouts, it's important to choose exercises that target different muscle groups and vary the intensity and duration of each exercise. You can also incorporate equipment, such as dumbbells or resistance bands, to add variety and challenge. Lastly, don't forget to listen to your body and rest when needed.

Conclusion of What Are Examples of HIIT Workouts

HIIT workouts are a great way to get a quick and effective workout in a short amount of time. With many different variations to choose from, you can find a HIIT workout that fits your fitness level and preferences. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned athlete, HIIT workouts can help you achieve your fitness goals.

Question and Answer

Q: What are the benefits of HIIT workouts?

A: HIIT workouts can improve cardiovascular health, increase endurance, boost metabolism, and burn more calories and fat than traditional steady-state cardio workouts.

Q: How long should a HIIT workout be?

A: HIIT workouts are typically around 20-30 minutes, but can be shorter or longer depending on your fitness level and goals.

Q: Can HIIT workouts be done at home?

A: Yes, HIIT workouts can be done virtually anywhere, including at home with little to no equipment.

Q: How often should I do HIIT workouts?

A: It's recommended to do HIIT workouts 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between.

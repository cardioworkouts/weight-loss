How Many Minutes Of Cardio After Weight Lifting

Written by Oliver Aug 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering how many minutes of cardio you should do after weight lifting? You’re not alone. This is a common question among gym-goers who want to maximize their workouts and achieve their fitness goals. In this tutorial blog post, we will provide you with all the information you need to know about how many minutes of cardio after weight lifting.

Pain Points

Many people who do weight lifting struggle with the idea of adding cardio to their routine. They may feel like they don't have enough time, or they may be concerned about losing muscle mass. Additionally, some people may not know how much cardio is enough to complement their weight lifting routine.

How Many Minutes of Cardio After Weight Lifting?

The recommended amount of cardio after weight lifting is 20-30 minutes. This will help you burn excess calories, improve your cardiovascular health, and aid in recovery. However, if you’re short on time, even just 10 minutes of cardio can be beneficial. The key is to make sure you’re doing something rather than nothing.

It’s important to note that the amount of cardio you should do after weight lifting depends on your fitness goals. If you’re looking to build muscle mass, you’ll want to focus more on weight lifting and less on cardio. On the other hand, if you’re looking to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular health, you’ll want to add more cardio to your routine.

Summary

In summary, the recommended amount of cardio after weight lifting is 20-30 minutes. However, the amount of cardio you should do depends on your fitness goals. If you’re short on time, even just 10 minutes of cardio can be beneficial.

Personal Experience: How Many Minutes of Cardio After Weight Lifting

As a personal trainer, I recommend my clients do 20-30 minutes of cardio after weight lifting. For me personally, I like to do 20 minutes on the elliptical machine after my weight lifting sessions. It helps me cool down and recover, and I feel like it gives me an extra boost of energy for the rest of my day.

Benefits of Cardio After Weight Lifting

Cardio after weight lifting has many benefits. It can help you burn excess calories, improve your cardiovascular health, and aid in recovery. Additionally, it can help you build endurance and improve your overall fitness level.

Cardio and Muscle Mass

Some people may be concerned about losing muscle mass if they do too much cardio after weight lifting. While it’s true that cardio can burn calories and potentially lead to muscle loss, this is only the case if you’re doing too much cardio or not eating enough to support your muscle growth. As long as you’re eating enough and doing the recommended amount of cardio, you shouldn't have to worry about losing muscle mass.

How to Incorporate Cardio After Weight Lifting

If you’re new to cardio after weight lifting, start by doing just 10 minutes and gradually increase the time each week. You can also switch up the types of cardio you do to keep it interesting and challenging. Some good options include the treadmill, elliptical, stationary bike, or rowing machine.

Personal Experience: How Many Minutes of Cardio After Weight Lifting

For me, adding cardio after weight lifting has been a game-changer. I used to struggle with losing weight and building endurance, but since I started doing 20 minutes of cardio after my weight lifting sessions, I’ve noticed a significant improvement in both areas. I feel more energized and motivated to keep pushing myself.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it necessary to do cardio after weight lifting?

A: No, it’s not necessary, but it can be beneficial for improving your cardiovascular health, aiding in recovery, and burning excess calories.

Q: Can cardio after weight lifting help me build muscle mass?

A: While cardio isn’t the best way to build muscle mass, it can still be beneficial for improving your overall fitness level and aiding in recovery.

Q: How much cardio should I do if I’m looking to lose weight?

A: If you’re looking to lose weight, it’s recommended to do at least 30 minutes of cardio 5 times per week.

Q: Can I do cardio before weight lifting?

A: Yes, you can do cardio before weight lifting, but it’s recommended to do weight lifting first as it requires more energy and focus.

Conclusion of How Many Minutes of Cardio After Weight Lifting

Adding cardio after weight lifting can be a great way to improve your overall fitness level and achieve your fitness goals. The recommended amount of cardio after weight lifting is 20-30 minutes, but the amount you should do depends on your fitness goals. If you’re new to cardio after weight lifting, start with just 10 minutes and gradually increase the time each week. Remember, even just a little bit of cardio is better than nothing.

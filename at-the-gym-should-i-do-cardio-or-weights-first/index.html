At The Gym Should I Do Cardio Or Weights First

Written by Daniel Oct 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Going to the gym can be overwhelming, especially if you're new to the scene. One of the biggest questions people have is whether they should do cardio or weights first. The answer is not a simple one, but we'll break it down for you in this post.

The Pain Points of Cardio vs. Weights

When it comes to deciding whether to start with cardio or weights, there are a few pain points to consider. For one, if you're short on time, you may feel like you have to choose between the two. Additionally, if you're trying to lose weight, you may think that cardio is the way to go. On the other hand, if you're looking to build muscle, you may think that weights are your only option.

The Answer to the Question: Cardio or Weights First?

The truth is, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. It depends on your personal goals and preferences. However, there are a few things to consider. If your primary goal is to build strength, you should start with weights. This is because if you tire yourself out with cardio first, you won't have the energy to properly lift weights. On the other hand, if your primary goal is to lose weight, starting with cardio may be more beneficial. This is because cardio burns more calories than weightlifting.

Main Points to Consider

Ultimately, the decision of whether to start with cardio or weights at the gym should be based on your personal goals. If you want to build strength, start with weights. If you want to lose weight, start with cardio. It's also important to remember that both forms of exercise are important for overall health and fitness.

Personal Experience with Cardio vs. Weights

Personally, I like to start with cardio because it helps me get warmed up and gets my heart rate up. However, I know that if I want to build muscle, I need to start with weights. It really just depends on what my goals are for that particular workout.

The Benefits of Cardio

Cardiovascular exercise has many benefits, including improving heart health and increasing endurance. It's also a great way to burn calories and lose weight. Some examples of cardio exercises include running, cycling, and swimming.

The Benefits of Weights

Weightlifting is great for building muscle and increasing strength. It also has the added benefit of increasing bone density, which is important for overall health. Some examples of weightlifting exercises include squats, deadlifts, and bench presses.

Tips for Cardio vs. Weights

If you're unsure about whether to start with cardio or weights, try alternating between the two. For example, you could start with 10 minutes of cardio, followed by 10 minutes of weights, and alternate until you've completed your workout. You could also try doing cardio and weights on separate days.

How to Incorporate Both Cardio and Weights into Your Workout

The best way to incorporate both cardio and weights into your workout is to create a plan that works for you. This may involve doing cardio on some days and weights on others, or it may involve alternating between the two in the same workout. It's important to listen to your body and do what feels best for you.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio or weights first if I'm trying to lose weight?

A: Starting with cardio may be more beneficial if your primary goal is to lose weight because cardio burns more calories than weightlifting.

Q: Is it better to do cardio or weights first if I'm trying to build muscle?

A: If your primary goal is to build strength, you should start with weights. If you tire yourself out with cardio first, you won't have the energy to properly lift weights.

Q: Can I do cardio and weights on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weights on the same day. It's important to listen to your body and do what feels best for you.

Q: How long should I do cardio for?

A: The amount of time you should do cardio for depends on your personal goals and fitness level. A good starting point is 30 minutes, but you can gradually increase this over time.

Conclusion

When it comes to deciding whether to do cardio or weights first at the gym, there is no one-size-fits-all answer. It depends on your personal goals and preferences. However, both forms of exercise are important for overall health and fitness, so it's important to incorporate both into your workout routine. Remember to listen to your body and do what feels best for you.

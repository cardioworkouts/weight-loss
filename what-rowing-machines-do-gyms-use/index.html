What Rowing Machines Do Gyms Use

Written by Bardi Nov 07, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you looking to add a new exercise to your gym routine? Rowing machines are a great option for a full-body workout. But with so many options available, it can be overwhelming to know which rowing machines gyms use. In this post, we’ll explore the different types of rowing machines that gyms typically have and what makes them a popular choice for fitness enthusiasts.

Rowing machines are a popular choice for gyms because they provide a low-impact workout that engages all major muscle groups. They offer a full-body workout that strengthens the legs, core, and arms, while also improving cardiovascular health. Additionally, rowing machines can help to improve posture and balance, making them a great option for seniors or those with joint pain.

There are two main types of rowing machines that gyms use: air resistance and magnetic resistance. Air resistance rowing machines use a fan to create resistance, while magnetic resistance rowing machines use magnets to generate resistance. Both types of machines have their benefits, but air resistance machines tend to be more popular in gyms due to their durability and ability to provide a more challenging workout.

In summary, rowing machines are a popular choice for gyms because they provide a full-body workout that is low-impact and engages all major muscle groups. Air resistance and magnetic resistance are the two main types of rowing machines that gyms use, with air resistance being the more popular choice due to its durability and ability to provide a challenging workout.

Why Are Air Resistance Rowing Machines Popular in Gyms?

When I first started going to the gym, I was intimidated by the rowing machine. But after trying it out, I quickly realized why air resistance rowing machines are so popular in gyms. Not only do they provide a challenging workout, but the resistance increases as you row harder, making it easy to track progress and set goals.

Additionally, air resistance rowing machines are durable and can withstand heavy use from multiple gym-goers. They are also easy to clean and maintain, making them a popular choice for gym owners.

What Makes Magnetic Resistance Rowing Machines a Good Option?

While air resistance rowing machines are more popular in gyms, magnetic resistance rowing machines have their own benefits. One of the main advantages of magnetic resistance machines is that they are quieter than air resistance machines, making them a great option for home use or smaller gyms.

Additionally, magnetic resistance machines offer a smoother rowing experience, which can be beneficial for those with joint pain or injuries. They also tend to be more affordable than air resistance machines, making them a good option for those on a budget.

How Do Rowing Machines Benefit Your Health?

Rowing machines provide a number of health benefits, including improved cardiovascular health, increased muscle strength, and improved posture and balance. Additionally, rowing is a low-impact exercise, making it a good option for those with joint pain or injuries.

One of the unique benefits of rowing machines is that they engage all major muscle groups, including the legs, core, and arms. This full-body workout can help to increase muscle strength and tone, while also burning calories and improving cardiovascular health. Additionally, rowing can help to improve posture and balance, making it a great option for seniors or those with back pain.

How Do You Properly Use a Rowing Machine?

Using a rowing machine is fairly straightforward, but it’s important to use proper form to avoid injury and get the most out of your workout. Here’s a step-by-step guide to using a rowing machine:

  1. Sit on the seat with your feet securely fastened to the footrests.
  2. Grab the handle with an overhand grip and sit up straight with your arms extended.
  3. Push off with your legs and lean back slightly, pulling the handle towards your chest.
  4. Reverse the movement by leaning forward and straightening your arms, then bending your knees to return to the starting position.

Conclusion of What Rowing Machines Do Gyms Use

Rowing machines are a popular choice for gyms because they provide a full-body workout that is low-impact and engages all major muscle groups. Air resistance and magnetic resistance are the two main types of rowing machines that gyms use, with air resistance being the more popular choice due to its durability and ability to provide a challenging workout. Whether you’re new to rowing or a seasoned pro, incorporating a rowing machine into your gym routine can help to improve your overall fitness and health.

How Many Cardio Days When Cutting A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Jul 31, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you trying to lose weight and wondering how many cardio days when cutting you should do? Are you confused about the right amount of cardio to reach your weight loss goals? You're not alone. Many people struggle with finding the right balance of exercise and diet when trying to lose weight.

The idea of cutting down on calories and increasing your cardio days can be overwhelming, especially if you are new to weight loss. It can be hard to know where to start and how much is too much. However, the good news is that you can figure out the right amount of cardio for your body and goals.

When it comes to how many cardio days when cutting, the answer is not one-size-fits-all. The amount of cardio you need to do depends on several factors, including your fitness level, weight loss goals, and the type of cardio you enjoy doing.

To help you figure out how many cardio days when cutting are right for you, we've put together this complete guide with everything you need to know about cardio and weight loss.

My Personal Experience with Cardio Days When Cutting

When I first started my weight loss journey, I was doing cardio every day for two hours. I thought the more cardio I did, the faster I would lose weight. However, I quickly realized that this was not sustainable, and I was burning out. I decided to switch to four days of cardio and focus on strength training the other days. This approach helped me to lose weight without feeling burnt out or overwhelmed.

The Right Amount of Cardio Days When Cutting

The right amount of cardio days when cutting depends on your weight loss goals and fitness level. If you are new to cardio, it's best to start slow and gradually increase the number of days you do cardio. A good starting point is two to three days a week for 30 minutes per session.

If your goal is to lose weight quickly, you may need to increase the number of cardio days when cutting. However, it's essential to find a balance between cardio and strength training. Strength training helps to build muscle and boost your metabolism, which can help you burn more calories throughout the day.

The Best Types of Cardio for Weight Loss

Not all cardio is created equal when it comes to weight loss. The best types of cardio are those that get your heart rate up and burn calories quickly. Some of the best cardio exercises for weight loss include:

  • Running or jogging
  • Swimming
  • High-intensity interval training (HIIT)
  • Cycling
  • Rowing

Tips for Adding More Cardio Days When Cutting

If you want to add more cardio days when cutting, there are a few things you can do to make it easier on yourself:

  • Find a cardio activity you enjoy
  • Start slow and gradually increase the number of days
  • Break up your cardio sessions into shorter sessions throughout the day
  • Listen to music or a podcast to make the time go by faster
  • Work out with a friend or hire a personal trainer for accountability

Question and Answer

Q: Can too much cardio be bad for weight loss?

A: Yes, too much cardio can be counterproductive for weight loss. Overdoing cardio can lead to burnout and can also cause your body to hold onto fat as a survival mechanism.

Q: How long should my cardio sessions be?

A: It's best to aim for at least 30 minutes per cardio session, but you can break it up into shorter sessions throughout the day if you need to.

Q: Can I do cardio on rest days?

A: Yes, you can do cardio on rest days. However, it's essential to listen to your body and not overdo it. Rest days are also important for muscle recovery.

Q: Do I need to do cardio to lose weight?

A: No, cardio is not necessary for weight loss. However, it can be helpful for burning calories and improving cardiovascular health.

Conclusion of How Many Cardio Days When Cutting

When it comes to how many cardio days when cutting, there is no one answer that fits all. The right amount of cardio depends on your fitness level, weight loss goals, and the type of cardio you prefer. It's essential to find a balance between cardio and strength training and to listen to your body. By following the tips and advice in this guide, you can find the right amount of cardio for your body and weight loss goals.

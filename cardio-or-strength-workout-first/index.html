Free Cardio Or Strength Workout First For Advanced Weight Training

Written by Aprilia Jun 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

In this article, we will discuss whether you should do cardio or strength workout first. Many people are confused about which one to do first and whether it makes any difference. We will answer all your questions related to cardio or strength workout first and provide you with a comprehensive understanding of both.

Working out comes with a lot of benefits, but it can also be painful, especially if you are new to it. Most people experience muscle soreness or stiffness after a workout, and it can be discouraging. That's why it's essential to understand the best way to approach your workout routine to minimize discomfort and maximize benefits.

Before we dive into the topic of cardio or strength workout first, let's discuss what each of them is and what their targets are. Cardio workout involves any activity that raises your heart rate and increases your breathing rate. The primary target of cardio is to improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and obesity. On the other hand, strength training involves any activity that strengthens your muscles and increases your strength. The primary target of strength training is to build muscle mass, increase bone density, and boost your metabolism.

In summary, cardio or strength workout first depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular health, you should do cardio first. However, if your primary goal is to build muscle mass, you should do strength training first.

The Benefits of Cardio or Strength Workout First

Personally, I prefer to do cardio first because it helps me warm up and get my heart rate up before I start strength training. Doing cardio first also helps me burn more calories during my workout. I noticed that when I do strength training first, I tend to get tired quickly, and my form suffers. When I do cardio first, I have more energy to lift weights, and my form is better. However, this is my personal preference, and it may not work for everyone.

On the other hand, some people prefer to do strength training first because it helps them build muscle mass faster. When you do strength training first, your muscles are fresh, and you can lift heavier weights, which leads to more muscle growth. Additionally, doing strength training first can help you burn more calories during your cardio workout since you will be using more energy to perform the exercises.

The Best Approach for Cardio or Strength Workout First

The best approach for cardio or strength workout first is to experiment and see what works best for you. Try doing cardio first for a week and then switch to strength training first for another week. Pay attention to how your body feels during and after each workout. If you notice that you have more energy and better form doing cardio first, then stick with it. However, if you notice that you are building muscle mass faster and have more strength doing strength training first, then switch to that.

Factors to Consider When Deciding Whether to Do Cardio or Strength Workout First

There are several factors to consider when deciding whether to do cardio or strength workout first. First, consider your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular health, then doing cardio first makes sense. However, if your primary goal is to build muscle mass, then doing strength training first makes sense.

Second, consider your energy levels. If you have more energy in the morning, then doing cardio or strength workout first thing in the morning might be the best approach for you. However, if you have more energy in the evening, then doing your workout in the evening might be the best approach for you.

How to Incorporate Cardio and Strength Training into Your Workout Routine

If you want to incorporate both cardio and strength training into your workout routine, the best approach is to alternate between the two. For example, you can do cardio on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, and strength training on Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday. This way, you can give your body enough time to recover between workouts and prevent injury.

Conclusion of Cardio or Strength Workout First

Cardio or strength workout first depends on your fitness goals and personal preference. If your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular health, then doing cardio first makes sense. However, if your primary goal is to build muscle mass, then doing strength training first makes sense. The best approach is to experiment and see what works best for you, and always listen to your body. Remember, consistency is key, so make sure to stick to your workout routine and have fun!

Question and Answer

Q: Does it matter if I do cardio or strength training first?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular health, then doing cardio first makes sense. However, if your primary goal is to build muscle mass, then doing strength training first makes sense.

Q: Can I do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and strength training on the same day. However, it's essential to alternate between the two and give your body enough time to recover between workouts.

Q: How long should I do cardio or strength training for?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals. For beginners, it's recommended to do 30 minutes of cardio and 20-30 minutes of strength training. For advanced exercisers, you can do up to an hour of cardio and 45-60 minutes of strength training.

Q: How often should I do cardio or strength training?

A: It's recommended to do cardio and strength training at least three times a week for optimal results. However, you can do more or less depending on your fitness level and goals.

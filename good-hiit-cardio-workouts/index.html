Good Hiit Cardio Workouts A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Nov 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of boring, monotonous cardio workouts that seem to drag on forever? Do you want to get your heart rate up and burn some serious calories in a short amount of time? If so, then you need to try HIIT cardio workouts.

Many people struggle with finding a cardio routine that they enjoy and that produces results. Traditional cardio workouts, such as running on a treadmill or using an elliptical, can become tedious and unexciting. HIIT, or high-intensity interval training, offers a solution to this problem.

HIIT cardio workouts are designed to be short, intense bursts of activity followed by brief periods of rest. These workouts can be done with a variety of exercises, such as running, cycling, or bodyweight exercises, and can be tailored to your fitness level.

In this article, we will explore the benefits of HIIT cardio workouts, the different types of exercises you can incorporate, and provide some sample workouts to get you started.

Tabata Training

Tabata training is a specific type of HIIT workout that involves 20 seconds of high-intensity exercise followed by 10 seconds of rest, repeated for a total of eight rounds. This type of workout is great for improving cardiovascular endurance and burning fat.

Personally, I love incorporating Tabata training into my routine. One of my favorite workouts is a Tabata-style jump rope routine. I jump rope for 20 seconds, rest for 10 seconds, and repeat for a total of eight rounds. It's a quick and effective way to get my heart rate up and burn some calories.

Circuit Training

Circuit training is another type of HIIT workout that involves a series of exercises performed back-to-back with little to no rest in between. This type of workout is great for building strength and improving cardiovascular fitness.

When I want to mix up my routine, I like to do a circuit-style workout that includes a variety of exercises, such as squats, lunges, push-ups, and burpees. I perform each exercise for 30 seconds, rest for 15 seconds, and move on to the next exercise. I repeat the circuit three times for a total of 15 minutes.

The Benefits of HIIT Cardio Workouts

There are numerous benefits to incorporating HIIT cardio workouts into your fitness routine. One of the biggest benefits is that they are time-efficient. HIIT workouts can be done in as little as 10-15 minutes, making them perfect for those with busy schedules.

Additionally, HIIT workouts are great for improving cardiovascular fitness and burning fat. They have been shown to increase metabolism and improve insulin sensitivity, making them a great choice for weight loss.

Tips for Getting Started with HIIT Cardio Workouts

Before starting any new workout routine, it's important to consult with your doctor to ensure that it's safe for you to do so. Once you've been cleared, you can start incorporating HIIT cardio workouts into your routine.

When first starting out, it's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. It's also important to listen to your body and take breaks when needed.

Sample HIIT Cardio Workouts

Here are a few sample HIIT cardio workouts to get you started:

Tabata-style Bodyweight Circuit

Perform each exercise for 20 seconds, rest for 10 seconds, and repeat for a total of eight rounds. Rest for one minute in between each exercise.

  • Jumping jacks
  • Push-ups
  • Squats
  • Mountain climbers

Circuit-style Running Workout

Perform each exercise for 30 seconds, rest for 15 seconds, and move on to the next exercise. Repeat the circuit three times for a total of 15 minutes.

  • High knees
  • Butt kicks
  • Sprint
  • Walking lunges

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do HIIT cardio workouts?

A: It's recommended to do HIIT workouts 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between.

Q: Can I do HIIT workouts if I'm a beginner?

A: Yes, but it's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.

Q: Do I need any equipment for HIIT cardio workouts?

A: No, many HIIT workouts can be done with just your bodyweight. However, if you want to incorporate equipment, you can use things like dumbbells, resistance bands, or a jump rope.

Q: Are HIIT cardio workouts safe?

A: As with any workout, it's important to consult with your doctor before starting. However, when done correctly, HIIT workouts are generally safe.

Conclusion of Good HIIT Cardio Workouts

HIIT cardio workouts are a great way to get your heart rate up and burn some serious calories in a short amount of time. With a variety of exercises to choose from and the ability to tailor the workouts to your fitness level, there's no excuse not to try HIIT. Incorporate these workouts into your routine and start seeing results today.

How Long Should You Cardio To Lose Weight

Written by Christine Sep 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you struggling to lose weight and wondering how long you need to do cardio to see results? It's a common pain point for many people who are trying to shed those extra pounds.

The answer to how long you should do cardio to lose weight is not straightforward as it depends on several factors such as your fitness level, age, weight, and the intensity of your workout. However, there are some general guidelines that you can follow.

The American Heart Association recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week to maintain good health. If you want to lose weight, you need to increase the duration or intensity of your workout.

To lose weight effectively, you should aim for at least 300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 150 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week. This translates to about an hour of cardio, five days a week, if you're doing moderate-intensity exercise. However, if you're doing high-intensity exercise, you can reduce the duration to 30 minutes per day, five days a week.

The Importance of Consistency

Now that you know how long you should do cardio to lose weight, it's important to be consistent with your workout routine. Consistency is key to seeing results.

When I started incorporating cardio into my workout routine, I struggled to stay consistent. I would do cardio for a week or two, and then skip a few days, which would turn into a week, and then I would lose motivation. However, when I started doing cardio for at least 30 minutes, five days a week, I saw significant changes in my body composition.

Variety is Key

Another important factor in losing weight through cardio is variety. Doing the same workout every day can get boring, and your body may adapt to the exercise, which can hinder your progress.

I like to mix up my cardio routine by doing different types of exercises such as running, cycling, swimming, and HIIT workouts. This not only keeps my workouts interesting but also challenges my body in different ways.

The Role of Diet

While cardio is an effective way to lose weight, it's important to remember that diet plays a crucial role in weight loss. You can't out-exercise a bad diet.

When I started my weight loss journey, I focused on eating whole foods such as fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and healthy fats. I also limited my intake of processed foods and sugary drinks. This, combined with regular cardio, helped me achieve my weight loss goals.

How to Incorporate Cardio into Your Routine

If you're new to cardio, start slow and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workout. You can also try different types of exercise to find what works best for you.

Here are some tips for incorporating cardio into your routine:

  • Find a workout buddy to keep you accountable
  • Set achievable goals and track your progress
  • Make your workouts fun by listening to music or podcasts
  • Incorporate interval training to increase intensity
  • Join a fitness class or group to stay motivated

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio every day?

A: It's safe to do cardio every day as long as you're not overexerting yourself. However, it's important to listen to your body and take rest days when needed.

Q: How long does it take to see results from cardio?

A: It varies from person to person, but you can expect to see significant changes in your body composition within 4-8 weeks of regular cardio exercise.

Q: Is cardio the only way to lose weight?

A: No, cardio is not the only way to lose weight. Strength training and a healthy diet also play important roles in weight loss.

Q: Can I do cardio at home?

A: Yes, you can do cardio at home with minimal equipment such as a jump rope or resistance bands. There are also many cardio workouts available online that you can follow along with.

Conclusion of How Long Should You Cardio to Lose Weight

So, how long should you cardio to lose weight? It's recommended to aim for at least 300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 150 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week. However, it's important to be consistent with your workouts, mix up your routine, and focus on a healthy diet to see the best results.

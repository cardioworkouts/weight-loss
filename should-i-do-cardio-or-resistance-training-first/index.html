Should I Do Cardio Or Resistance Training First A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you confused about whether you should start your workout with cardio or resistance training? Don't worry; you're not alone. Many people struggle with this question, and it's essential to understand the right approach for maximum benefits. In this post, we'll explore the advantages and disadvantages of both options and help you determine what works best for you.

The Pain Points of Should I Do Cardio or Resistance Training First

Before we dive into the details, let's address the pain points related to this topic. Some people find it challenging to prioritize their workouts, while others struggle to balance their time between cardio and resistance training. Moreover, some people are unsure about the right approach that can help them get the desired results. All these factors make it difficult to decide whether you should do cardio or resistance training first.

The Target of Should I Do Cardio or Resistance Training First

The target of this post is to help you understand the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio or resistance training first. We'll explore the science behind both approaches and help you determine what works best for you.

Benefits and Drawbacks of Cardio Training First

Cardio training involves any exercise that increases your heart rate, such as running, cycling, or swimming. Doing cardio training first can help you warm up your muscles and prepare your body for more intense workouts. Moreover, it can help you burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular health. However, doing too much cardio before resistance training can tire you out and reduce your strength and performance during weightlifting exercises.

How to Optimize Cardio Training First?

If you prefer to start with cardio training, it's essential to keep it short and intense. Aim for 10-15 minutes of high-intensity cardio to warm up your body and get your heart rate up. You can also try combining cardio and resistance training with circuit training, which involves alternating between cardio and strength exercises. This approach can help you burn more calories and build muscle at the same time.

Benefits and Drawbacks of Resistance Training First

Resistance training involves any exercise that uses weights or resistance to build muscle, such as weightlifting or bodyweight exercises. Starting with resistance training can help you build strength and increase your muscle mass. Moreover, it can help you burn more calories by boosting your metabolism. However, doing too much resistance training before cardio can reduce your endurance and make it harder to perform high-intensity cardio exercises.

How to Optimize Resistance Training First?

If you prefer to start with resistance training, it's essential to focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at the same time. This approach can help you burn more calories and improve your overall strength and performance. It's also crucial to keep your workouts short and intense to avoid fatigue and maximize your gains.

Question and Answer

Q. Can I do cardio and resistance training on the same day?

A. Yes, you can do both cardio and resistance training on the same day. However, it's essential to prioritize your workouts based on your goals and fitness level. If you're new to working out, it's best to start with one type of exercise and gradually add the other.

Q. How long should I wait between cardio and resistance training?

A. It's best to wait at least 30 minutes between cardio and resistance training to allow your body to recover and avoid fatigue. You can also try doing cardio and resistance training on alternate days to avoid overtraining.

Q. What's the best time to do cardio or resistance training?

A. The best time to do cardio or resistance training depends on your schedule and personal preference. Some people prefer to work out in the morning for an energy boost, while others prefer to exercise in the evening to relieve stress. The most important thing is to find a time that works for you and stick to it consistently.

Q. How often should I do cardio or resistance training?

A. It's best to do cardio and resistance training at least three times a week for maximum benefits. However, the frequency and intensity of your workouts depend on your fitness goals and level. It's essential to listen to your body and adjust your workouts accordingly.

Conclusion of Should I Do Cardio or Resistance Training First

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio or resistance training first depends on your fitness goals and personal preference. Both approaches have their benefits and drawbacks, and it's essential to find the right balance that works for you. Remember, consistency is key, and it's essential to maintain a regular exercise routine to achieve your fitness goals.

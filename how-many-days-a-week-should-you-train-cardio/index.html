How Many Days A Week Should You Train Cardio

Written by Christine Jun 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering how often you should be doing cardio workouts? Are you unsure if you're doing too much or not enough? It can be tough to figure out the optimal amount of cardio to do each week. But don't worry, we’ve got you covered!

The answer to how many days a week you should be doing cardio depends on several factors, including your fitness goals, current fitness level, and overall health. Generally, it is recommended to do cardio 3-5 times per week for most people.

Cardiovascular exercise, or cardio for short, is any type of exercise that raises your heart rate and works your cardiovascular system. Examples of cardio include running, cycling, swimming, and dancing. Doing cardio regularly can help improve your heart health, boost your mood, and even aid in weight loss.

In summary, the recommended amount of cardio per week is 3-5 days for most people. However, it's important to listen to your body and adjust accordingly. If you're new to working out, start with 2-3 days per week and gradually increase as you get stronger. If you're already doing cardio 5 days per week and feel burned out, it's okay to scale back.

Personal Experience with Cardio

Personally, I aim to do cardio 4 times a week. I find that this amount works well for me in terms of improving my fitness level and maintaining a healthy weight. However, there have been times where I've done too much cardio and felt exhausted and burnt out. It's important to find a balance that works for you.

The Benefits of Cardio

Aside from improving heart health and aiding in weight loss, there are many other benefits to doing cardio. It can help reduce stress and anxiety, improve sleep quality, and even boost brain function. Plus, cardio can be a fun way to get your blood pumping and release endorphins.

Tips for Getting Started with Cardio

If you're new to cardio, it's important to start slow and gradually build up. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  1. Choose an activity that you enjoy - this will make it more likely that you'll stick with it.
  2. Start with shorter sessions and gradually increase the time and intensity.
  3. Listen to your body - if something feels too difficult, take a break or scale back.
  4. Make it a habit - schedule your cardio workouts in advance and stick to them.

How to Make Cardio Fun

Cardio doesn't have to be boring! Here are some ideas for making it more enjoyable:

  1. Listen to music or a podcast while you exercise.
  2. Find a workout buddy to keep you accountable and make it more social.
  3. Vary your routine - try different activities or workouts to keep things interesting.
  4. Reward yourself - treat yourself to a healthy snack or a relaxing bath after a tough workout.

My Favorite Cardio Workouts

My favorite cardio workouts include running, cycling, and dancing. I love running outdoors and feeling the fresh air on my face. Cycling is a great low-impact option that still gets your heart pumping. And dancing is a fun way to get moving and release stress.

Question and Answer

Q: Can you do cardio every day?

A: While it is possible to do cardio every day, it's not necessarily recommended. Your body needs rest days to recover and prevent injury. If you want to do some form of exercise every day, try mixing in strength training or other low-impact activities.

Q: How long should a cardio workout be?

A: A cardio workout should be at least 30 minutes to be effective. However, if you're short on time, even 10-15 minutes can be beneficial.

Q: Can you do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do both cardio and strength training on the same day. However, it's important to prioritize your goals and schedule your workouts accordingly. If you're focusing on building strength, do strength training first and cardio after. If you're training for a race or trying to improve your endurance, do cardio first and strength training after.

Q: How can I tell if I'm doing too much cardio?

A: If you're feeling exhausted, burnt out, or experiencing pain or injury, you may be doing too much cardio. It's important to listen to your body and scale back if necessary. It's also a good idea to mix in other types of exercise and take rest days as needed.

Conclusion of How Many Days a Week Should You Train Cardio

Cardio is an important component of a healthy lifestyle, but it's important to find a balance that works for you. Aim for 3-5 days per week and listen to your body. Don't forget to mix in other types of exercise and make it fun!

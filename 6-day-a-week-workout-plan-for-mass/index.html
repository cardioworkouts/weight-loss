6 Day A Week Workout Plan For Mass A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 01, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking to build muscle mass and get in shape? Do you want to know how to create a 6 day a week workout plan for mass? If so, you've come to the right place. In this article, we will provide you with a complete guide on how to create a workout plan that will help you gain mass and achieve your fitness goals.

If you're struggling to gain muscle mass, you're not alone. Many people find it difficult to build muscle, even with regular exercise and a healthy diet. The key to success is having a well-designed workout plan that targets all the major muscle groups and includes the right exercises and sets/reps.

The target of a 6 day a week workout plan for mass is to increase muscle hypertrophy, strength, and endurance. This type of training involves working out for six days a week, with one day of rest. The workout plan should focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once, such as squats, deadlifts, bench press, and pull-ups.

In summary, a 6 day a week workout plan for mass is an effective way to build muscle and get in shape. To create a successful plan, you should focus on compound exercises, work out for six days a week, and include a rest day.

Compound Exercises: The Foundation of 6 Day a Week Workout Plan for Mass

When it comes to building muscle mass, compound exercises are essential. These exercises work multiple muscle groups at once, allowing you to lift heavier weights and stimulate more muscle fibers. Some examples of compound exercises include squats, deadlifts, bench press, and pull-ups.

I've personally found that compound exercises are the most effective way to build muscle mass. When I first started working out, I made the mistake of focusing on isolation exercises, such as bicep curls and tricep extensions. While these exercises can be effective, they don't provide the same level of muscle stimulation as compound exercises.

When designing your 6 day a week workout plan for mass, make sure to include plenty of compound exercises. Aim to work out all the major muscle groups, including your chest, back, legs, shoulders, biceps, and triceps. You should also vary your exercises to prevent boredom and plateauing.

The Importance of Rest and Recovery

While working out six days a week may seem intense, it's important to remember that rest and recovery are just as important as exercise. Your muscles need time to repair and grow after a workout, so make sure to include at least one rest day in your plan.

During your rest day, you can still be active, but you should avoid intense exercise. Instead, focus on low-impact activities, such as walking, yoga, or stretching. This will help improve your flexibility, reduce soreness, and prevent injury.

Creating Your 6 Day a Week Workout Plan for Mass

When creating your 6 day a week workout plan for mass, there are a few key things to consider:

  • Focus on compound exercises
  • Include all major muscle groups
  • Vary your exercises and sets/reps
  • Include at least one rest day

Here's an example of a 6 day a week workout plan for mass:

  • Monday: Chest and Triceps
  • Tuesday: Back and Biceps
  • Wednesday: Legs
  • Thursday: Shoulders and Traps
  • Friday: Chest and Triceps
  • Saturday: Back and Biceps
  • Sunday: Rest Day

How to Progress with Your 6 Day a Week Workout Plan for Mass

In order to continue building muscle mass, it's important to progress your workouts over time. This can be achieved by increasing the weight you lift, adding more sets/reps, or reducing your rest time between sets.

It's important to note that progress should be gradual and sustainable. Don't push yourself too hard too quickly, as this can lead to injury or burnout. Aim to make small improvements each week, and track your progress using a workout log or app.

Question and Answer

Q: Is a 6 day a week workout plan for mass suitable for beginners?

A: It depends on your fitness level and experience. If you're new to working out, it's best to start with a simpler plan and gradually increase your intensity over time.

Q: Can I do cardio on my rest day?

A: Yes, you can do low-impact cardio on your rest day, such as walking or cycling. Just make sure to avoid intense exercise that could interfere with your recovery.

Q: How many sets/reps should I do for each exercise?

A: It depends on your goals and fitness level. Generally, for mass-building, aim for 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps per exercise.

Q: How long should I rest between sets?

A: Rest time can vary depending on your goals and fitness level. Generally, aim for 1-2 minutes of rest between sets for mass-building exercises.

Conclusion of 6 Day a Week Workout Plan for Mass

A 6 day a week workout plan for mass can be an effective way to build muscle and achieve your fitness goals. By focusing on compound exercises, including all major muscle groups, and incorporating rest and recovery into your plan, you can create a workout routine that helps you gain mass and improve your overall health and fitness.

What Is High Intensity Cardio A Beginner s Guide

Written by Petter Jul 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a way to shake up your fitness routine and take your cardio to the next level? High intensity cardio might be exactly what you're looking for. In this beginner's guide, we'll explore what high intensity cardio is, the benefits it offers, and how to get started.

The Pain Points of Traditional Cardio

If you've ever hit the treadmill for a long, slow jog and found yourself bored or frustrated, you're not alone. Traditional cardio can be monotonous, time-consuming, and often fails to deliver the results you're looking for. Additionally, low-intensity cardio can be tough on your joints and may not challenge your cardiovascular system enough to make a significant impact on your overall health.

What Is High Intensity Cardio?

High intensity cardio is a form of exercise that involves short bursts of all-out effort, followed by periods of rest or active recovery. This type of cardio is designed to push your body to its limits and challenge your cardiovascular system to adapt and improve over time. Examples of high intensity cardio include sprinting, jumping jacks, burpees, and hill sprints.

The target of high intensity cardio is to increase your heart rate and challenge your body to work harder than it normally would during traditional cardio. This type of exercise can help you burn more calories in a shorter amount of time, boost your metabolism, and improve your cardiovascular health.

My Personal Experience with High Intensity Cardio

When I first started incorporating high intensity cardio into my fitness routine, I was intimidated. I had always been a slow and steady cardio person and the idea of pushing myself to my limits was scary. But once I got started, I found that I loved the challenge and the energy it brought to my workouts. I started with short bursts of high intensity cardio, like 30-second sprints, and gradually worked my way up to longer intervals.

Now, I'm hooked on high intensity cardio and love the way it makes me feel. I have more energy throughout the day, I've seen improvements in my body composition, and I feel more confident in my fitness abilities.

The Benefits of High Intensity Cardio

So, what are the benefits of high intensity cardio? Let's take a look:

  • Improved cardiovascular health
  • Increased calorie burn and fat loss
  • Boosted metabolism
  • Increased energy and endurance
  • Improved body composition
  • Time-efficient workouts

Getting Started with High Intensity Cardio

If you're interested in trying high intensity cardio for yourself, it's important to start slowly and work your way up. Here are some tips for getting started:

  • Choose exercises that challenge you but are also safe for your fitness level
  • Start with short intervals and gradually increase the length of your high intensity periods
  • Make sure to warm up properly before your workout
  • Stay hydrated and listen to your body

Incorporating High Intensity Cardio into Your Routine

If you're not sure how to incorporate high intensity cardio into your fitness routine, there are a few ways to get started:

  • Add high intensity intervals to your traditional cardio workouts
  • Try a high intensity cardio class, like HIIT or Tabata
  • Use bodyweight exercises to create your own high intensity circuit

FAQs About High Intensity Cardio

Q: How often should I do high intensity cardio?

A: It's recommended to do high intensity cardio 1-2 times per week, in addition to other forms of exercise.

Q: Can high intensity cardio be dangerous?

A: Like any form of exercise, high intensity cardio can be dangerous if not done properly. Make sure to warm up, choose exercises that are safe for your fitness level, and listen to your body.

Q: Is high intensity cardio better than traditional cardio?

A: It depends on your goals and fitness level. High intensity cardio can be more time-efficient and offer greater benefits for fat loss and cardiovascular health, but traditional cardio still has its place in a well-rounded fitness routine.

Q: Can I do high intensity cardio if I have joint pain?

A: It's important to choose exercises that are safe for your joints and avoid high-impact movements if you have joint pain. Consult with a doctor or physical therapist before starting any new exercise routine.

Conclusion of What Is High Intensity Cardio

If you're looking to shake up your cardio routine and challenge your body in new ways, high intensity cardio might be exactly what you need. With its many benefits and time-efficient workouts, high intensity cardio can help you achieve your fitness goals and improve your overall health. Remember to start slowly, choose exercises that are safe for your fitness level, and listen to your body as you incorporate high intensity cardio into your routine. Happy sweating!

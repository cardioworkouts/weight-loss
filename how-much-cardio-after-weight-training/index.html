How Much Cardio After Weight Training A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 29, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you someone who has been hitting the gym regularly but is still confused about how much cardio you should be doing after weight training? It can be overwhelming to navigate through the plethora of information available online. In this blog post, we will be discussing everything you need to know about how much cardio after weight training, answering all of your questions and concerns.

Pain Points Related to How Much Cardio After Weight Training

One of the biggest pain points that people have when it comes to cardio after weight training is not knowing how much is too much or too little. There is also confusion about the ideal time to do cardio and whether it should be done before or after weight training. Additionally, there is a common misconception that cardio can hinder muscle growth, which makes people hesitant to incorporate it into their routine.

Answering the Target of How Much Cardio After Weight Training

The amount of cardio you should do after weight training depends on your fitness goals. If you are looking to lose weight, you should aim for at least 20-30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio after your weight training session. On the other hand, if you are looking to build muscle, you can limit your cardio to 10-15 minutes of high-intensity interval training (HIIT) once or twice a week. It is also recommended to do cardio after weight training as it helps in utilizing stored fat for energy.

It is important to keep in mind that cardio should not be done at the expense of weight training. Weight training is crucial for building muscle and boosting metabolism, and cardio should be used as a supplement to it. Additionally, doing cardio before weight training can lead to fatigue, which can hinder your performance during weight training.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, the ideal amount of cardio after weight training depends on your fitness goals. If you are looking to lose weight, aim for at least 20-30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio after your weight training session. If you are looking to build muscle, limit your cardio to 10-15 minutes of HIIT once or twice a week. Cardio should be done after weight training, and weight training should not be compromised for cardio.

How Much Cardio After Weight Training: Personal Experience

As someone who is interested in fitness and weight training, I have experimented with different amounts of cardio after weight training. Initially, I used to do around 45 minutes of cardio after weight training, thinking that it would accelerate my weight loss. However, I soon realized that it was hindering my muscle growth, and I was not seeing the desired results. After doing some research, I decided to limit my cardio to 20-30 minutes of moderate-intensity after weight training, and I noticed a significant difference in my body composition. I was losing fat and gaining muscle, which was my ultimate goal.

However, it is important to note that everyone's body is different, and what works for one person may not work for another. It is crucial to listen to your body and adjust your cardio accordingly.

The Ideal Time for Cardio After Weight Training

As mentioned earlier, cardio after weight training is recommended. The reason behind this is that weight training depletes your body's glycogen stores, which leads to the utilization of stored fat for energy. Doing cardio after weight training helps in further utilizing this stored fat, leading to more efficient weight loss. Additionally, doing cardio before weight training can lead to fatigue, which can hinder your performance during weight training.

Cardio and Muscle Growth

There is a common misconception that cardio can hinder muscle growth, which makes people hesitant to incorporate it into their routine. However, this is not entirely true. While excessive cardio can lead to muscle loss, moderate amounts of cardio can actually aid in muscle growth. Cardio helps in increasing blood flow and oxygen to the muscles, which aids in recovery and growth. Additionally, cardio can help in reducing muscle soreness and preventing injury.

How to Incorporate Cardio After Weight Training

It is recommended to start with 20-30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio after weight training and gradually increase it as per your fitness goals. Additionally, it is important to switch up your cardio routine to prevent boredom and plateauing. Incorporating different types of cardio such as running, cycling, or swimming can help in keeping things interesting.

Personal Experience with Cardio After Weight Training

As someone who has been weight training for a while, I have found that incorporating cardio after weight training has been beneficial for my fitness goals. Initially, I was hesitant to do cardio as I thought it would hinder my muscle growth. However, after doing some research and experimenting, I found that cardio has helped me in losing fat and improving my endurance. I usually do 20-30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio on the treadmill or elliptical after my weight training session. I have also started incorporating HIIT once a week, which has been challenging but rewarding.

Question and Answer

Q: How much cardio should I do if I am looking to maintain my weight?

A: If you are looking to maintain your weight, you can limit your cardio to 10-15 minutes of HIIT once a week or 20-30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio after weight training.

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weight training?

A: It is recommended to do cardio after weight training as weight training depletes your body's glycogen stores, leading to the utilization of stored fat for energy. Additionally, doing cardio before weight training can lead to fatigue, which can hinder your performance during weight training.

Q: Can I do cardio on rest days?

A: Yes, you can do cardio on rest days. However, it is important to listen to your body and not overdo it. Additionally, doing cardio on rest days can aid in recovery and improve overall fitness.

Q: Can I skip cardio altogether?

A: While cardio is not essential for weight loss or muscle growth, it can aid in both of these goals. Additionally, cardio has numerous health benefits such as improving cardiovascular health and reducing the risk of chronic diseases. Therefore, it is recommended to incorporate some form of cardio in your fitness routine.

Conclusion of How Much Cardio After Weight Training

In conclusion, the ideal amount of cardio after weight training depends on your fitness goals. If you are looking to lose weight, aim for at least 20-30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio after your weight training session. If you are looking to build muscle, limit your cardio to 10-15 minutes of HIIT once or twice a week. Cardio should be done after weight training, and weight training should not be compromised for cardio. It is also important to listen to your body and adjust your cardio accordingly.

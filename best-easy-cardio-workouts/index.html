Best Easy Cardio Workouts A Beginner s Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you looking for a way to get in shape and improve your health without spending hours at the gym? Cardio workouts are a great way to get your heart rate up and burn calories, but they can be intimidating for beginners. In this post, we'll explore the best easy cardio workouts that will get you moving and feeling great.

Starting a new workout routine can be overwhelming, especially if you're not familiar with the terminology or equipment. You may feel self-conscious or unsure of yourself, which can make it difficult to stick to your goals. Additionally, many people struggle with finding the time or motivation to exercise regularly. But don't worry - with a little guidance and some easy-to-follow workouts, you can achieve your fitness goals and feel confident in your abilities.

What Are the Best Easy Cardio Workouts?

The best easy cardio workouts are those that get your heart rate up and keep it there for an extended period of time, without putting too much strain on your body. Some examples include walking, jogging, cycling, swimming, and dancing. These workouts are low-impact and can be modified to fit your fitness level and preferences.

Walking is a great way to start your fitness journey. It requires no equipment and can be done anywhere, making it the perfect choice for beginners. If you're looking for a more challenging workout, jogging or running can provide a higher intensity workout. Cycling is another low-impact option that can be done indoors or outdoors, while swimming is a great way to get a full-body workout without putting stress on your joints. And if you're looking for a fun, social way to exercise, dancing is a great choice that can improve your coordination and balance.

My Experience with Walking and Jogging

Personally, I started my fitness journey with walking and eventually moved on to jogging. I found that walking helped me build up my endurance and stamina, and I was able to gradually increase my speed and distance. Once I felt comfortable with walking, I began incorporating short jogs into my routine. Over time, I was able to run for longer periods of time and eventually completed my first 5K race.

One of the things I love about walking and jogging is that they require minimal equipment and can be done anywhere. I enjoy getting outside and exploring new neighborhoods, and I find that walking and jogging help me clear my mind and reduce stress. Plus, they're a great way to get some fresh air and vitamin D!

Cycling for a Low-Impact Workout

If you're looking for a low-impact workout that's easy on your joints, cycling is a great option. You can ride a stationary bike at the gym or invest in a bike for outdoor rides. Cycling is a great way to improve your cardiovascular health and build leg strength. Plus, it's a fun way to explore your surroundings and get some fresh air.

One of the benefits of cycling is that you can vary the intensity of your workout by adjusting the resistance on your bike. This means you can challenge yourself without putting too much strain on your body. And if you're a fan of group fitness, many gyms offer cycling classes that are led by an instructor and set to music.

The Benefits of Swimming

Swimming is another low-impact cardio workout that's great for beginners. It's a full-body workout that can improve your cardiovascular health, build muscle, and increase flexibility. Plus, it's an excellent way to beat the heat during the summer months.

Swimming can be done in a pool or open water, depending on your preferences. If you're new to swimming, it's a good idea to take some lessons or work with a coach to improve your technique and ensure your safety. And if you're not comfortable swimming laps, you can still get a great workout by doing water aerobics or other pool-based exercises.

Dancing for Fun and Fitness

If you're looking for a cardio workout that doesn't feel like exercise, dancing is a great choice. It's a fun, social way to get your heart rate up and burn calories. Plus, it can improve your balance, coordination, and flexibility.

There are many different styles of dance to choose from, including hip hop, salsa, ballroom, and Zumba. You can take classes at a dance studio or gym, or follow along with online videos in the comfort of your own home. And if you're feeling extra adventurous, you can even join a dance team or perform in a recital!

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio workouts?

A: It's recommended that adults get at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise. You can spread this out over the week however you like - for example, you could do 30 minutes of exercise five days a week, or 60 minutes three times a week.

Q: How do I know if I'm exercising at the right intensity?

A: One way to measure your exercise intensity is by using the Borg Rating of Perceived Exertion (RPE) scale. This is a subjective measure that asks you to rate your perceived exertion on a scale of 0-10, with 0 being no exertion and 10 being maximal exertion. You should aim to exercise at an RPE of 5-6 for moderate-intensity exercise, and 7-8 for vigorous-intensity exercise.

Q: Can I do cardio workouts if I have joint pain?

A: Yes! There are many low-impact cardio workouts that are easy on your joints, such as walking, cycling, and swimming. Additionally, there are modifications you can make to high-impact workouts to reduce impact and stress on your joints.

Q: How do I stay motivated to exercise?

A: There are many ways to stay motivated to exercise, such as setting realistic goals, finding an exercise buddy or group, tracking your progress, and rewarding yourself for reaching milestones. It's important to find workouts that you enjoy and that fit your lifestyle, so that exercise becomes a habit rather than a chore.

Conclusion of Best Easy Cardio Workouts

Cardio workouts are a great way to improve your health and fitness, and they don't have to be intimidating or difficult. By choosing low-impact workouts that you enjoy, you can make exercise a part of your daily routine and feel confident in your abilities. Remember to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity and duration, and don't be afraid to ask for help or guidance if you need it. With the right mindset and a little motivation, you can achieve your fitness goals and feel great in the process!

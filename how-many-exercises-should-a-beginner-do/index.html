How Many Exercises Should A Beginner Do A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Nov 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Starting a workout routine can be overwhelming for beginners. With so many exercises out there, it can be difficult to know where to start and how many exercises to include in a workout. In this guide, we’ll answer the question of how many exercises should a beginner do and provide tips for creating a successful workout routine.

As a beginner, it’s common to experience pain points such as not knowing which exercises to do or how many to include. It’s important to remember that starting small and gradually increasing intensity is the key to success. Overdoing it in the beginning can lead to injury and burnout, which can set you back in your fitness journey.

The recommended number of exercises for a beginner is around 8-10. This number includes both upper and lower body exercises, as well as core work. It’s important to focus on compound movements that work multiple muscle groups at once, such as squats and push-ups. This will help you get the most out of your workout and save time in the gym.

In summary, as a beginner, it’s important to start small and gradually increase intensity. The recommended number of exercises is around 8-10, focusing on compound movements that work multiple muscle groups.

Why 8-10 Exercises is the Sweet Spot for Beginners

When I first started working out, I made the mistake of trying to do too much too soon. I was overwhelmed by the number of exercises out there and wanted to include them all in my workouts. This approach led to burnout and injury, which set me back in my fitness journey.

After doing some research, I discovered that the recommended number of exercises for beginners is around 8-10. This number allows for a well-rounded workout that targets all major muscle groups, without overwhelming the body with too much intensity.

The Importance of Compound Movements for Beginners

Compound movements are exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once. These exercises are important for beginners because they allow you to get the most out of your workout and save time in the gym. Examples of compound movements include squats, deadlifts, push-ups, and pull-ups.

When starting out, it’s important to focus on perfecting your form with these exercises before moving on to more advanced variations. This will help you avoid injury and ensure that you’re getting the most out of your workout.

How to Incorporate Cardio into Your Beginner Workout Routine

Cardiovascular exercise is an important component of any workout routine. As a beginner, it’s important to start small and gradually increase intensity. A good starting point is 20-30 minutes of cardio, 3-4 times per week.

Examples of cardio include running, cycling, swimming, and jumping rope. It’s important to choose an activity that you enjoy, so that you’re more likely to stick with it long-term.

Tips for Creating a Successful Beginner Workout Routine

1. Start small and gradually increase intensity.

2. Focus on compound movements that work multiple muscle groups.

3. Incorporate cardio into your routine gradually, starting with 20-30 minutes, 3-4 times per week.

4. Choose exercises and activities that you enjoy, so that you’re more likely to stick with it long-term.

My Personal Experience with Beginner Workouts

When I first started working out, I was overwhelmed by the number of exercises out there. I tried to include too many exercises in my workouts, which led to burnout and injury. After doing some research, I discovered that focusing on compound movements and starting small was the key to success.

Now, my workouts consist of around 8-10 exercises, focusing on compound movements such as squats, deadlifts, and push-ups. I’ve also incorporated cardio into my routine gradually, starting with 20-30 minutes, 3-4 times per week.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I include more than 10 exercises in my beginner workout?

A: While it’s possible to include more than 10 exercises in your workout, it’s not recommended for beginners. Starting small and gradually increasing intensity is the key to success.

Q: Should I focus on upper or lower body exercises?

A: It’s important to focus on both upper and lower body exercises for a well-rounded workout. Compound movements that work multiple muscle groups are the most effective.

Q: How often should I do cardio?

A: As a beginner, it’s recommended to start with 20-30 minutes of cardio, 3-4 times per week. Gradually increase intensity over time.

Q: Can I change my workout routine as I progress?

A: Yes, as you progress, you can change your workout routine to include more advanced exercises and increase intensity.

Conclusion of How Many Exercises Should a Beginner Do

As a beginner, it’s important to start small and gradually increase intensity. The recommended number of exercises is around 8-10, focusing on compound movements that work multiple muscle groups. Incorporating cardio into your routine gradually is also important for overall fitness. By following these tips, you can create a successful beginner workout routine that will set you up for long-term success.

Should I Do Weights Before Or After Cardio The Ultimate Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you confused about whether you should do weights before or after cardio? You are not alone! This is a common question that many fitness enthusiasts struggle with. The good news is that there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. Your decision should be based on your fitness goals, personal preference, and schedule.

Many people struggle with this decision because they have different pain points. Some people are concerned about losing muscle mass if they do cardio before weights. Others worry about not having enough energy for cardio if they do weights first. Some people are simply short on time and want to know the most efficient way to get the most out of their workout.

The bottom line is that there is no right or wrong answer when it comes to should i do weights before or after cardio. Your decision will depend on your goals and preferences. However, there are some things to consider when making this decision.

Here are some important things to keep in mind when deciding whether to do weights before or after cardio:

Target of Should I Do Weights Before or After Cardio

My personal experience has been that doing weights before cardio works best for me. I find that I have more energy and can lift heavier weights when I do weights first. This is because my muscles are fresh and not fatigued from cardio. Additionally, I find that I can get a better cardiovascular workout after lifting weights because my heart rate is already elevated.

However, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular endurance or burn more calories, then you may want to do cardio before weights. This is because you will have more energy for cardio if you do it first. Additionally, doing cardio before weights can help you burn more calories because your body will burn glycogen stores first, which are replenished during weightlifting.

Personal Experience

One thing to keep in mind is that everyone is different. What works for me may not work for you. It is important to experiment with different workout routines and see what works best for you. Try doing weights before cardio for a few weeks and then switch to doing cardio before weights for a few weeks. See which routine you enjoy more and which one helps you achieve your fitness goals.

Factors to Consider

When deciding whether to do weights before or after cardio, there are several factors to consider. These include your fitness goals, personal preferences, and schedule. If you are short on time, you may want to combine cardio and weights into one workout. This can be done by doing circuit training, where you alternate between cardio and weightlifting exercises.

How to Combine Cardio and Weights

If you decide to combine cardio and weights into one workout, there are several ways to do this. One way is to do circuit training, where you alternate between cardio and weightlifting exercises. Another way is to do supersets, where you do two exercises back-to-back with no rest in between. This can help you save time and increase the intensity of your workout.

Personal Experience

Personally, I prefer to do circuit training because it allows me to get a full-body workout in a shorter amount of time. I usually start with a five-minute warm-up on the treadmill, followed by a weightlifting exercise, then a cardio exercise, and so on. I find that this routine keeps me energized throughout the workout and helps me burn more calories.

Question and Answer

Q: Will doing cardio before weights burn muscle?

A: No, doing cardio before weights will not burn muscle. However, if you do too much cardio, you may burn more calories than you consume, which can lead to muscle loss.

Q: Can I do weights and cardio on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do weights and cardio on the same day. However, it is important to space them out so that you have enough energy for both workouts.

Q: How long should I wait between weights and cardio?

A: It is recommended to wait at least 30 minutes between weights and cardio to allow your muscles to recover.

Q: Should I do high-intensity cardio or low-intensity cardio before weights?

A: It depends on your goals. If you want to improve your cardiovascular endurance, then high-intensity cardio may be better. However, if you want to conserve energy for weightlifting, then low-intensity cardio may be a better choice.

Conclusion of Should I Do Weights Before or After Cardio

Whether you do weights before or after cardio is a personal decision that should be based on your goals and preferences. However, there are some things to consider when making this decision, such as your energy levels and fitness goals. Remember to experiment with different workout routines to see what works best for you.

Should I Do Aerobic Exercise Or Strength Training First A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who is confused about whether you should do aerobic exercise or strength training first? If yes, then you are not alone. This is a question that many fitness enthusiasts have been asking for years. Both types of exercises have their own benefits, and it can be challenging to choose one over the other. In this article, we will discuss the pros and cons of doing each exercise first and help you make an informed decision.

Pain Points Related to Should I Do Aerobic Exercise or Strength Training First

When it comes to fitness, most people have a specific goal they want to achieve. For some, it's losing weight, while for others, it's gaining muscle mass. Regardless of your goal, it's essential to choose the right type of exercise and the right order of doing them. Doing the wrong exercise first can lead to fatigue, decreased performance, and even injuries. So, it's vital to understand the pain points related to should I do aerobic exercise or strength training first.

Answer to the Target of Should I Do Aerobic Exercise or Strength Training First

The answer to this question depends on your fitness goals. If you are trying to lose weight, then doing aerobic exercise first can be beneficial. Aerobic exercise, also known as cardio, helps burn calories and fat. On the other hand, if you want to build muscle mass, then strength training should be your priority. Strength training helps build muscle and increases metabolism, which helps burn fat in the long run.

Summary of Main Points Related to Should I Do Aerobic Exercise or Strength Training First

In summary, the order of doing exercises depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight, start with aerobic exercise. If you want to build muscle mass, start with strength training. But, keep in mind that it's essential to do both types of exercises to achieve optimal fitness.

Why Start with Aerobic Exercise?

If your fitness goal is to lose weight, you should start with aerobic exercise. Aerobic exercise helps burn calories and fat, making it an excellent choice for weight loss. Additionally, aerobic exercise also helps improve heart health, increases endurance, and reduces the risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes and heart disease. Personally, I always start my workout with a 20-minute jog on the treadmill before doing any strength training exercises. This helps me warm up and get my heart rate up, making it easier to do the rest of the exercises.

Why Start with Strength Training?

If your fitness goal is to build muscle mass, you should start with strength training. Strength training helps build muscle, increases metabolism, and improves bone density. Additionally, strength training also helps burn fat in the long run. Personally, I always start my workout with a few sets of squats, deadlifts, or bench press before doing any aerobic exercise. This helps me warm up and prepare my muscles for the rest of the workout.

Should I Do Both Aerobic Exercise and Strength Training in the Same Workout?

Yes, you should do both types of exercises in the same workout. Doing both aerobic exercise and strength training can help you achieve optimal fitness. Additionally, combining both exercises can help burn more calories and fat in the long run.

How to Incorporate Both Types of Exercises in the Same Workout?

The best way to incorporate both types of exercises in the same workout is to do strength training exercises first and then follow it up with aerobic exercise. This order helps you get the most out of both types of exercises. Additionally, it's essential to give your body enough rest between exercises to avoid fatigue and injuries.

Benefits of Doing Aerobic Exercise and Strength Training

There are numerous benefits of doing both aerobic exercise and strength training. Some of the benefits are:

  • Burns calories and fat
  • Builds muscle mass
  • Increases metabolism
  • Improves heart health
  • Increases endurance
  • Reduces the risk of chronic diseases

Question and Answer about Should I Do Aerobic Exercise or Strength Training First

Q. Can I Do Aerobic Exercise and Strength Training on the Same Day?

A. Yes, you can do both types of exercises on the same day. However, it's essential to give your body enough rest between exercises to avoid fatigue and injuries.

Q. How Long Should I Do Aerobic Exercise and Strength Training?

A. It's recommended to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week. Additionally, it's recommended to do strength training exercises at least two days per week.

Q. Can I Do Aerobic Exercise and Strength Training on Alternate Days?

A. Yes, you can do both types of exercises on alternate days. However, it's essential to give your body enough rest between exercises to avoid fatigue and injuries.

Q. Should I Consult a Fitness Trainer Before Starting Any Exercise Program?

A. Yes, it's recommended to consult a fitness trainer before starting any exercise program. A fitness trainer can help you create a personalized exercise program that suits your fitness goals and abilities.

Conclusion of Should I Do Aerobic Exercise or Strength Training First

In conclusion, the order of doing exercises depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight, start with aerobic exercise. If you want to build muscle mass, start with strength training. But, keep in mind that it's essential to do both types of exercises to achieve optimal fitness. Additionally, it's recommended to consult a fitness trainer before starting any exercise program to avoid injuries and achieve your fitness goals.

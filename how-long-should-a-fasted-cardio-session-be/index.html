How Long Should A Fasted Cardio Session Be

Written by Christine Aug 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to improve your cardiovascular health and burn fat? Fasted cardio might be the answer you've been searching for. But how long should a fasted cardio session be? Let's take a closer look.

If you're new to fasted cardio, you might be wondering about the benefits of working out in a fasted state. When you exercise on an empty stomach, your body is forced to rely on stored fat for energy. This can lead to increased fat burning, improved insulin sensitivity, and a more efficient metabolism. However, it's important to note that fasted cardio isn't for everyone. If you have a medical condition, are pregnant, or have a history of eating disorders, it's important to consult with your doctor before trying fasted cardio.

My Personal Experience with Fasted Cardio

Before we dive into how long a fasted cardio session should be, let me share my personal experience with this type of workout. I started doing fasted cardio a few months ago, and I've noticed a significant difference in my body composition. I've been able to shed fat and maintain muscle, and I feel more energized throughout the day. However, I had to work up to longer sessions gradually. At first, I could only handle 20-30 minutes of fasted cardio. Now, I'm up to 45-60 minutes.

How Long Should a Fasted Cardio Session Be?

The ideal length of a fasted cardio session depends on your fitness level, goals, and schedule. Generally, experts recommend starting with shorter sessions and working your way up to longer ones. If you're new to fasted cardio, start with 20-30 minutes and gradually increase your time over the course of several weeks. Once you've built up your endurance, you can aim for 45-60 minute sessions.

Factors to Consider

When deciding how long your fasted cardio session should be, there are a few factors to consider. First, think about your fitness level. If you're new to exercise, you might need to start with shorter sessions and gradually increase your time. Second, consider your goals. If you're looking to burn fat and improve your cardiovascular health, longer sessions might be more effective. Finally, think about your schedule. If you have limited time to work out, shorter sessions might be more realistic.

How to Maximize Your Fasted Cardio Workout

To get the most out of your fasted cardio session, there are a few things you can do. First, make sure to stay hydrated before, during, and after your workout. Second, consider incorporating high-intensity intervals into your routine. This can help increase your fat-burning potential and improve your endurance. Finally, don't forget to refuel after your workout. Eating a balanced meal with protein and carbohydrates can help your body recover and prepare for your next workout.

When to Avoid Fasted Cardio

While fasted cardio can be beneficial for many people, there are some instances when it's best to avoid this type of workout. If you have a medical condition, are pregnant, or have a history of eating disorders, it's important to consult with your doctor before trying fasted cardio. Additionally, if you feel dizzy, lightheaded, or nauseous during your workout, it's important to stop and rest.

Conclusion

So, how long should a fasted cardio session be? Ultimately, the answer depends on your fitness level, goals, and schedule. However, starting with shorter sessions and gradually increasing your time is a good rule of thumb. Remember to stay hydrated, refuel after your workout, and listen to your body. With a little patience and dedication, you can reap the benefits of fasted cardio and improve your overall health.

Question and Answer

Q: Is fasted cardio safe?

A: For most people, fasted cardio is safe. However, if you have a medical condition, are pregnant, or have a history of eating disorders, it's important to consult with your doctor before trying fasted cardio.

Q: How often should I do fasted cardio?

A: The frequency of your fasted cardio workouts depends on your goals and your body's response to the exercise. Generally, starting with 2-3 sessions per week and gradually increasing your frequency is a good approach.

Q: Can I do fasted cardio in the evening?

A: While fasted cardio is traditionally done in the morning, you can do it at any time of day. Just make sure to wait at least 2-3 hours after your last meal before starting your workout.

Q: Should I eat before or after my fasted cardio session?

A: It's best to eat a balanced meal with protein and carbohydrates after your fasted cardio session. This can help your body recover and prepare for your next workout.

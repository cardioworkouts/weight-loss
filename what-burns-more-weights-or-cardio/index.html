What Burns More Weights Or Cardio

Written by Aprilia Jul 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you wondering which exercise burns more calories: lifting weights or doing cardio? You're not alone. With so much conflicting information out there, it can be tough to know which approach is best. But fear not, as we break down the science behind what burns more weights or cardio, and give you the answers you seek.

Many people struggle with weight loss, and exercise is often touted as a solution. However, it's not always clear which type of exercise is best. Should you be lifting weights or doing cardio? The truth is, it depends on your goals and preferences, as well as your body type and fitness level.

The short answer is that both types of exercise can help you burn calories and lose weight. However, the way in which they do so is different. Weightlifting is a form of anaerobic exercise, which means it doesn't rely on oxygen for energy. Cardio, on the other hand, is aerobic exercise, which does rely on oxygen. This difference can impact the number of calories you burn during and after your workout.

In summary, both weights and cardio can help you burn calories and lose weight. The best approach is to find a balance that works for you and your body.

Personal Experience: Cardio vs. Weightlifting

Personally, I have found that a combination of cardio and weightlifting has been the most effective for me. When I focus solely on cardio, I tend to lose weight but also lose muscle mass. On the other hand, when I focus only on weightlifting, I gain muscle but don't see as much of a difference in my overall weight.

That's why I found that incorporating both types of exercise into my routine has been the best approach. I typically do cardio three times a week and lift weights twice a week. This allows me to burn calories, build muscle, and maintain a healthy weight.

The Benefits of Cardio

Cardio has a number of benefits beyond weight loss. It can improve your heart health, increase your endurance, and boost your mood. Plus, there are many different types of cardio to choose from, such as running, cycling, swimming, and dancing. This variety can help keep your workouts interesting and prevent boredom.

How Cardio Burns Calories

Cardio burns calories by increasing your heart rate and breathing rate. This in turn increases your metabolism, which means you'll continue to burn calories even after you've finished your workout. The number of calories you burn will depend on a variety of factors, such as your weight, the intensity of your workout, and the duration of your workout.

Tips for Effective Cardio Workouts

If you want to make the most of your cardio workouts, there are a few tips to keep in mind. First, aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio per day, five days a week. This can include a combination of moderate-intensity and high-intensity workouts. Second, mix things up by trying different types of cardio, such as running, cycling, and swimming. Finally, make sure you're fueling your body with healthy foods to support your workouts.

The Benefits of Weightlifting

Weightlifting is often associated with building muscle mass, but it has a number of other benefits as well. It can increase your bone density, improve your posture, and boost your metabolism. Plus, lifting weights can help you burn calories even when you're not working out.

How Weightlifting Burns Calories

Weightlifting burns calories by increasing your muscle mass. Muscle tissue burns more calories than fat tissue, which means that the more muscle you have, the more calories you'll burn at rest. In addition, weightlifting can help you build lean muscle mass, which can help give you a toned and defined look.

Tips for Effective Weightlifting Workouts

If you're new to weightlifting, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase your weights and reps over time. It's also important to focus on proper form to prevent injury. Finally, make sure you're giving your muscles time to recover between workouts, as this can help prevent overuse injuries and improve your overall results.

Combining Cardio and Weightlifting

As I mentioned earlier, I have found that a combination of cardio and weightlifting has been the most effective for me. And research supports this approach as well. A study published in the journal Obesity found that a combination of cardio and weightlifting was more effective for weight loss than either type of exercise alone.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Does cardio or weightlifting burn more calories during a workout?

A: Cardio typically burns more calories during a workout, as it raises your heart rate and breathing rate more than weightlifting does.

Q: Does weightlifting or cardio burn more calories after a workout?

A: Weightlifting typically burns more calories after a workout, as it increases your muscle mass and metabolism.

Q: Can you lose weight by weightlifting alone?

A: Yes, you can lose weight by weightlifting alone, but it may be more effective to combine weightlifting with cardio for optimal results.

Q: How often should I do cardio and weightlifting?

A: It depends on your goals and fitness level, but a good starting point is to aim for three cardio workouts and two weightlifting workouts per week.

Conclusion of What Burns More: Weights or Cardio

When it comes to what burns more weights or cardio, the answer is that both types of exercise can be effective for weight loss and overall health. The best approach is to find a balance that works for you and your body, and to incorporate both types of exercise into your routine. Remember to start slowly, focus on proper form, and give your body time to recover between workouts. With persistence and dedication, you can achieve your fitness goals and feel great in your own skin.

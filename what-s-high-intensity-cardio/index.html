What s High Intensity Cardio A Beginner s Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old boring cardio routine? Do you want to take your fitness to the next level? High intensity cardio might be just what you need to spice up your workout and achieve your fitness goals.

Many people struggle with the monotony of traditional cardio workouts. Spending hours on a treadmill or elliptical can be boring and unproductive. High intensity cardio, on the other hand, offers a more dynamic and efficient way to burn calories and improve your overall fitness.

What's High Intensity Cardio?

High intensity cardio, also known as HIIT (High Intensity Interval Training), is a type of workout that involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. The goal of HIIT is to get your heart rate up and keep it there for a sustained period of time.

During a HIIT workout, you'll alternate between high-intensity exercises (such as sprinting, jumping jacks, or burpees) and periods of rest or low-intensity exercise (such as jogging or walking). This type of workout is designed to be challenging and intense, but also highly effective in terms of burning calories and improving cardiovascular health.

Overall, HIIT is a great way to improve your fitness and burn fat in a shorter amount of time than traditional cardio workouts. It's also a great way to challenge yourself and break out of your workout rut.

My Experience with High Intensity Cardio

As someone who used to dread cardio workouts, I was hesitant to try high intensity cardio at first. But once I gave it a chance, I was hooked. I loved the challenge of pushing myself to my limits and the feeling of accomplishment after completing a tough HIIT workout.

One of my favorite HIIT workouts involves sprinting for 30 seconds followed by 30 seconds of rest, repeated for 20-30 minutes. It's a tough workout, but it always leaves me feeling energized and accomplished.

The Benefits of High Intensity Cardio

There are many benefits to incorporating high intensity cardio into your fitness routine. Here are just a few:

  • Burns more calories than traditional cardio
  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Increases endurance and stamina
  • Can be done anywhere with little to no equipment
  • Challenges your body and helps prevent workout boredom

How to Incorporate High Intensity Cardio into Your Routine

If you're interested in trying high intensity cardio, there are many ways to incorporate it into your fitness routine. Here are a few tips:

  • Start slowly and gradually increase intensity
  • Mix up your exercises to prevent boredom and challenge your body
  • Try a HIIT workout class or find a workout video online
  • Experiment with different work-to-rest ratios to find what works best for you

What to Expect During a High Intensity Cardio Workout

During a high intensity cardio workout, you can expect to feel challenged and energized. You'll likely sweat and breathe heavily, but you'll also feel a sense of accomplishment as you push yourself to your limits.

It's important to listen to your body and take breaks as needed. Don't be afraid to modify exercises or take a longer rest period if you need to.

FAQs About High Intensity Cardio

1. Is high intensity cardio safe for beginners?

Yes, high intensity cardio can be safe for beginners as long as you start slowly and gradually increase intensity. It's important to listen to your body and take breaks as needed.

2. How often should I do high intensity cardio?

It's recommended to do high intensity cardio 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between workouts.

3. Can I do high intensity cardio if I have joint pain?

If you have joint pain, it's important to talk to your doctor before starting any new exercise routine. High impact exercises (such as jumping jacks or burpees) may not be suitable for those with joint pain.

4. Can I do high intensity cardio if I don't have access to a gym?

Yes, high intensity cardio can be done anywhere with little to no equipment. You can try a HIIT workout video online or create your own workout using bodyweight exercises.

Conclusion of What's High Intensity Cardio

Overall, high intensity cardio is a great way to improve your fitness and burn fat in a shorter amount of time than traditional cardio workouts. It's a challenging and dynamic way to push yourself to your limits and achieve your fitness goals.

Is Walking Or Elliptical Better For Weight Loss

Written by Robby Oct 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you trying to lose weight but not sure whether walking or using an elliptical is the better option? It can be confusing to choose between the two, but the good news is that both can help you shed those extra pounds. In this post, we'll explore the benefits of walking and using an elliptical for weight loss, and help you decide which one is right for you.

Pain Points

When it comes to weight loss, many people struggle with finding an exercise routine that works for them. Some people may find walking to be too slow or boring, while others may find an elliptical to be too intense. It can be frustrating to put in the effort and not see the results you want.

Is Walking or Elliptical Better for Weight Loss?

The truth is, both walking and using an elliptical can be effective for weight loss. The key is to find the one that works best for you and your goals. Here are some things to consider:

Calories Burned: Walking and using an elliptical can both burn calories, but the amount you burn depends on factors such as your weight, speed, and intensity. According to Harvard Health Publishing, a 155-pound person can burn roughly 260 calories in 30 minutes of brisk walking, and around 335 calories in 30 minutes of using an elliptical.

Impact on Joints: Walking is a low-impact exercise, which means it puts less stress on your joints. This can be beneficial for people who have joint pain or injuries. Using an elliptical is also low-impact, but it can put more stress on your knees if you use it incorrectly.

Muscle Engagement: Walking primarily works your leg muscles, while using an elliptical can engage more muscles, including your arms and core. This can help you burn more calories and tone your entire body.

Personal Experience: Walking

Personally, I prefer walking for weight loss. It's a simple and straightforward exercise that I can do anywhere, anytime. I like to listen to music or podcasts while I walk, which makes the time go by faster. Plus, walking outside can be a great way to get some fresh air and vitamin D.

One thing to keep in mind is that if you want to see significant weight loss results from walking, you may need to increase your speed or distance over time. It's also important to incorporate other forms of exercise and a healthy diet into your routine.

Personal Experience: Elliptical

On the other hand, some people may prefer using an elliptical for weight loss. It can be a more intense workout that gets your heart rate up, and it can be easier on your joints than running or other high-impact exercises. I personally find the elliptical to be a bit boring, but I know others who love it.

One thing to keep in mind with the elliptical is to make sure you're using proper form. If you're hunching over or leaning on the handles, you may not be engaging your core or getting the full benefits of the exercise.

Benefits of Walking for Weight Loss

Walking has several benefits for weight loss, including:

  • Low-impact exercise that's easy on your joints
  • Can be done anywhere, anytime
  • Can be a social activity if you walk with a friend or group
  • Can improve your mood and reduce stress
  • Can be a gateway exercise that leads to more intense workouts

Tips for Walking for Weight Loss

If you want to make the most of your walking workouts, try these tips:

  • Wear comfortable, supportive shoes
  • Incorporate intervals of faster walking or uphill walking
  • Incorporate strength training exercises (like lunges or squats) during your walk
  • Track your steps or distance using a pedometer or fitness tracker
  • Make walking a habit by scheduling it into your day

Personal Experience: Conclusion

Overall, both walking and using an elliptical can be effective for weight loss. It's important to choose the one that works best for you and your goals, and to make sure you're incorporating other forms of exercise and a healthy diet into your routine. Personally, I prefer walking for weight loss because it's simple, low-impact, and enjoyable for me. But everyone is different, so find what works best for you!

Question and Answer

Q: Can you lose weight just by walking?

A: Yes, you can lose weight by walking, but it may take longer than other forms of exercise. To see significant weight loss results, you may need to increase your speed or distance over time.

Q: Is the elliptical better than walking for burning calories?

A: According to Harvard Health Publishing, using an elliptical can burn more calories than walking, but it depends on factors such as your weight, speed, and intensity.

Q: Can walking on an incline help with weight loss?

A: Yes, walking on an incline can help you burn more calories and engage different muscles. You can also try interval training, where you alternate between walking on a flat surface and walking on an incline.

Q: How often should I walk or use an elliptical for weight loss?

A: It's recommended to aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week (such as brisk walking) or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week (such as using an elliptical). You can break this up into smaller sessions throughout the week.

Conclusion of Is Walking or Elliptical Better for Weight Loss

Whether you choose walking or using an elliptical for weight loss, the most important thing is to find an exercise routine that you enjoy and can stick to. Both can help you burn calories, improve your health, and reach your weight loss goals. Experiment with both and see what works best for you!

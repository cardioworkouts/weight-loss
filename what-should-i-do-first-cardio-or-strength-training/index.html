What Should I Do First Cardio Or Strength Training

Written by Robby Jun 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you struggling to decide whether you should start your workout with cardio or strength training? This is a common question among fitness enthusiasts. Some believe that cardio should be done first, while others swear by starting with strength training. In this article, we will explore this topic in detail and help you determine what you should do first.

Pain Points

Deciding whether to do cardio or strength training first can be a daunting task. There are many factors to consider, such as your fitness goals, time constraints, and personal preferences. Making the wrong choice can impact the effectiveness of your workout and even lead to injury. It is important to make an informed decision based on your individual needs.

Answering the Target

The answer to the question of which one you should do first depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular health or lose weight, then starting with cardio is the way to go. Cardio exercises such as running, cycling, or swimming, get your heart rate up and burn calories effectively. Doing cardio first can also help you warm up your muscles and prepare your body for strength training.

On the other hand, if your goal is to build muscle and increase strength, then starting with strength training is the better option. Strength exercises such as weightlifting, push-ups, or squats, help you build lean muscle mass and boost your metabolic rate. By starting with strength training, you can maximize your energy levels and focus on proper form and technique.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, the decision of whether to do cardio or strength training first depends on your fitness goals. If you want to improve your cardiovascular health or lose weight, start with cardio. If you want to build muscle and increase strength, start with strength training. It is important to select the right exercise based on your goals and preferences to have an effective and safe workout.

Cardio First

Personally, I prefer to start my workout with cardio. I find that doing cardio first helps me warm up my muscles and get my heart rate up. It also helps me clear my mind and get into the right mindset for the rest of my workout. I usually start with a 10-15 minute jog on the treadmill or a quick cycling session before moving on to strength training.

Strength Training First

However, some people prefer to start with strength training. They argue that starting with strength training helps them maximize their energy levels and focus on proper form and technique. By starting with strength training, they can also avoid fatigue that may occur after doing cardio.

Factors to Consider

When deciding what to do first - cardio or strength training, there are several factors to consider. These include your fitness level, time constraints, and personal preferences. If you are a beginner, it may be helpful to start with cardio to build up your endurance and stamina. If you have limited time, you can combine both cardio and strength training in one session. If you have a personal preference or enjoy one type of exercise more, you can start with that and switch it up every once in a while.

Tips for Success

Regardless of whether you choose to start with cardio or strength training, there are some tips for success to keep in mind. First, make sure to warm up properly and stretch before starting your workout. Second, stay hydrated and fuel your body with healthy food and snacks. Third, focus on proper form and technique to avoid injury and maximize your results. And finally, listen to your body and adjust your workout as needed.

Personal Experience

Personally, I have found that starting with cardio helps me get in the right mindset for my workout. I usually start with a quick 10-minute run on the treadmill to warm up my muscles and get my heart rate up. This helps me feel energized and focused for the rest of my workout. However, I know that everyone is different and what works for me may not work for others.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio and strength training in the same session?

A: Yes, you can combine both cardio and strength training in one session. This is known as a hybrid workout and can be an effective way to save time and get the best of both worlds.

Q: How long should I do cardio before starting strength training?

A: This depends on your fitness level and goals. It is recommended to do at least 10-15 minutes of cardio to warm up your muscles and get your heart rate up before starting strength training.

Q: What are the benefits of doing cardio first?

A: Doing cardio first can help you warm up your muscles, get your heart rate up, and prepare your body for strength training. It can also help you clear your mind and get in the right mindset for your workout.

Q: What are the benefits of doing strength training first?

A: Doing strength training first can help you maximize your energy levels, focus on proper form and technique, and avoid fatigue that may occur after doing cardio.

Conclusion of What Should I Do First: Cardio or Strength Training

In conclusion, the decision of whether to do cardio or strength training first depends on your fitness goals, personal preferences, and time constraints. Both cardio and strength training offer numerous health benefits and can be combined for an effective workout. It is important to listen to your body and choose the right exercise based on your needs and preferences to have an effective and safe workout.

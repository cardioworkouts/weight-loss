Full Body Workout Routine For Weight Loss At Gym

Written by Aprilia Sep 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling to lose weight even with regular exercise? One of the most effective ways to achieve weight loss goals is through a full body workout routine at the gym. It is important to target all major muscle groups in order to increase metabolism and burn calories. In this blog post, we will discuss the benefits of a full body workout routine for weight loss at the gym and provide a sample workout plan to get you started.

The struggle to lose weight can be frustrating, especially when you are putting in effort at the gym but not seeing results. This is often due to a lack of variety in your workout routine, or not targeting all muscle groups effectively. A full body workout routine at the gym can help you break through this plateau and see the results you desire.

A full body workout routine for weight loss at the gym involves targeting all major muscle groups in one workout session. This includes exercises for the legs, chest, back, arms, and core. By targeting all muscle groups, you increase your metabolism and burn more calories even after your workout is complete. This type of workout routine is also great for building muscle and improving overall strength.

In summary, a full body workout routine for weight loss at the gym is a great way to achieve your weight loss goals. By targeting all major muscle groups, you increase metabolism and burn more calories even after your workout is complete. It is important to incorporate a variety of exercises and to challenge yourself with heavier weights or more reps as you progress.

Leg Exercises

One of the most important muscle groups to target in a full body workout routine is the legs. Strong legs not only improve overall strength but also help increase metabolism and burn calories. Here is a sample leg workout to incorporate into your routine:

1. Squats - 3 sets of 10 reps

2. Lunges - 3 sets of 10 reps each leg

3. Leg Press - 3 sets of 10 reps

4. Deadlifts - 3 sets of 10 reps

Chest and Back Exercises

The chest and back muscles are also important to target in a full body workout routine. Here is a sample workout to incorporate into your routine:

1. Bench Press - 3 sets of 10 reps

2. Pull-ups - 3 sets of 10 reps

3. Dumbbell Rows - 3 sets of 10 reps each arm

4. Push-ups - 3 sets of 10 reps

Arm Exercises

In addition to the legs, chest, and back, it is important to target the arms in a full body workout routine. Here is a sample arm workout to incorporate into your routine:

1. Bicep Curls - 3 sets of 10 reps

2. Tricep Dips - 3 sets of 10 reps

3. Hammer Curls - 3 sets of 10 reps

4. Overhead Tricep Extension - 3 sets of 10 reps

Core Exercises

The core muscles are essential for overall strength and stability. Here is a sample core workout to incorporate into your routine:

1. Plank - 3 sets of 30 seconds

2. Russian Twists - 3 sets of 10 reps each side

3. Bicycle Crunches - 3 sets of 10 reps each side

4. Leg Raises - 3 sets of 10 reps

Q&A

Q: How often should I do a full body workout routine for weight loss at the gym?

A: It is recommended to do a full body workout routine 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between each session.

Q: Can I still do cardio in addition to a full body workout routine?

A: Yes, cardio is a great way to supplement a full body workout routine and further increase weight loss.

Q: Do I need to use heavy weights in a full body workout routine?

A: It is important to challenge yourself with weights, but you do not need to use heavy weights to see results. Focus on proper form and gradually increase weight as you progress.

Q: Can I modify the exercises in a full body workout routine?

A: Yes, it is important to listen to your body and modify exercises as needed. Consult with a trainer or fitness professional for guidance on modifications.

Conclusion of Full Body Workout Routine for Weight Loss at Gym

A full body workout routine for weight loss at the gym is an effective way to achieve your weight loss goals. By targeting all major muscle groups, you increase metabolism and burn more calories even after your workout is complete. It is important to incorporate a variety of exercises and to challenge yourself with heavier weights or more reps as you progress. Remember to listen to your body and consult with a trainer or fitness professional for guidance and modifications.

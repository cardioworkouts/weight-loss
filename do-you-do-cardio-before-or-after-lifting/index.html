Do You Do Cardio Before Or After Lifting The Ultimate Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who is confused about whether to do cardio before or after lifting? Don't worry; you're not alone. Many people struggle with this question as they aim to achieve their fitness goals. In this guide, we'll explore the benefits and drawbacks of both approaches, so you can decide what works best for you.

Pain Points Related to Do You Do Cardio Before or After Lifting

One of the most significant pain points of this dilemma is the fear of losing muscle mass while doing cardio before lifting. The second pain point is not being able to perform at their best during the lifting session if they do cardio before. Lastly, people worry about not losing enough weight if they do cardio after lifting.

Do You Do Cardio Before or After Lifting?

The answer to this question depends on your fitness goals. If your aim is to build muscle mass or lift heavier weights, you should do your weightlifting session first. Cardio before lifting could tire you out, making it challenging to lift at your best. However, if your primary goal is weight loss or increasing endurance, doing cardio before lifting can boost your metabolism and help you burn more calories.

On the other hand, if you prioritize muscle gain and strength, doing cardio after lifting can be beneficial. Cardio after lifting helps reduce the risk of injury and can also help you burn additional calories as your body continues to burn calories even after your workout.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Lifting

Doing cardio before lifting has several benefits. Firstly, it can help you warm up and mobilize your joints, reducing the risk of injury during the weightlifting session. Secondly, it can boost your metabolism, help you burn more calories, and improve your endurance. Lastly, doing cardio before lifting can help you get a better night's sleep as it helps to reduce stress levels.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio After Lifting

Doing cardio after lifting has its own set of benefits. Firstly, it can help you burn more calories and lose weight faster. Secondly, it reduces the risk of injury during the lifting session as it helps loosen up tight muscles. Lastly, it can help you recover faster as cardio can help flush out lactic acid buildup.

How to Decide Whether to Do Cardio Before or After Lifting?

The decision to do cardio before or after lifting is ultimately yours. However, there are a few things you can consider when making this decision. If your primary goal is to build muscle mass, doing cardio after lifting would be more beneficial. If you're looking to increase endurance or lose weight, doing cardio before lifting can be ideal. Another way to decide is by experimenting with both approaches and seeing what works best for you.

Tips for Combining Cardio and Lifting

If you choose to combine cardio and lifting, here are some tips to help you get the most out of your workout:

  • Start with a good warm-up to prevent injury.
  • Choose the right cardio exercise that complements your lifting routine.
  • Avoid overtraining by limiting the duration and frequency of your workouts.
  • Stay hydrated throughout your workout.
  • Don't forget to stretch after your workout to prevent soreness and stiffness.

Question and Answer

Q: Will I lose muscle mass if I do cardio before lifting?

A: It's unlikely that you'll lose muscle mass if you do cardio before lifting. However, it could affect your lifting performance.

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after lifting if I want to lose weight?

A: If your primary goal is weight loss, doing cardio before lifting can help you burn more calories and lose weight faster.

Q: Can I do cardio and lifting on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do both cardio and lifting on the same day. However, it's essential to ensure you're not overtraining and that you're giving your body adequate rest and recovery time.

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio after lifting?

A: It's best to wait at least 30 minutes after lifting to do cardio. This allows your body to recover from the lifting session and reduce the risk of injury.

Conclusion of Do You Do Cardio Before or After Lifting

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio before or after lifting depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle mass, do your weightlifting session first. If your primary goal is weight loss or increasing endurance, doing cardio before lifting can be beneficial. However, if you prioritize muscle gain and strength, doing cardio after lifting can be more beneficial. Ultimately, the choice is yours, and the best way to decide is to experiment with both approaches and see what works best for you.

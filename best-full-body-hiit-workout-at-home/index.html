How to Do a Full Body HIIT Workout at Home

To do a full body HIIT workout at home, you'll need to choose a variety of exercises that challenge your cardiovascular endurance, strength, and agility. Here are some ideas:

Exercise 1: Burpees

Burpees are a full body exercise that work your legs, core, arms, and shoulders. Here's how to do them:

  1. Start in a standing position with your feet shoulder-width apart.
  2. Lower your body into a squat position and place your hands on the floor in front of you.
  3. Jump your feet back into a plank position.
  4. Do a push-up.
  5. Jump your feet back to the squat position.
  6. Stand up and jump as high as you can.

Tips:

Start with 10 reps and work your way up to 20 or more. To modify the exercise, you can step your feet back instead of jumping, or skip the push-up.

Exercise 2: Mountain Climbers

Mountain climbers are a great cardio exercise that work your legs, core, and arms. Here's how to do them:

  1. Start in a plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart.
  2. Bring your right knee towards your chest and then quickly switch to bring your left knee towards your chest.
  3. Continue to switch legs quickly, as if you are running in place.

Tips:

Start with 30 seconds and work your way up to 1 minute or more. To modify the exercise, you can slow down the pace or do the exercise from a kneeling position.

FAQs

Q: How often should I do a full body HIIT workout at home?

A: It's recommended to do a full body HIIT workout 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between.

Q: Can I do a full body HIIT workout at home if I'm a beginner?

A: Yes, you can modify the exercises to your fitness level and gradually work your way up.

Q: Do I need any equipment to do a full body HIIT workout at home?

A: No, you don't need any equipment. However, you can use dumbbells or resistance bands to make the exercises more challenging.

Q: How long should a full body HIIT workout at home be?

A: A full body HIIT workout at home can be as short as 20 minutes or as long as 45 minutes, depending on your fitness level and goals.

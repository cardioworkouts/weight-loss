Can I Do Cardio First Then Weights A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 02, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you confused about whether to do cardio before or after weight training? Do you want to know the benefits and drawbacks of both methods? If yes, then you have come to the right place. This post will address the common question, "Can I do cardio first then weights?" and provide you with all the information you need to make an informed decision.

Pain Points Related to Can I Do Cardio First Then Weights

Many people are unsure about whether to do cardio before or after weight training. This confusion stems from the fact that both methods have their advantages and disadvantages. Additionally, different fitness experts have different opinions, which only adds to the confusion. As a result, people often end up choosing the wrong method, which can negatively impact their fitness goals.

Answering the Target of Can I Do Cardio First Then Weights

The short answer is, "It depends on your fitness goals." If your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular endurance, then doing cardio first might be the best option. On the other hand, if your primary goal is to build muscle or strength, then weight training should come first. However, there are exceptions to this rule, which we will discuss in detail below.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, the answer to the question, "Can I do cardio first then weights?" depends on your fitness goals. If you want to improve cardiovascular endurance, then do cardio first. If you want to build muscle or strength, then do weight training first. However, there are exceptions to this rule, which we will discuss in detail in the following sections.

Can I Do Cardio First Then Weights for Weight Loss?

If your primary goal is weight loss, then you should do both cardio and weight training. However, the order in which you do them does not matter as much. Both cardio and weight training burn calories, but weight training also helps build muscle, which increases your metabolic rate. Therefore, doing both types of exercise can help you lose weight and improve your body composition.

Can I Do Cardio First Then Weights for Endurance?

If your primary goal is to improve cardiovascular endurance, then you should do cardio first. This is because weight training can fatigue your muscles, which can negatively impact your endurance performance. Additionally, cardio can help warm up your body and prepare it for weight training.

The Exception: High-Intensity Interval Training

If you want to improve both cardiovascular endurance and build muscle, then high-intensity interval training (HIIT) might be the best option. HIIT involves alternating between short bursts of high-intensity exercise (e.g., sprints) and periods of rest. This type of training can improve cardiovascular endurance and build muscle simultaneously. However, it's important to note that HIIT is very intense and should only be done by people who are already in good physical condition.

How to Do Cardio and Weight Training on the Same Day?

If you want to do both cardio and weight training on the same day, there are two ways to do it. The first method is to do them in separate sessions, with a few hours of rest in between. For example, you could do cardio in the morning and weight training in the evening. The second method is to do them in the same session, but with weight training first and cardio second. This method can help you burn more fat during cardio, as your body will already be in a fat-burning state after weight training.

FAQs about Can I Do Cardio First Then Weights

Q1. Is it bad to do cardio before lifting?

A1. It's not necessarily bad to do cardio before lifting, but it can negatively impact your weight training performance. This is because cardio can fatigue your muscles, making it harder for you to lift heavy weights.

Q2. Is it better to do cardio or weights first?

A2. It depends on your fitness goals. If you want to improve cardiovascular endurance, then do cardio first. If you want to build muscle or strength, then do weight training first.

Q3. How long should I wait to lift weights after cardio?

A3. It's best to wait at least 30 minutes to lift weights after cardio. This will give your body enough time to recover and avoid muscle fatigue.

Q4. Can I do cardio and weights on the same day?

A4. Yes, you can do cardio and weight training on the same day. However, it's best to do them in separate sessions or do weight training first and cardio second.

Conclusion of Can I Do Cardio First Then Weights

In conclusion, the answer to the question, "Can I do cardio first then weights?" depends on your fitness goals. If you want to improve cardiovascular endurance, then do cardio first. If you want to build muscle or strength, then do weight training first. However, if you want to improve both endurance and muscle, then HIIT might be the best option. Additionally, if you want to lose weight, then you should do both cardio and weight training. Regardless of the order in which you do them, it's important to listen to your body and avoid overtraining.

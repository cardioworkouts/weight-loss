Beginner s Guide To Weight Training Lifting Straps

Written by Aprilia Sep 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your grip strength limiting your weightlifting progress? Do you struggle to hold on to heavy weights during deadlifts or pull-ups? If so, weight training lifting straps may be the solution you've been looking for.

The Pain Points of Weight Training Lifting Straps

Weightlifting can be a challenging sport, requiring strength, endurance, and proper form. One common issue that weightlifters face is grip strength. When attempting to lift heavy weights, it's not uncommon for the weightlifter's grip to give out before their muscles do. This can be frustrating, especially if you're trying to increase your weightlifting PRs or compete in a powerlifting competition.

What are Weight Training Lifting Straps?

Weight training lifting straps are a piece of equipment designed to help weightlifters improve their grip strength. They're typically made of durable fabric or leather and wrap around the weightlifter's wrists and the barbell or dumbbell they're lifting. The straps essentially act as an extension of the weightlifter's grip, allowing them to hold onto heavy weights for longer periods of time.

Weight training lifting straps come in a variety of styles and materials, so it's important to choose the right type for your needs. Some straps are designed specifically for deadlifts, while others are more versatile and can be used for a variety of exercises.

The Benefits of Weight Training Lifting Straps

Using weight training lifting straps can provide a number of benefits for weightlifters. Here are some of the main advantages:

  • Improved grip strength
  • Ability to lift heavier weights
  • Reduced risk of injury due to grip failure
  • Increased muscle activation

How to Use Weight Training Lifting Straps

Using weight training lifting straps is relatively simple, but it's important to use them correctly to avoid injury. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wrap the strap around your wrist, making sure it's snug but not too tight.
  2. Wrap the other end of the strap around the barbell or dumbbell you're lifting, making sure it's secure.
  3. Grasp the barbell or dumbbell with your hand, making sure your grip is secure.
  4. Tighten the strap by pulling on the loose end, making sure it's tight enough to provide support but not so tight that it cuts off circulation.
  5. Perform your lift as usual, focusing on maintaining proper form and breathing.

Weight Training Lifting Straps vs. Gloves

While weight training gloves are another common piece of equipment designed to improve grip strength, they're not always the best option. Gloves can be hot and uncomfortable, and they don't provide as much support as lifting straps. Additionally, some weightlifters find that gloves actually reduce their grip strength by making the barbell or dumbbell harder to hold onto.

Question and Answer

Q: How do I know what type of weight training lifting straps to buy?

A: The type of weight training lifting straps you should buy depends on the exercises you plan to use them for. If you primarily do deadlifts, you'll want to look for straps designed specifically for deadlifts. If you want a more versatile option, look for lifting straps that can be used for a variety of exercises.

Q: Can using weight training lifting straps lead to a decrease in grip strength?

A: There is some concern that using weight training lifting straps too often can lead to a decrease in grip strength over time. However, as long as you're still incorporating grip-strengthening exercises into your routine, using lifting straps in moderation shouldn't have a negative impact on your grip strength.

Q: Are weight training lifting straps only for advanced weightlifters?

A: No, weight training lifting straps can be helpful for weightlifters of all levels. However, it's important to make sure you're using proper form and not relying too heavily on the straps for support.

Q: How often should I use weight training lifting straps?

A: This depends on your individual needs and goals. If grip strength is a major limiting factor for you, you may want to use lifting straps more frequently. However, if you're primarily looking to use them for heavier lifts, you may only need to use them for one or two sets per workout.

Conclusion of Weight Training Lifting Straps

Weight training lifting straps can be a valuable tool for weightlifters looking to improve their grip strength and lift heavier weights. By choosing the right type of straps and using them correctly, you can enjoy the benefits of increased muscle activation and reduced risk of injury due to grip failure. Remember to use lifting straps in moderation and incorporate grip-strengthening exercises into your routine to maintain overall grip strength.

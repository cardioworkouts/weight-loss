What Is Considered Interval Training

Written by Daniel Aug 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you want to improve your fitness level and burn more calories in a shorter amount of time? Are you tired of spending hours at the gym without seeing any real results? If so, interval training might be the solution you've been searching for.

Interval training is a type of workout that alternates between high-intensity exercise and periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. This type of training is designed to challenge your body and push it to its limits, which can lead to significant improvements in your overall fitness level.

The goal of interval training is to increase your heart rate and keep it elevated for a set period of time, which can help you burn more calories and fat than traditional steady-state cardio exercises. This type of training can be done with nearly any type of exercise, including running, cycling, swimming, and weightlifting.

In this article, we'll dive deeper into what is considered interval training and related keywords, its benefits, and how to get started with this type of workout.

What is Considered Interval Training?

Interval training is a type of workout that involves alternating periods of high-intensity exercise with periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. This type of training can help you burn more calories and fat in a shorter amount of time than traditional steady-state cardio exercises.

During high-intensity intervals, you push yourself to work at or near your maximum capacity. This can be done with exercises such as sprints, high knees, or burpees. During low-intensity intervals, you allow your heart rate to come down before starting the next high-intensity interval.

Interval training can be done with nearly any type of exercise, including running, cycling, swimming, and weightlifting. The key is to find exercises that you enjoy and can perform at a high intensity.

The Benefits of Interval Training

Interval training has numerous benefits for your health and fitness level. Here are just a few:

1. Increased Calorie Burn

Interval training can help you burn more calories and fat than traditional steady-state cardio exercises. This is because the high-intensity intervals force your body to work harder and faster, which can lead to a higher calorie burn.

2. Improved Cardiovascular Health

Interval training can also improve your cardiovascular health by increasing your heart rate and improving your heart function. This can lead to a stronger, healthier heart and a reduced risk of heart disease.

3. Time-Saving

Interval training is a time-saving workout because you can burn more calories and fat in a shorter amount of time than traditional steady-state cardio exercises. This makes it a great option for people who are short on time but want to see real results.

4. Increased Metabolism

Interval training can also increase your metabolism, which can help you burn more calories throughout the day. This is because high-intensity exercise can cause your body to continue burning calories even after your workout is over.

Getting Started with Interval Training

Before you start interval training, it's important to warm up properly to prevent injury. This can be done with a few minutes of low-intensity exercise or dynamic stretching.

Once you're warmed up, you can start your interval workout by performing a high-intensity exercise for a set amount of time, followed by a period of rest or low-intensity exercise. You can repeat this cycle several times, depending on the length of your workout.

Remember to cool down properly after your workout to prevent injury and help your body recover.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do interval training?

A: It's recommended to do interval training 2-3 times per week, with at least one rest day in between workouts.

Q: Can anyone do interval training?

A: Interval training can be done by anyone, but it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts to prevent injury.

Q: How long should each high-intensity interval last?

A: High-intensity intervals can last anywhere from 20-60 seconds, depending on your fitness level and the type of exercise you're doing.

Q: What are some examples of high-intensity exercises?

A: Some examples of high-intensity exercises include sprints, burpees, jumping jacks, and mountain climbers.

Conclusion of What is Considered Interval Training

Interval training is a highly effective workout that can help you burn more calories and fat in a shorter amount of time than traditional steady-state cardio exercises. By alternating between periods of high-intensity exercise and rest or low-intensity exercise, you can challenge your body and see real improvements in your fitness level.

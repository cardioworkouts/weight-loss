What Can I Eat Before A Workout In The Morning

Written by Christine Jul 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who regularly works out in the morning but struggles to figure out what to eat before heading to the gym? Do you find it hard to stay energized during your workout or feel nauseous because of the food you ate? If so, then you are not alone!

Many people face this dilemma of what to eat before a morning workout. Eating the wrong thing can lead to digestive issues, cramping, and an overall lack of energy during your workout. The key is to find the right balance between consuming enough calories to fuel your workout but not eating too much that it sits in your stomach.

So, what can you eat before a workout in the morning? The answer lies in the type of exercise you'll be doing and your personal preferences.

Some people prefer to work out on an empty stomach, while others need to eat something to have enough energy to power through their routine. The goal is to focus on food that can provide you with energy and nutrients that your body needs without weighing you down.

My Personal Experience

As someone who regularly exercises in the morning, I found that I perform my best when I eat a small snack before heading to the gym. My go-to snack is a banana with a tablespoon of peanut butter. The banana provides me with the necessary carbs to fuel my workout, while the peanut butter provides me with some protein and healthy fats to keep me full and energized.

What Can You Eat Before a Cardio Workout?

If you are planning to do cardio in the morning, then it's important to consume food that can help you maintain your energy levels during your workout. Some good options include:

  • A small banana or apple
  • Whole-grain toast with nut butter
  • A smoothie with fruit and protein powder

What Can You Eat Before a Strength Training Workout?

For strength training workouts, it's important to focus on food that can provide you with the necessary energy to lift weights and build muscle. Some good options include:

  • A protein shake with a banana
  • Greek yogurt with fruit and granola
  • Whole-grain toast with avocado and an egg

Why Hydration is Also Important

While it's important to focus on what you eat before a workout, it's equally important to stay hydrated. Drinking enough water before and during your workout can help you maintain your energy levels and prevent cramping. Aim to drink at least 8 ounces of water before your workout and continue to sip on water during your routine.

How to Time Your Meals

Timing your meals is also crucial when it comes to working out in the morning. You want to make sure that you give your body enough time to digest your food before hitting the gym. Aim to eat your pre-workout meal at least 30 minutes to an hour before your workout to allow for proper digestion.

Conclusion

Choosing the right food to eat before a morning workout can be challenging, but it's essential to ensure that you have the necessary energy to complete your routine. Focus on food that can provide you with the right balance of carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats without weighing you down. Remember to stay hydrated, time your meals appropriately, and listen to your body to determine what works best for you.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I work out on an empty stomach?

A: Yes, some people prefer to work out on an empty stomach. However, it's important to listen to your body and make sure that you have enough energy to complete your routine.

Q: Is it okay to eat a heavy meal before a morning workout?

A: No, it's not recommended to eat a heavy meal before a morning workout. Heavy meals take longer to digest and can lead to digestive issues and discomfort during your workout.

Q: What should I drink before a morning workout?

A: Water is the best choice to drink before and during your workout. However, if you need an extra boost of energy, you can opt for a pre-workout drink that contains caffeine.

Q: How long should I wait to work out after eating?

A: It's recommended to wait at least 30 minutes to an hour after eating before working out to allow for proper digestion.

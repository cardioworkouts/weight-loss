The Best Music For Hiit Workout Pump Up Your Exercise With The Right Tunes

Written by Aprilia Jul 10, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of feeling uninspired during your HIIT workouts? Do you struggle to stay motivated and push through the pain? One solution might be as simple as finding the right music to listen to. Music has been proven to enhance exercise performance and make workouts more enjoyable. In this article, we'll explore the best music for HIIT workout and how it can help you get the most out of your exercise routine.

High-intensity interval training, or HIIT, can be a challenging exercise method that requires a lot of energy and focus. It involves short bursts of intense activity followed by brief periods of rest or lower-intensity exercise. While the benefits of HIIT are well-known, it can also be difficult to maintain the necessary level of intensity without burning out. That's where music comes in.

Research has shown that listening to music during exercise can improve performance, increase endurance, and reduce perceived effort. When you listen to music that you enjoy, your brain releases dopamine, a neurotransmitter that can improve your mood and motivation. The right music can also help you maintain a steady pace, distract you from discomfort, and even synchronize your movements with the beat.

So what is the best music for HIIT workout? The answer might vary depending on your personal taste, but there are some general guidelines that can help you choose the right tunes. In general, you'll want to look for music that is fast-paced, upbeat, and motivating. The tempo should match the intensity of your workout, with higher tempo songs for more intense intervals and lower tempo songs for rest periods.

The Power of Pop

One genre of music that is particularly well-suited for HIIT workouts is pop music. Pop songs are often high-energy, with catchy hooks and driving beats that can keep you moving. Many pop songs also have a tempo that falls within the ideal range for HIIT, around 120-140 beats per minute. Some popular pop songs to add to your workout playlist include "Can't Stop the Feeling" by Justin Timberlake, "Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson ft. Bruno Mars, and "Shake It Off" by Taylor Swift.

Personally, I love to listen to pop music during my HIIT workouts. It helps me get into the zone and stay motivated, even when I'm feeling tired or sore. I find that the catchy melodies and upbeat rhythms keep me focused and energized throughout the entire workout. Plus, singing along to my favorite pop songs makes the time fly by!

The Strength of EDM

If pop music isn't your thing, another genre that works well for HIIT is electronic dance music, or EDM. This type of music is known for its fast tempos, driving beats, and pulsing rhythms that can help you maintain a steady pace and push through the burn. Many EDM songs have tempos above 140 beats per minute, making them perfect for high-intensity intervals. Some popular EDM songs to add to your workout playlist include "Levels" by Avicii, "Animals" by Martin Garrix, and "Titanium" by David Guetta ft. Sia.

Personally, I find that EDM music is great for really pushing myself during my HIIT workouts. The high-energy beats and intense drops help me power through each interval and give it my all. The repetitive rhythms and driving bass also help me maintain a consistent pace and avoid slowing down or taking unnecessary breaks.

Other Genres to Consider

Of course, there are many other types of music that can work well for HIIT workouts, depending on your personal preferences. Some people prefer to listen to rap or hip-hop for their workouts, while others enjoy rock or heavy metal. The key is to find music that you enjoy and that motivates you to push yourself. You can experiment with different genres and tempos to see what works best for you.

Tips for Creating the Perfect Playlist

Now that you know some of the best music genres for HIIT workouts, how do you go about creating the perfect playlist? Here are some tips:

1. Choose a variety of songs that you love and that fit the tempo and intensity of your workout.

2. Mix up the genres to keep things interesting and prevent boredom.

3. Start with slower songs for your warm-up and cool-down periods, then gradually increase the tempo for your high-intensity intervals.

4. Pay attention to the lyrics and choose songs that have positive, empowering messages.

Question and Answer

Q: Do I have to listen to music during my HIIT workout to see benefits?

A: No, you don't have to listen to music to get the benefits of HIIT. However, many people find that music helps them stay motivated and focused during their workouts.

Q: Can I listen to any type of music during my HIIT workout?

A: You can listen to any type of music that motivates you and fits the tempo and intensity of your workout. However, some genres, such as pop and EDM, are particularly well-suited for HIIT.

Q: How loud should I play my music during my HIIT workout?

A: It's important to be mindful of your surroundings and not disturb others, especially if you're working out in a public space. However, you should play your music loud enough to hear it over your breathing and any equipment noise.

Q: Should I invest in good quality headphones for my HIIT workout?

A: Good quality headphones can enhance your listening experience and help you stay in the zone during your workout. However, they're not essential. You can still enjoy the benefits of music with any type of headphones or speakers.

Conclusion of Best Music for HIIT Workout

Choosing the right music for your HIIT workout can make a big difference in your performance and enjoyment. Whether you prefer pop, EDM, or another genre, look for music that is fast-paced, upbeat, and motivating. Experiment with different tempos and genres to find what works best for you, and create a playlist that will keep you energized and focused throughout your entire workout. With the right music, you can take your HIIT workouts to the next level.

How To Make A Resume In 2 Pages A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you struggling to fit your entire work history on one-page resume? Or maybe you're worried that a two-page resume will be too long and won't get read? Finding the right balance can be challenging, but it's not impossible. In this post, we'll show you how to make a resume in 2 pages, so you can showcase your skills and qualifications without overwhelming the reader.

When it comes to creating a two-page resume, the biggest challenge is deciding what to include and what to leave out. It's important to remember that your resume is not an autobiography, it's a marketing tool. Your goal is to highlight your most relevant experience and skills, while also making it easy for the reader to navigate your document.

So, how do you make a resume in 2 pages? Follow these simple steps:

Step 1: Choose the Right Format

Before you start writing, it's important to choose the right format for your resume. There are three main types of resume formats: chronological, functional, and combination. For a two-page resume, a combination format is usually the best choice. This format allows you to highlight your skills and achievements, while also providing a clear work history.

Step 2: Prioritize Your Information

Once you've chosen your format, it's time to prioritize your information. Start by making a list of all your work history, education, skills, and achievements. Then, take a look at the job description you're applying for and highlight the key skills and qualifications. Use this information to guide your decision-making process.

Step 3: Use Bullet Points and White Space

To make your two-page resume easy to navigate, use bullet points and plenty of white space. Use bullet points to highlight your achievements and skills, and break up long paragraphs into shorter, easy-to-read sections. Make sure there is enough white space between sections, so the reader can quickly scan your resume.

Step 4: Tailor Your Resume to the Job Description

Finally, it's important to tailor your resume to the job description you're applying for. Use the keywords and phrases from the job description throughout your resume, and highlight your most relevant experience and skills. This will show the reader that you're a good fit for the job.

Personal Experience: My Two-Page Resume Journey

When I first started my job search, I was convinced that a one-page resume was the only way to go. But as I started applying for jobs, I realized that I was leaving out important information. That's when I decided to create a two-page resume.

At first, I was worried that my resume would be too long and overwhelming. But I quickly realized that by using bullet points and white space, I could make my resume easy to read and navigate. I also made sure to tailor my resume to each job description, using the keywords and phrases from the job posting.

FAQs About Making a Two-Page Resume

Q: Should I include all my work history on my two-page resume?

A: No, it's not necessary to include your entire work history on your resume. Focus on your most relevant experience and skills, and make sure to highlight achievements that demonstrate your value.

Q: What font size should I use for my two-page resume?

A: It's best to use a font size between 10 and 12 points for your resume. This will make your resume easy to read, without making it too small.

Q: Can I include a photo on my two-page resume?

A: It's generally not recommended to include a photo on your resume, as it can be seen as unprofessional. However, if you're applying for a job in a creative field, a photo may be appropriate.

Q: How do I make sure my two-page resume is easy to read?

A: To make your resume easy to read, use bullet points, plenty of white space, and a clear, easy-to-read font. Use headings and subheadings to break up your resume into sections, and make sure there is enough white space between each section.

Conclusion

Making a two-page resume can be a challenge, but by following these simple steps, you can create a document that showcases your skills and qualifications without overwhelming the reader. Remember to prioritize your information, use bullet points and white space, tailor your resume to the job description, and use a clear, easy-to-read font. Good luck!

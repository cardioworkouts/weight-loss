Maximizing Your Workout Doing Cardio And Lifting Same Day

Written by Daniel Jul 16, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you struggling to fit both cardio and lifting into your busy schedule? Do you worry about the effectiveness of doing both on the same day? You're not alone. Many people wonder if doing cardio and lifting same day is beneficial or harmful to their fitness goals. In this article, we’ll explore the benefits and drawbacks of combining cardio and lifting, and provide tips to help you maximize your workout.

Whether you are a beginner or an experienced athlete, the thought of doing cardio and lifting on the same day can be daunting. One of the biggest concerns is whether it is safe and effective to do both in one session. Others worry about whether doing cardio will interfere with their ability to lift heavy weights.

The truth is, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to whether you should do cardio and lifting same day. It largely depends on your fitness goals and individual preferences. However, there are some general guidelines that can help you decide if combining both workouts is right for you.

First, it's important to understand the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio and lifting same day. On the one hand, combining both workouts can save time and increase the efficiency of your workout. On the other hand, some experts argue that doing cardio can interfere with your ability to lift heavy weights and vice versa. Additionally, too much cardio can lead to muscle loss, which is counterproductive if you are trying to build muscle.

The Benefits of Combining Cardio and Lifting

Personally, I have found that combining cardio and lifting same day has been beneficial for my fitness goals. By doing both in one session, I have been able to save time and increase the efficiency of my workout. Additionally, doing cardio before lifting has helped me warm up and prepare my muscles for heavier lifts.

Furthermore, combining cardio and lifting can help you burn more calories and lose weight faster. By alternating between cardio and lifting, you can keep your heart rate up and increase your metabolism, which can lead to faster weight loss.

The Drawbacks of Combining Cardio and Lifting

While there are many benefits to doing cardio and lifting same day, it is important to be aware of the drawbacks as well. One of the biggest concerns is that doing cardio can interfere with your ability to lift heavy weights. This is especially true if you do too much cardio or do it immediately before lifting.

Additionally, too much cardio can lead to muscle loss, which is counterproductive if you are trying to build muscle. This is because cardio can break down muscle tissue, which can make it harder to build and maintain muscle mass.

How to Maximize Your Workout with Cardio and Lifting

If you decide to combine cardio and lifting same day, it's important to do it in a way that maximizes the benefits and minimizes the drawbacks. Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your workout:

1. Do cardio after lifting

If you want to lift heavy weights, it's best to do it before cardio. This is because lifting requires more energy and strength than cardio, so doing it first can help you maximize your lifting performance. However, if your primary goal is weight loss, it's better to do cardio first to burn more calories.

2. Don't do too much cardio

While cardio can be beneficial, doing too much of it can lead to muscle loss and interfere with your ability to build muscle. Aim for 20-30 minutes of cardio per session, and try to alternate between high-intensity interval training (HIIT) and steady-state cardio.

3. Eat enough protein

Protein is essential for building and maintaining muscle mass. If you are doing cardio and lifting same day, make sure you are eating enough protein to support your muscle growth. Aim for at least 1 gram of protein per pound of body weight per day.

4. Listen to your body

Everyone is different, and what works for one person may not work for another. Pay attention to how your body responds to doing cardio and lifting same day, and adjust your workout accordingly. If you feel fatigued or notice a decrease in performance, it may be a sign that you need to reduce your cardio or take a break from lifting.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it safe to do cardio and lifting same day?

A: Yes, it is safe to do cardio and lifting same day as long as you do it in a way that maximizes the benefits and minimizes the drawbacks.

Q: Will doing cardio interfere with my ability to lift heavy weights?

A: It can if you do too much cardio or do it immediately before lifting. If your primary goal is to build muscle, it's best to do lifting first and cardio second.

Q: How much cardio should I do if I am combining it with lifting?

A: Aim for 20-30 minutes of cardio per session, and try to alternate between high-intensity interval training (HIIT) and steady-state cardio.

Q: Can doing cardio and lifting same day help me lose weight faster?

A: Yes, combining cardio and lifting can help you burn more calories and lose weight faster. By alternating between cardio and lifting, you can keep your heart rate up and increase your metabolism.

Conclusion of Doing Cardio and Lifting Same Day

Doing cardio and lifting same day can be a great way to save time and increase the efficiency of your workout. However, it's important to do it in a way that maximizes the benefits and minimizes the drawbacks. By following the tips in this article, you can safely and effectively combine cardio and lifting to achieve your fitness goals.

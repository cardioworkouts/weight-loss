Why You Should Add Cardio Exercise After Weight Training

Written by Daniel Jun 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you one of those people who finish their weight training and immediately head out of the gym? If yes, then you might be missing out on a crucial part of your exercise routine- cardio exercise after weight training.

Many people tend to skip cardio exercise after weight training because they feel exhausted after lifting weights or because they believe that it will lead to muscle loss. However, not doing cardio exercise after weight training can cause several issues such as decreased endurance, slow recovery, and increased risk of injury.

The target of cardio exercise after weight training is to improve your heart health, endurance, and metabolism. It helps to increase blood flow to your muscles, which speeds up the recovery process and reduces the risk of muscle soreness. Additionally, it helps to burn calories, reduce body fat, and improve overall fitness.

In summary, cardio exercise after weight training is a crucial part of your fitness routine. It helps you to improve your heart health, endurance, and metabolism. It also speeds up the recovery process and reduces the risk of muscle soreness. Personally, I used to skip cardio exercise after weight training because I felt exhausted after lifting weights. However, I noticed that skipping cardio exercise after weight training affected my endurance and slowed down my recovery. So, I started adding 20-30 minutes of cardio exercise after weight training, and I noticed a significant improvement in my overall fitness.

The Best Cardio Exercise After Weight Training

The best cardio exercise after weight training is low to moderate intensity cardio such as walking, cycling, or using the elliptical machine. High-intensity cardio such as sprinting or HIIT can interfere with your body's recovery process and increase the risk of injury.

Personally, I prefer using the elliptical machine as it provides a low-impact workout and targets several muscle groups. I also find it easier to adjust the intensity and resistance according to my fitness level.

The Benefits of Low to Moderate Intensity Cardio

Low to moderate intensity cardio provides several benefits such as:

  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Boosts metabolism
  • Burns fat and calories
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Improves overall fitness

How to Add Cardio Exercise After Weight Training

To add cardio exercise after weight training, you can follow these simple steps:

  1. Start with low to moderate intensity cardio for 20-30 minutes
  2. Gradually increase the duration and intensity according to your fitness level
  3. Choose a cardio exercise that you enjoy and that targets several muscle groups
  4. Stretch after cardio exercise to reduce the risk of injury and muscle soreness

The Bottom Line

Cardio exercise after weight training is a crucial part of your fitness routine. It helps you to improve your heart health, endurance, and metabolism. It also speeds up the recovery process and reduces the risk of muscle soreness. So, make sure to add cardio exercise after weight training and enjoy the benefits of a complete workout!

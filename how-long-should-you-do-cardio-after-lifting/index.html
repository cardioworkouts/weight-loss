How Long Should You Do Cardio After Lifting

Written by Frank Oct 17, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you someone who likes to lift weights and do cardio but unsure of how long you should do each exercise? If so, you're not alone. Many fitness enthusiasts struggle with the same question. In this blog post, we'll discuss how long you should do cardio after lifting and related keywords to help you optimize your workout routine.

What is the Pain Point Related to How Long Should You Do Cardio After Lifting?

The pain point related to how long you should do cardio after lifting is that many people are unsure of the best way to structure their workouts. They may not know how much time to allocate to each exercise, causing them to either cut their cardio short or neglect their weightlifting routine. This can lead to frustration and a lack of progress in achieving their fitness goals.

How Long Should You Do Cardio After Lifting?

It's generally recommended to do cardio for at least 20-30 minutes after lifting weights. This is because weightlifting primarily uses anaerobic energy, while cardio uses aerobic energy. Doing cardio after lifting can help you burn extra calories, improve cardiovascular health, and aid in recovery. However, it's important to listen to your body and not overdo it. If you're feeling fatigued or experiencing muscle soreness, it's okay to cut your cardio session short or skip it altogether.

Additionally, if your goal is to build muscle, you may want to limit your cardio to a shorter duration or a lower intensity. This is because excessive cardio can interfere with muscle growth by depleting glycogen stores and increasing cortisol levels.

Summary

In summary, doing cardio for 20-30 minutes after lifting weights is a good rule of thumb to follow. However, it's important to listen to your body and adjust accordingly. If your goal is to build muscle, limit your cardio to a shorter duration or lower intensity.

Personal Experience with How Long Should You Do Cardio After Lifting

As someone who enjoys weightlifting and cardio, I've experimented with different workout routines to find what works best for me. I've found that doing cardio for 30 minutes after lifting helps me burn extra calories and improve my overall fitness level. However, on days when I'm feeling particularly fatigued, I'll cut my cardio session short or skip it altogether to avoid overtraining.

Benefits of Doing Cardio After Lifting

There are several benefits to doing cardio after lifting weights. One of the main benefits is that it can help you burn extra calories and lose weight. It also improves cardiovascular health by strengthening the heart and lungs. Additionally, doing cardio after lifting can aid in recovery by increasing blood flow and reducing muscle soreness.

Tips for Optimizing Your Cardio After Lifting

If you want to optimize your cardio after lifting, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose an activity you enjoy, such as running, cycling, or swimming
  • Gradually increase the duration and intensity of your cardio sessions
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water before, during, and after your workout
  • Stretch before and after your workout to reduce the risk of injury

How to Incorporate Cardio into Your Weightlifting Routine

If you're unsure of how to incorporate cardio into your weightlifting routine, here are a few ideas:

  • Do a quick warm-up on the treadmill or stationary bike before lifting weights
  • Alternate between weightlifting and cardio intervals, such as 5 minutes of lifting followed by 5 minutes of running
  • Finish your workout with a cardio session to burn extra calories and aid in recovery

Personal Experience with Cardio After Lifting

Personally, I like to finish my weightlifting sessions with a 20-30 minute cardio session on the elliptical or treadmill. This helps me burn extra calories and improve my cardiovascular health. I also find that it helps me recover faster between workouts.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after lifting weights?

A: It's generally recommended to do weightlifting before cardio. This is because weightlifting primarily uses anaerobic energy, while cardio uses aerobic energy. Doing cardio before lifting can deplete your glycogen stores and decrease your strength and endurance during weightlifting.

Q: How many times a week should I do cardio after lifting?

A: It depends on your fitness goals and schedule. If your goal is to lose weight, you may want to do cardio after every weightlifting session. If your goal is to build muscle, you may want to limit your cardio to 1-2 sessions per week.

Q: Can I do cardio on my rest days?

A: Yes, you can do cardio on your rest days. However, it's important to listen to your body and not overdo it. If you're feeling fatigued or experiencing muscle soreness, it's okay to take a break and rest.

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio after lifting?

A: It's generally recommended to wait at least 10-15 minutes after lifting weights before doing cardio. This allows your body to recover and reduce the risk of injury.

Conclusion of How Long Should You Do Cardio After Lifting

Knowing how long you should do cardio after lifting can help you optimize your workout routine and achieve your fitness goals. By following the recommendations outlined in this blog post, you can ensure that you're getting the most out of your cardio and weightlifting sessions.

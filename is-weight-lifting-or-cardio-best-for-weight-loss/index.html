Is Weight Lifting Or Cardio Best For Weight Loss

Written by Aprilia Oct 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling to lose weight and wondering which exercise is best for your weight loss journey? This article will guide you through the benefits and drawbacks of weight lifting and cardio for weight loss.

Many people find it challenging to lose weight, and the weight loss industry is filled with various diets, supplements, and exercise programs. However, the question remains, is weight lifting or cardio best for weight loss?

The answer is both. Both weight lifting and cardio can help you lose weight, but the approach and results can vary.

Weight lifting can help you build lean muscle mass, which increases your metabolism and burns calories even when you're at rest. Cardio, on the other hand, helps you burn calories during the workout and improves your cardiovascular health.

My Personal Experience with Weight Lifting and Cardio

When I started my weight loss journey, I focused on cardio and lost some weight. However, I noticed that my body wasn't toned and firm. I started incorporating weight lifting into my routine, and I noticed significant changes in my body composition. I became leaner, stronger, and more toned.

Weight lifting can be intimidating, especially if you're a beginner. However, it's essential to start small and focus on proper form to prevent injury. I started with bodyweight exercises and gradually increased the weight as I became stronger.

The Benefits of Weight Lifting for Weight Loss

Weight lifting has several benefits for weight loss, including:

Increased Metabolism

Weight lifting helps you build lean muscle mass, which increases your metabolism and burns calories even when you're at rest. The more muscle you have, the more calories you burn.

Improved Body Composition

Weight lifting helps you build lean muscle mass and reduce body fat, resulting in a toned and firm body.

Increased Strength and Endurance

Weight lifting improves your strength and endurance, making daily tasks easier and more comfortable.

Tips for Weight Lifting

If you're new to weight lifting, here are some tips to get started:

  • Start with bodyweight exercises and gradually increase the weight as you become stronger.
  • Focus on proper form to prevent injury.
  • Include compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups, such as squats, deadlifts, and bench press.
  • Rest for at least 48 hours between weight lifting sessions to allow your muscles to recover.

The Benefits of Cardio for Weight Loss

Cardio has several benefits for weight loss, including:

Increased Calorie Burn

Cardio burns calories, which can help you create a calorie deficit and lose weight.

Improved Cardiovascular Health

Cardio improves your cardiovascular health, reducing the risk of heart disease, high blood pressure, and stroke.

Improved Mood

Cardio releases endorphins, which can improve your mood and reduce stress.

Tips for Cardio

If you're new to cardio, here are some tips to get started:

  • Start with low-impact exercises, such as walking, cycling, or swimming.
  • Gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workouts.
  • Include a variety of cardio exercises to prevent boredom and challenge your body.
  • Listen to your body and rest when needed.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do weight lifting or cardio?

A: It depends on your fitness goals and schedule. Ideally, you should aim for at least three weight lifting sessions and three cardio sessions per week.

Q: Can I do weight lifting and cardio on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do weight lifting and cardio on the same day. However, it's essential to prioritize your goals and not overtrain.

Q: Which is better for weight loss, high-intensity interval training (HIIT) or steady-state cardio?

A: Both HIIT and steady-state cardio can help you lose weight. However, HIIT may be more effective in burning calories and fat in a shorter amount of time.

Q: Do I need to lift heavy weights to see results?

A: No, you don't need to lift heavy weights to see results. You can achieve significant results with bodyweight exercises or lighter weights with higher reps.

Conclusion of Is Weight Lifting or Cardio Best for Weight Loss

In conclusion, both weight lifting and cardio can help you lose weight and improve your health. The approach and results can vary depending on your fitness goals, schedule, and preferences. It's essential to find a balance between weight lifting and cardio that works for you and prioritize proper form and recovery to prevent injury.

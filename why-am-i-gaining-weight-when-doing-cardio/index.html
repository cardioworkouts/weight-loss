Why Am I Gaining Weight When Doing Cardio

Written by Luffy Jun 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who has recently started doing cardio to lose weight but instead you are gaining weight? This can be frustrating and confusing. You are supposed to lose weight with cardio, right? So, what is the reason behind gaining weight when doing cardio?

The first reason for gaining weight when doing cardio could be the amount of food you are consuming. It is essential to keep a check on your calorie intake. If you are consuming more calories than you are burning, you will gain weight, no matter how much cardio you do.

Another reason for gaining weight when doing cardio is muscle gain. Muscle weighs more than fat, so when you start doing cardio, your body begins to burn fat and build muscle. This can result in weight gain, but it is a good sign as it means you are getting stronger and more toned.

In conclusion, gaining weight when doing cardio can be due to multiple reasons, including a high-calorie intake and muscle gain. It is essential to keep track of your food intake and exercise routine to ensure that you are on the right track to reach your weight loss goals.

Why Am I Gaining Weight When Doing Cardio? - Personal Experience

When I started doing cardio, I was expecting to lose weight. However, I noticed that I was gaining weight instead. It was frustrating, and I couldn't understand why. After consulting with my trainer, I realized that I was consuming more calories than I was burning. Once I started keeping a check on my calorie intake and exercised regularly, I started losing weight.

Muscle Gain and Weight Gain

When you start doing cardio, your body begins to burn fat and build muscle. As we mentioned earlier, muscle weighs more than fat, so this can result in weight gain. It is a good sign as it means you are getting stronger and more toned. However, it is essential to keep a check on your food intake and exercise routine to ensure that you are on the right track.

High-Calorie Intake

One of the main reasons for gaining weight when doing cardio is a high-calorie intake. It is essential to keep a check on your food intake and ensure that you are not consuming more calories than you are burning. You can also consult a nutritionist to help you plan a healthy diet that complements your exercise routine.

How to Overcome Weight Gain When Doing Cardio?

To overcome weight gain when doing cardio, you need to focus on your calorie intake and exercise routine. Keep a check on your food intake and ensure that you are not consuming more calories than you are burning. Incorporate strength training into your exercise routine to help build muscle and burn fat. Consult with a trainer or nutritionist to help you plan a healthy diet that complements your exercise routine.

Question and Answer

Q: Can cardio help in weight loss?

A: Yes, cardio can help in weight loss, but it is essential to keep a check on your calorie intake and exercise routine.

Q: How often should I do cardio?

A: It is recommended to do cardio for at least 30 minutes, five days a week.

Q: Can muscle gain result in weight gain?

A: Yes, muscle weighs more than fat, so muscle gain can result in weight gain.

Q: Should I consult with a trainer or nutritionist?

A: Yes, consulting with a trainer or nutritionist can help you plan a healthy diet and exercise routine that complements your weight loss goals.

Conclusion of Why Am I Gaining Weight When Doing Cardio

Gaining weight when doing cardio can be frustrating, but it is essential to understand the reasons behind it. It could be due to a high-calorie intake, muscle gain, or not incorporating strength training into your exercise routine. To overcome weight gain, keep a check on your food intake, exercise routine, and incorporate strength training.

How Long Should We Do Cardio Exercise

Written by Robby Nov 03, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you wondering how long should we do cardio exercise to get the best results? Many people are confused about the duration of their cardio workouts. Some think that longer is better, while others believe that a short session is enough. The truth is, the duration of your cardio exercise depends on several factors, including your fitness level, health status, and goals. In this tutorial, we will discuss how long should we do cardio exercise to achieve optimal results.

The duration of your cardio exercise should depend on your fitness level and goals. If you are a beginner, you should start with short sessions of about 10-15 minutes and gradually increase the time as your fitness level improves. On the other hand, if you are an experienced athlete, you may need longer cardio sessions of up to 60 minutes to challenge your body and achieve your goals.

The American Heart Association recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate cardio exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous cardio exercise per week. This translates to about 30 minutes of moderate cardio exercise or 15 minutes of vigorous cardio exercise per day, five days a week. However, this is just a general guideline, and you should adjust the duration of your workouts based on your individual needs and goals.

In summary, how long should we do cardio exercise varies depending on several factors. Your fitness level, health status, and goals should guide you in determining the duration of your workouts. It is important to start with short sessions and gradually increase the time as your fitness level improves. The American Heart Association recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate cardio exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous cardio exercise per week.

How Long Should We Do Cardio Exercise for Weight Loss?

When it comes to weight loss, how long should we do cardio exercise? The answer is, it depends on your calorie intake and expenditure. To lose weight, you need to create a calorie deficit by burning more calories than you consume. Cardio exercise can help you burn calories and create a deficit. The duration of your cardio exercise for weight loss should depend on your calorie needs and goals.

Personally, I have found that doing 30-45 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise, such as cycling or brisk walking, five days a week, has helped me lose weight and maintain my weight loss. However, you may need to do more or less depending on your individual needs and goals.

How Long Should We Do Cardio Exercise for Heart Health?

Cardio exercise is essential for heart health. It helps improve cardiovascular function, lower blood pressure, and reduce the risk of heart disease. So, how long should we do cardio exercise for heart health?

According to the American Heart Association, you should aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate cardio exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous cardio exercise per week to improve your heart health. You can break this down into 30 minutes of moderate cardio exercise or 15 minutes of vigorous cardio exercise per day, five days a week.

Benefits of Cardio Exercise

Cardio exercise has numerous benefits, including:

  • Improved cardiovascular function
  • Increased endurance and stamina
  • Weight loss and weight management
  • Reduced risk of chronic diseases, such as heart disease, diabetes, and cancer
  • Improved mood and mental health

Tips for Doing Cardio Exercise

Here are some tips for doing cardio exercise:

  • Choose an activity that you enjoy
  • Start with short sessions and gradually increase the time and intensity
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothes
  • Stay hydrated by drinking water before, during, and after your workout
  • Warm up and cool down properly to prevent injuries

How Long Should We Do Cardio Exercise for Stress Relief?

Cardio exercise is also a great way to relieve stress and improve mental health. It helps release endorphins, which are natural feel-good chemicals that can boost your mood and reduce stress.

Personally, I have found that doing 30-45 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise, such as jogging or swimming, three to four times a week, has helped me manage my stress levels and improve my mental health. However, you may need to do more or less depending on your individual needs and goals.

Question and Answer:

Q: Can I do cardio exercise every day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio exercise every day, but you should vary the intensity and duration of your workouts to prevent overuse injuries and burnout.

Q: How long should I wait to eat after cardio exercise?

A: You should wait at least 30 minutes to eat after cardio exercise to allow your body to cool down and recover. However, if you feel hungry, you can have a light snack, such as a piece of fruit or a protein shake, within 15-30 minutes of your workout.

Q: Should I do cardio exercise before or after strength training?

A: It depends on your goals. If you want to improve your cardiovascular fitness, you should do cardio exercise before strength training. If you want to build muscle and strength, you should do strength training before cardio exercise.

Q: How can I make cardio exercise more fun?

A: You can make cardio exercise more fun by trying different activities, such as dancing, hiking, or playing a sport. You can also listen to music or podcasts, exercise with a friend or group, or set goals and track your progress.

Conclusion of How Long Should We Do Cardio Exercise:

The duration of your cardio exercise depends on your individual needs and goals. To improve your fitness level, start with short sessions and gradually increase the time and intensity. To lose weight, create a calorie deficit and aim for 30-60 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise most days of the week. To improve your heart health, aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate cardio exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous cardio exercise per week. To relieve stress and improve mental health, aim for 30-45 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise three to four times a week. Remember to listen to your body and adjust the duration and intensity of your workouts as needed.

Does Running After Lifting Ruin Your Workout The Truth Revealed

Written by Petter Oct 05, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you one of those fitness enthusiasts who hit the gym for weightlifting and then go for a run? Do you wonder if running after lifting ruins your workout? Well, you're not alone. Many people have the same question in mind. In this blog post, we'll answer the question once and for all.

The Pain Points of Running After Lifting

It's not uncommon to feel exhausted after a rigorous weightlifting session. And then going for a run can be challenging. Some people believe that running after lifting can ruin their workout. They feel that their muscles won't recover well, and they won't see the desired results.

Does Running After Lifting Ruin Your Workout?

The short answer is no. Running after lifting doesn't ruin your workout. In fact, it can be beneficial in some ways. Running helps you burn more calories, improves your cardiovascular health, and aids in muscle recovery. However, the order in which you do these exercises matters. If you do cardio before weightlifting, it can affect your strength and endurance. But doing cardio after weightlifting can improve your performance.

Moreover, it's crucial to give your muscles enough time to recover after weightlifting. If you're doing both exercises on the same day, make sure to leave a gap of at least 30 minutes between them. This way, your muscles will have enough time to recover before you start running.

Main Points

In summary, running after lifting doesn't ruin your workout. In fact, it can help you achieve your fitness goals. However, it's important to keep in mind the following:

  • Do weightlifting before running.
  • Leave a gap of at least 30 minutes between the two exercises.
  • Ensure that your muscles have enough time to recover.

My Personal Experience

As someone who enjoys both weightlifting and running, I've tried doing them in different orders. I found that doing weightlifting before running improved my overall performance. I felt more energized and was able to run longer distances. Moreover, I noticed that my muscles recovered faster when I ran after weightlifting. However, it's crucial to listen to your body and do what works best for you.

The Benefits of Running After Lifting

As mentioned earlier, running after lifting can be beneficial in several ways. It can improve your cardiovascular health and help you burn more calories. Moreover, it aids in muscle recovery by increasing blood flow to the muscles. This way, your muscles get the oxygen and nutrients they need to recover faster.

The Best Time to Run After Lifting

The best time to run after lifting is when your muscles are warm and loose. This way, you'll prevent injury and improve your overall performance. Moreover, make sure to stretch after weightlifting to prevent soreness and improve flexibility.

How to Incorporate Running into Your Weightlifting Routine

Here are some tips to incorporate running into your weightlifting routine:

  • Start with a warm-up before weightlifting.
  • Do weightlifting before running.
  • Leave a gap of at least 30 minutes between the two exercises.
  • Stretch after weightlifting to prevent soreness.
  • Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your runs.

FAQs

Q: Is it better to run before or after lifting weights?

A: It's better to lift weights before running. This way, you'll have more energy and strength to perform both exercises.

Q: How long should I wait to run after lifting?

A: Wait at least 30 minutes before running after lifting. This way, your muscles will have enough time to recover.

Q: Can running after lifting cause injury?

A: Running after lifting can cause injury if you don't warm up properly or don't give your muscles enough time to recover. Make sure to stretch before and after exercising and leave a gap of at least 30 minutes between weightlifting and running.

Q: Can running after lifting help me lose weight?

A: Yes, running after lifting can help you burn more calories and lose weight. However, it's important to maintain a balanced diet and exercise routine to see results.

Conclusion

Running after lifting doesn't ruin your workout. In fact, it can be beneficial in several ways. However, it's crucial to follow the right order and leave enough time for your muscles to recover. Incorporating running into your weightlifting routine can help you achieve your fitness goals and lead a healthy lifestyle.

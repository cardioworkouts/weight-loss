Is It Good To Do Cardio After Workout

Written by Eveline Nov 15, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you wondering whether you should do cardio after your workout? Perhaps you’ve heard mixed opinions on this topic and are unsure which to believe. In this post, we’ll explore the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio after a workout, so you can make an informed decision about whether it’s right for you.

The question of whether to do cardio after a workout is a common pain point for many people. On one hand, some experts claim that it’s an effective way to burn more calories and improve cardiovascular health. On the other hand, some believe that it can interfere with muscle recovery and lead to overtraining.

The simple answer to the question of whether it’s good to do cardio after a workout is that it depends on your goals and preferences. If your primary goal is to improve cardiovascular health or burn more calories, then adding some cardio after your workout can be a great idea. However, if your main focus is on building muscle and strength, then doing cardio after your workout may not be the best choice.

In summary, here are the main points you should consider when deciding whether to do cardio after a workout:

  • Cardio can help improve cardiovascular health and burn calories.
  • Cardio after a workout may interfere with muscle recovery.
  • It’s important to balance cardio with strength training based on your goals.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio After a Workout

Personally, I’ve found that doing cardio after a workout is an effective way to improve my cardiovascular endurance and burn more calories. When I’m short on time, adding a quick cardio session to the end of my workout allows me to get in some extra activity without having to spend more time at the gym.

From a physiological standpoint, doing cardio after a workout can help improve your body’s ability to metabolize fat and use it for energy. This is because your body has already depleted its glycogen stores during your workout, which forces it to turn to other sources of fuel like fat.

The Drawbacks of Doing Cardio After a Workout

However, it’s important to note that doing cardio after a workout can also have some drawbacks. For example, it can interfere with your body’s ability to recover and repair muscle tissue, which can result in overtraining and injury.

Additionally, doing too much cardio can lead to a catabolic state in which your body breaks down muscle tissue for energy. This is especially true if you’re not consuming enough calories or protein to support muscle growth and repair.

How to Decide Whether to Do Cardio After a Workout

The key to deciding whether to do cardio after a workout is to consider your goals and preferences. If your primary goal is to improve cardiovascular health or burn more calories, then adding some cardio after your workout can be a great idea.

However, if you’re focused on building muscle and strength, then doing cardio after your workout may not be the best choice. Instead, you may want to focus on strength training and consume enough calories and protein to support muscle growth and repair.

Tips for Doing Cardio After a Workout

If you do decide to do cardio after a workout, there are a few tips you should keep in mind:

  • Start with a low intensity and gradually increase as your fitness improves.
  • Avoid doing too much cardio, as this can lead to overtraining and injury.
  • Be sure to properly fuel your body with carbohydrates and protein to support muscle recovery and growth.

Personal Experience

Personally, I’ve found that doing cardio after a workout is an effective way to improve my cardiovascular endurance and burn more calories. I usually start with a 10-minute low-intensity cardio session, such as walking on the treadmill, and gradually increase the intensity over time.

However, I make sure not to overdo it with cardio, as I’ve experienced overtraining and injury in the past. I also make sure to properly fuel my body with carbohydrates and protein to support muscle recovery and growth.

Question and Answer

Q: Is doing cardio after a workout bad for muscle recovery?

A: It can be if you’re not careful. Doing too much cardio after a workout can interfere with muscle recovery and repair, which can lead to overtraining and injury.

Q: Can doing cardio after a workout help me burn more calories?

A: Yes, doing cardio after a workout can help you burn more calories by increasing your overall energy expenditure.

Q: Should I do cardio after every workout?

A: It depends on your goals and preferences. If your primary goal is to improve cardiovascular health or burn more calories, then adding some cardio after your workout can be a great idea. However, if you’re focused on building muscle and strength, then doing cardio after every workout may not be the best choice.

Q: How much cardio should I do after a workout?

A: The amount of cardio you should do after a workout depends on your fitness level and goals. It’s best to start with a low intensity and gradually increase as your fitness improves, while being careful not to overdo it.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the answer to the question of whether to do cardio after a workout is not a straightforward one. It depends on your goals and preferences, as well as how careful you are about balancing cardio with strength training and muscle recovery. By considering these factors and following the tips outlined in this post, you can decide whether cardio after a workout is right for you.

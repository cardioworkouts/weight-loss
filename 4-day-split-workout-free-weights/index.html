4 Day Split Workout Free Weights A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Aug 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for an effective workout plan that utilizes free weights and can be completed in just four days? Look no further than the 4 day split workout free weights routine. This comprehensive guide will provide you with all the information you need to get started on this challenging but rewarding workout plan.

For many fitness enthusiasts, the idea of a four-day workout plan that incorporates free weights can be intimidating. However, with the right preparation and guidance, this workout plan can be an excellent way to build strength, increase muscle mass, and improve overall fitness.

The 4 day split workout free weights routine is designed to target specific muscle groups on designated days of the week. This plan typically includes two upper body days and two lower body days, with each day focusing on a different set of muscles. This approach allows for maximum muscle growth and recovery time.

In summary, the 4 day split workout free weights routine is a challenging but effective way to build muscle and improve overall fitness. By targeting specific muscle groups on designated days of the week and incorporating free weights, this workout plan can help you achieve your fitness goals.

Upper Body Day 1: Chest and Triceps

On the first upper body day of the week, the focus is on the chest and triceps muscles. This day typically includes exercises such as bench presses, flyes, and tricep pushdowns. Here is an example workout:

1. Bench Press: 4 sets x 8-10 reps

2. Incline Dumbbell Fly: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

3. Cable Crossover: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

4. Skullcrusher: 4 sets x 8-10 reps

5. Tricep Pushdown: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

During this workout, it's important to focus on proper form and technique, as well as gradually increasing weight and reps over time to continue challenging your muscles.

Lower Body Day 1: Legs and Abs

The first lower body day of the week focuses on the legs and abs muscles. This day typically includes exercises such as squats, lunges, and leg curls. Here is an example workout:

1. Barbell Squat: 4 sets x 8-10 reps

2. Leg Press: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

3. Walking Lunge: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

4. Leg Curl: 4 sets x 8-10 reps

5. Hanging Leg Raise: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

As with the upper body day, it's important to focus on proper form and gradually increase weight and reps over time to continue challenging your muscles.

The Benefits of Free Weight Workouts

One of the primary benefits of incorporating free weights into your workout routine is the increased level of muscle activation these exercises provide. When you lift free weights, your muscles must work harder to stabilize the weight and maintain proper form, leading to increased muscle growth and strength.

Tips for a Successful 4 Day Split Workout Free Weights Routine

To ensure a successful 4 day split workout free weights routine, it's important to focus on proper form and gradually increasing weight and reps over time. Additionally, be sure to give your muscles adequate time to rest and recover between workouts. Consider incorporating foam rolling, stretching, and rest days into your routine to prevent injury and promote muscle recovery.

Upper Body Day 2: Back and Biceps

The second upper body day of the week focuses on the back and biceps muscles. This day typically includes exercises such as pull-ups, rows, and bicep curls. Here is an example workout:

1. Pull-Ups: 4 sets x 8-10 reps

2. Seated Cable Row: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

3. Lat Pulldown: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

4. Barbell Curl: 4 sets x 8-10 reps

5. Hammer Curl: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

Again, focus on proper form and gradually increasing weight and reps over time to challenge your muscles and promote growth.

Lower Body Day 2: Glutes and Hamstrings

The second lower body day of the week focuses on the glutes and hamstring muscles. This day typically includes exercises such as deadlifts, hip thrusts, and hamstring curls. Here is an example workout:

1. Deadlifts: 4 sets x 8-10 reps

2. Hip Thrusts: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

3. Reverse Lunge: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

4. Hamstring Curl: 4 sets x 8-10 reps

5. Calf Raises: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

As with all workouts, focus on proper form and gradually increasing weight and reps over time to promote muscle growth and prevent injury.

Question and Answer

Q: Can beginners do the 4 day split workout free weights routine?

A: While this workout plan can be challenging, beginners can certainly incorporate it into their routine. It's important to start with lighter weights and focus on proper form to prevent injury and gradually increase weight and reps over time.

Q: How often should I do the 4 day split workout free weights routine?

A: This workout plan is typically done four days a week, with rest days in between to allow for muscle recovery. However, it's important to listen to your body and adjust the frequency and intensity of your workouts as needed.

Q: Can I do cardio on rest days?

A: Yes! Incorporating cardio on rest days can be a great way to improve cardiovascular health and overall fitness. Just be sure to listen to your body and rest when needed.

Q: How long should each workout be?

A: Each workout should be between 45-60 minutes, depending on the intensity and number of exercises included.

Conclusion of 4 Day Split Workout Free Weights

The 4 day split workout free weights routine is a challenging but effective way to build muscle and improve overall fitness. By targeting specific muscle groups on designated days of the week and incorporating free weights, this workout plan can help you achieve your fitness goals. Remember to focus on proper form, gradually increase weight and reps over time, and give your muscles adequate time to rest and recover.

How Many Times A Week Should You Do Cardio Exercise

Written by Bardi Nov 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering how many times a week you should do cardio exercise? It can be confusing to know what's best for your body, especially when there is so much conflicting advice out there. Maybe you're worried about overdoing it, or not doing enough. Whatever the case, we've got you covered.

Many people struggle with finding the right balance when it comes to cardio exercise. Some people do too much, while others don't do enough. It's important to find a happy medium that works for you, your body, and your lifestyle.

So, how many times a week should you do cardio exercise? According to the American Heart Association, adults should aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week. This equates to about 30 minutes of exercise five days a week for moderate-intensity, or 25 minutes of exercise three days a week for vigorous-intensity.

In summary, it's recommended to do cardio exercise at least 150 minutes per week. However, the amount of exercise you need depends on your individual health and fitness goals. It's important to consult with your doctor before starting any new exercise routine.

Personal Experience with Cardio Exercise

As someone who has struggled with finding the right balance of cardio exercise, I can tell you that it's not always easy. At first, I was doing too much and ended up injuring myself. Then, I wasn't doing enough and felt like I wasn't making any progress. It took some trial and error, but I eventually found a routine that worked for me.

Now, I aim to do cardio exercise at least three times a week for 30 minutes each session. I like to switch it up between running, cycling, and using the elliptical. I also incorporate strength training and stretching into my routine to keep my body balanced.

The Benefits of Cardio Exercise

Cardio exercise has numerous benefits for your physical and mental health. It can help improve your heart health, increase your endurance, and even boost your mood. Regular cardio exercise can also help reduce your risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes, obesity, and heart disease.

Tips for Starting a Cardio Exercise Routine

If you're new to cardio exercise, it's important to start slow and gradually increase your intensity. Here are some tips for starting a cardio exercise routine:

- Start with a warm-up and cool down to prevent injury

- Choose an exercise you enjoy, such as running, cycling, or swimming

- Gradually increase your intensity over time

- Aim for at least 30 minutes of exercise per session

- Incorporate strength training and stretching into your routine

How to Stay Motivated

Staying motivated can be a challenge, especially when it comes to exercise. Here are some tips to help you stay on track:

- Set realistic goals and track your progress

- Find a workout buddy for accountability

- Mix it up by trying new exercises or classes

- Reward yourself for reaching milestones

Personal Experience with Staying Motivated

As someone who struggles with staying motivated, I've found that setting realistic goals and tracking my progress helps me stay on track. I also like to mix up my routine by trying new exercises or classes. Having a workout buddy has also been helpful for accountability.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it okay to do cardio exercise every day?

A: It's generally safe to do cardio exercise every day, but it's important to listen to your body and take rest days when needed.

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio exercise after eating?

A: It's recommended to wait at least an hour after eating before doing cardio exercise to avoid discomfort or cramping.

Q: Is cardio exercise better in the morning or at night?

A: The best time of day to do cardio exercise depends on your personal preference and schedule. Some people prefer morning workouts to start their day, while others prefer evening workouts to de-stress after work.

Q: Can I do cardio exercise if I have a chronic condition?

A: It's important to consult with your doctor before starting any new exercise routine if you have a chronic condition. Your doctor can help you determine what type of exercise is safe for you.

Conclusion of How Many Times a Week Should You Do Cardio Exercise

In conclusion, it's recommended to do cardio exercise at least 150 minutes per week. However, the amount of exercise you need depends on your individual health and fitness goals. It's important to consult with your doctor before starting any new exercise routine, and to listen to your body to avoid injury or overtraining. Remember to start slow, gradually increase your intensity, and mix it up to stay motivated.

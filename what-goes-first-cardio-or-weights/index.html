What Goes First Cardio Or Weights The Ultimate Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you confused about what goes first cardio or weights? Do you feel like you're not making progress in your fitness routine? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this question and it's time to put the debate to rest once and for all.

Pain Points

There are several pain points associated with what goes first cardio or weights. Some people worry that doing cardio before weights will tire them out and make it harder to lift weights. Others are concerned that lifting weights before cardio will make them too tired to complete their cardio workout. There's also the question of which type of exercise is more effective for weight loss and overall health.

Answering the Question

The answer to what goes first cardio or weights is that it depends on your goals. If you're primarily focused on weight loss and improving your cardiovascular health, then starting with cardio is the way to go. This will help you burn more calories and improve your endurance. However, if your goal is to build muscle and increase your strength, then starting with weights is the better option. This will help you build lean muscle mass and improve your overall body composition.

Main Points

In summary, what goes first cardio or weights depends on your goals. If you're focused on weight loss and cardiovascular health, start with cardio. If you're focused on building muscle and strength, start with weights. It's important to find a balance between the two types of exercise for optimal results.

The Benefits of Cardio First

Personally, I prefer to start with cardio because it gets my heart rate up and helps me to warm up before lifting weights. I find that I have more energy for my weight lifting workouts when I start with cardio. Additionally, doing cardio first can help you burn more calories during your workout, which is great for weight loss goals.

The Benefits of Weights First

On the other hand, starting with weights can help you build more muscle and increase your overall strength. When you lift weights, your body burns calories even after your workout is over, which can lead to long-term weight loss results. Additionally, building lean muscle mass can help you look more toned and fit.

The Importance of a Balanced Routine

No matter which type of exercise you choose to start with, it's important to have a balanced routine that includes both cardio and weights. This will help you achieve your fitness goals and improve your overall health. You should aim to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio and two strength training sessions per week.

How to Create a Balanced Routine

The best way to create a balanced routine is to alternate between cardio and weights on different days. For example, you can do cardio on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday and weights on Tuesday and Thursday. This will give your body time to recover between workouts and prevent burnout.

The Bottom Line

At the end of the day, what goes first cardio or weights depends on your goals and personal preference. Both types of exercise are important for overall health and fitness, and a balanced routine is key. Find what works best for you and stick to it for optimal results.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio or weights first?

A: It depends on your goals. If you're focused on weight loss and cardiovascular health, start with cardio. If you're focused on building muscle and strength, start with weights.

Q: How much cardio should I do each week?

A: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week.

Q: Can I do cardio and weights on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weights on the same day, but be sure to give your body time to recover between workouts.

Q: How long should I wait between cardio and weights?

A: Aim to wait at least 30 minutes between cardio and weights to give your body time to recover.

Conclusion of What Goes First Cardio or Weights

In conclusion, what goes first cardio or weights depends on your goals and personal preference. Both types of exercise are important for overall health and fitness, and a balanced routine is key. Find what works best for you and stick to it for optimal results.

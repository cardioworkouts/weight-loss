Should You Take A Pre Workout Before Cardio

Written by Oliver Oct 13, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you someone who likes to hit the gym or go for a run in the morning to start your day? If yes, then you might be wondering whether you should take a pre workout supplement before your cardio workout. In this article, we will discuss the benefits and drawbacks of taking a pre workout before cardio, so you can make an informed decision.

Pain Points of Taking a Pre Workout Before Cardio

Before we dive into whether you should take a pre workout before cardio, let's first understand why someone would consider taking it. Pre workouts are designed to boost energy, endurance, and focus during a workout. However, taking a pre workout before cardio can have some drawbacks as well.

Answering the Target Question

The answer to whether you should take a pre workout before cardio is not a simple one. It depends on your fitness goals, the type of workout you are doing, and your body's tolerance to the ingredients in the pre workout supplement.

Personal Experience with Taking Pre Workout Before Cardio

As a fitness enthusiast, I have tried taking a pre workout before cardio and have experienced both positive and negative effects. On days when I take a pre workout, I feel more energized and focused during my workout. However, on some occasions, I have experienced jitters, nausea, and an increased heart rate.

When it comes to taking a pre workout before cardio, it's important to consider the type of workout you are doing. If you're doing low to moderate intensity cardio, such as jogging or walking, you may not need a pre workout supplement. However, if you're doing high-intensity interval training (HIIT) or long-distance running, a pre workout supplement can be beneficial.

Benefits of Taking a Pre Workout Before Cardio

One of the main benefits of taking a pre workout before cardio is increased energy and endurance. Pre workouts contain ingredients like caffeine, beta-alanine, and nitric oxide boosters, which help improve blood flow and oxygen delivery to your muscles. This can help you work out harder and longer, leading to better results.

Drawbacks of Taking a Pre Workout Before Cardio

However, taking a pre workout before cardio can have some drawbacks as well. Pre workouts can cause side effects like jitters, nausea, increased heart rate, and high blood pressure. Additionally, some pre workouts contain artificial sweeteners and colors, which may not be suitable for everyone.

Ingredients to Look for in a Pre Workout Before Cardio

If you've decided to take a pre workout before cardio, look for supplements that contain caffeine, beta-alanine, and nitric oxide boosters. These ingredients can help improve endurance, strength, and focus during your workout. Additionally, opt for pre workouts that are free from artificial sweeteners and colors.

How to Choose the Right Pre Workout Before Cardio

Before choosing a pre workout supplement, it's important to do your research and read reviews from other users. Make sure to choose a pre workout that is suitable for your fitness goals and body type. Additionally, start with a lower dosage and gradually increase it to avoid any negative side effects.

Question and Answer

Q. Can taking a pre workout before cardio help me burn more fat?

A. Yes, taking a pre workout before cardio can help you burn more fat. Pre workouts contain ingredients that help improve blood flow and oxygen delivery to your muscles, leading to better fat burning during your workout.

Q. Can taking a pre workout before cardio cause dehydration?

A. Yes, taking a pre workout before cardio can cause dehydration. Pre workouts contain caffeine, which is a diuretic and can cause increased urination. Make sure to drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout to avoid dehydration.

Q. Can I take a pre workout before morning cardio?

A. Yes, you can take a pre workout before morning cardio. However, make sure to start with a lower dosage and gradually increase it to avoid any negative side effects. Additionally, make sure to eat a light meal or snack before your workout to avoid any stomach discomfort.

Q. How long before cardio should I take a pre workout?

A. It's recommended to take a pre workout supplement 30-45 minutes before your workout to give it time to kick in. However, make sure to check the instructions on the label of your pre workout supplement, as different supplements may have different recommended dosages and timings.

Conclusion of Should You Take a Pre Workout Before Cardio

In conclusion, whether you should take a pre workout before cardio depends on your fitness goals, the type of workout you are doing, and your body's tolerance to the ingredients in the pre workout supplement. Make sure to do your research and read reviews before choosing a pre workout supplement. Additionally, start with a lower dosage and gradually increase it to avoid any negative side effects. Finally, make sure to drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout to stay hydrated.

Should You Do Cardio Or Strength Training First The Ultimate Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 16, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you confused about whether to start your workout with cardio or strength training? You're not alone. It's a common question among gym-goers, and the answer can vary depending on your fitness goals. In this article, we'll discuss the pros and cons of both options and help you decide which one is best for you.

Pain Points of Should You Do Cardio or Strength Training First

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to deciding whether to do cardio or strength training first is time management. Most people have limited time to work out, and they want to make the most of it. Another pain point is the fear of not getting the desired results. People want to know what will give them the most bang for their buck.

Answering the Target of Should You Do Cardio or Strength Training First

The answer to this question depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to lose weight, doing cardio first may be more effective. Cardio exercises burn more calories per minute than strength training exercises, making it the better choice for weight loss. However, if your goal is to build muscle, strength training should come first. Strength training exercises help you build lean muscle mass, which in turn burns more calories even when you're not working out.

It's important to note that there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. Your fitness goals, personal preferences, and workout routine will all play a role in determining whether you should do cardio or strength training first.

Summary of the Main Points

In summary, whether you should do cardio or strength training first depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight, doing cardio first may be more effective. If you want to build muscle, strength training should come first. It's important to consider your personal preferences, workout routine, and time constraints when making this decision.

Personal Experience with Doing Cardio or Strength Training First

As a personal trainer, I've seen clients achieve great results with both options. However, I've found that doing strength training first is usually the better choice for most people. This is because strength training exercises can be more challenging and require more energy and focus. By doing them first, you'll be able to give them your full attention and effort, which will lead to better results.

Additionally, doing strength training first can help you prevent injury during cardio exercises. Strength training exercises help prepare your muscles and joints for the movements and impact of cardio exercises, reducing your risk of injury.

The Pros and Cons of Doing Cardio First

There are pros and cons to starting your workout with cardio exercises. One of the biggest pros is that it can help you burn more calories overall, which is great for weight loss. Cardio exercises also help improve your cardiovascular health and endurance.

However, there are also some cons to doing cardio first. One of the biggest cons is that it can leave you feeling fatigued and less motivated to do strength training exercises. This can lead to a less effective workout overall.

The Pros and Cons of Doing Strength Training First

Starting your workout with strength training exercises also has its pros and cons. One of the biggest pros is that it can help you build muscle and increase your metabolism, which is great for weight loss. Strength training exercises also help improve your bone density and reduce your risk of injury.

One of the cons of doing strength training first is that it can be more challenging and require more energy and focus. This can make it harder to get through your cardio exercises, which can lead to a less effective workout overall.

Why Strength Training Is Important for Weight Loss

Strength training is an important part of any weight loss program. When you build lean muscle mass through strength training exercises, you increase your metabolism, which means you burn more calories even when you're not working out. This can help you achieve your weight loss goals more quickly and effectively.

How to Incorporate Cardio and Strength Training into Your Workout Routine

The best way to incorporate both cardio and strength training into your workout routine is to alternate between the two. For example, you could do strength training exercises on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, and cardio exercises on Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday. This will give your body time to rest and recover between workouts, and ensure that you're getting a well-rounded workout overall.

Personal Experience with Incorporating Cardio and Strength Training into My Workout Routine

As someone who enjoys both cardio and strength training, I've found that alternating between the two is the best way to achieve my fitness goals. By doing strength training exercises first, I'm able to give them my full attention and effort, which leads to better results. And by incorporating cardio exercises into my routine, I'm able to improve my cardiovascular health and endurance, which helps me perform better in my other workouts.

Question and Answer

Q: Should I do cardio or strength training first if I want to build muscle?

A: If your goal is to build muscle, you should do strength training first. Strength training exercises help you build lean muscle mass, which in turn burns more calories even when you're not working out.

Q: Should I do cardio or strength training first if I want to lose weight?

A: If your goal is to lose weight, doing cardio first may be more effective. Cardio exercises burn more calories per minute than strength training exercises, making it the better choice for weight loss.

Q: How often should I do cardio and strength training?

A: It's recommended that you do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week, along with at least two strength training sessions per week.

Q: How long should I wait between cardio and strength training exercises?

A: It's recommended that you wait at least 24 hours between strength training sessions to allow your muscles time to rest and recover. You can do cardio exercises on the days in between.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio or Strength Training First

Deciding whether to do cardio or strength training first can be a confusing and daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. By considering your fitness goals, personal preferences, and workout routine, you can make an informed decision that will help you achieve the results you're looking for. Remember to listen to your body and give yourself time to rest and recover between workouts, and you'll be well on your way to a healthier, happier you.

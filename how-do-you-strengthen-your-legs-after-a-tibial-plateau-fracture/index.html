How To Strengthen Your Legs After A Tibial Plateau Fracture

Written by Eveline Jun 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Breaking your leg can be a traumatic experience, especially if it's a tibial plateau fracture. After a serious injury like this, it can be challenging to regain strength and mobility in your leg. In this blog post, we'll discuss how to strengthen your legs after a tibial plateau fracture and get back to your daily routine.

Dealing with a tibial plateau fracture can cause significant pain and discomfort, making it difficult to move around. It can be frustrating not to be able to do the things you used to do with ease. With proper exercises and rehabilitation techniques, you can strengthen your legs and overcome the effects of this injury.

What is a Tibial Plateau Fracture?

A tibial plateau fracture is a severe injury that occurs in the upper part of the shinbone, near the knee joint. The tibial plateau is the flat surface on top of the shinbone that connects to the knee joint. When this area is fractured, it can cause pain, swelling, and difficulty walking. Sometimes surgery is needed to fix the fracture, and rehabilitation is necessary to regain strength and mobility.

Here are some tips on how to strengthen your legs after a tibial plateau fracture:

1. Start with Range of Motion Exercises

When recovering from a tibial plateau fracture, it's important to start with gentle range-of-motion exercises to prevent stiffness and improve flexibility. Ankle pumps, knee bends, and heel slides are good exercises to start with. These exercises can help you regain your range of motion and prepare your leg for more challenging exercises later on.

2. Strengthen Your Leg Muscles

Once you have regained some of your range of motion, it's time to start strengthening your leg muscles. Quadriceps, hamstring, and calf muscles are essential for walking and running. Strengthening these muscles can help you get back to your daily routine. Leg presses, wall sits, and lunges are some of the best exercises to strengthen your leg muscles.

3. Use Resistance Bands

Resistance bands are an excellent tool for strengthening your leg muscles. They can provide resistance during exercises and help you build strength gradually. You can use resistance bands for leg extensions, leg curls, and squats. Start with a light resistance band and increase the resistance as you get stronger.

4. Work with a Physical Therapist

Working with a physical therapist is an excellent way to get the support and guidance you need during your recovery. A physical therapist can help you develop a personalized exercise plan based on your specific needs and goals. They can teach you proper form, monitor your progress, and adjust your exercises as needed to ensure you are making progress.

Personal Experience

After my tibial plateau fracture, I felt overwhelmed and discouraged. However, with the help of my physical therapist, I was able to regain strength and mobility in my leg. We started with gentle range-of-motion exercises and gradually progressed to more challenging exercises. Resistance bands were an essential part of my recovery, and I noticed significant improvement in my leg strength over time.

FAQs

Q1. How long does it take to recover from a tibial plateau fracture?

Recovery time can vary depending on the severity of the fracture and the individual's overall health. It can take anywhere from six weeks to several months to recover fully from a tibial plateau fracture.

Q2. Can I walk after a tibial plateau fracture?

It may be challenging to walk immediately after a tibial plateau fracture. However, with proper treatment and rehabilitation, most people can regain the ability to walk without pain or discomfort.

Q3. Can I do exercises at home to strengthen my legs after a tibial plateau fracture?

Yes, there are many exercises you can do at home to strengthen your legs after a tibial plateau fracture. However, it's essential to work with a physical therapist to develop a personalized exercise plan that meets your specific needs and goals.

Q4. Is surgery necessary for a tibial plateau fracture?

Sometimes surgery is necessary to fix a tibial plateau fracture, especially if the fracture is severe or involves displacement of bone fragments. Your doctor will determine if surgery is necessary based on your specific situation.

Conclusion

Recovering from a tibial plateau fracture can be challenging, but with proper rehabilitation and exercise, you can regain strength and mobility in your leg. It's important to start with gentle range-of-motion exercises, move on to strengthening exercises, use resistance bands, and work with a physical therapist. With patience and persistence, you can get back to your daily routine and enjoy the activities you love.

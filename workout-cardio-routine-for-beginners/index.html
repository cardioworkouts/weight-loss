Workout Cardio Routine For Beginners A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Oct 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a beginner looking to start a workout cardio routine but have no idea where to begin? Look no further! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to start your cardio routine, the benefits of doing cardio, and some tips to help you stay motivated.

Starting a workout cardio routine can be challenging, especially if you have never exercised before. You may feel overwhelmed, confused, or unsure of where to start. Additionally, you may be worried about the pain and discomfort that comes with working out. However, these are all normal feelings, and everyone experiences them at some point.

The target of this article is to provide you with the necessary information to start and maintain a workout cardio routine. We will give you tips on how to choose the right cardio exercises, how to create a workout schedule, and how to stay motivated.

In summary, this article will cover the following:

  • Choosing the right cardio exercises
  • Creating a workout schedule
  • Staying motivated

Choosing the Right Cardio Exercises

When it comes to cardio exercises, there are many options to choose from. Some examples include running, cycling, swimming, and dancing. The most important thing is to choose an activity that you enjoy and can stick with. If you hate running, don't force yourself to do it. Instead, try something else that you find more enjoyable.

Personally, I started with running and quickly found out that it wasn't for me. However, I discovered that I love cycling and dancing. These activities have helped me to stay motivated and consistent with my cardio routine.

Additionally, it's important to consider your fitness level when choosing cardio exercises. As a beginner, you may want to start with low-impact exercises such as walking or cycling. As you progress, you can gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.

Creating a Workout Schedule

Creating a workout schedule is crucial for maintaining a consistent cardio routine. It's important to set aside time for exercise and make it a priority. Depending on your schedule, you may want to work out in the morning, afternoon, or evening.

Personally, I prefer to work out in the morning before starting my day. This helps me to stay energized throughout the day and ensures that I don't miss my workout due to other commitments.

When creating your workout schedule, start with a realistic goal. For example, you may want to aim for three 30-minute cardio sessions per week. As you progress, you can gradually increase the frequency and duration of your workouts.

Staying Motivated

Staying motivated is key to maintaining a consistent cardio routine. One way to stay motivated is to set realistic goals and track your progress. For example, you may want to aim to run for 10 minutes without stopping, and gradually increase the time each week.

Another way to stay motivated is to find a workout buddy or join a fitness class. Having someone to exercise with can make the experience more enjoyable and hold you accountable.

Additionally, it's important to mix up your routine and try new things. Doing the same workout every day can quickly become boring and lead to burnout. Try new exercises or switch up the intensity and duration of your workouts.

Q&A: Common Questions About Workout Cardio Routine for Beginners

Q: How often should I do cardio as a beginner?

A: As a beginner, aim for three to four cardio sessions per week. Gradually increase the frequency and duration of your workouts as you progress.

Q: What should I eat before and after my cardio workout?

A: It's important to fuel your body with the right nutrients before and after your workout. Before your workout, eat a small meal containing carbohydrates and protein. After your workout, eat a meal containing protein and healthy fats to help your muscles recover.

Q: Can I do cardio every day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio every day, but it's important to listen to your body and take rest days when needed. Overtraining can lead to burnout and injury.

Q: How long should a cardio workout be?

A: As a beginner, aim for 30 minutes of cardio per session. Gradually increase the duration as you progress.

Conclusion of Workout Cardio Routine for Beginners

Starting a workout cardio routine can be challenging, but with the right mindset and approach, it can also be enjoyable and rewarding. Remember to choose the right cardio exercises, create a workout schedule, and stay motivated. With time and consistency, you will see progress and improvements in your fitness level.

What Burns The Most Calories Lifting Weights Or Cardio

Written by Robby Nov 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you trying to lose weight and wondering what burns the most calories: lifting weights or cardio? The debate between lifting weights and doing cardio has been around for years, with many people swearing by one or the other. But which one is truly better for burning calories and losing weight?

When it comes to weight loss, the most important factor is creating a calorie deficit. This means burning more calories than you consume. Both lifting weights and doing cardio can help you achieve this deficit, but they do so in different ways.

Cardio exercises, such as running or cycling, are known for burning a lot of calories in a short amount of time. This is because cardio exercises are typically high-intensity, which means they get your heart rate up and keep it there for an extended period. On the other hand, lifting weights burns fewer calories during the workout itself, but it has a lasting effect on your metabolism, meaning your body continues to burn calories even after you've finished your workout.

In this article, we will explore which exercise burns the most calories, lifting weights or cardio, and provide some tips on how to get the most out of your workouts.

My Personal Experience with Lifting Weights and Cardio

As someone who has tried both lifting weights and cardio to lose weight, I can say that both have their benefits. When I first started my weight loss journey, I focused primarily on cardio, running on the treadmill or cycling for an hour a day. While I did see some weight loss, I found that I quickly hit a plateau and wasn't seeing the results I wanted.

That's when I decided to switch things up and start lifting weights. I was hesitant at first because I didn't want to bulk up, but I quickly learned that lifting weights is an excellent way to burn fat and build lean muscle. Not only did I start to see more significant changes in my body, but I also noticed that my metabolism was working faster, and I was burning more calories throughout the day.

The Benefits of Lifting Weights for Weight Loss

While cardio is an effective way to burn calories, lifting weights has some unique benefits when it comes to weight loss. For one, lifting weights helps build lean muscle mass, which increases your metabolism and helps you burn more calories throughout the day. Additionally, weightlifting helps to maintain bone density, which can help prevent osteoporosis as you age.

Another benefit of lifting weights is that it allows for more flexibility in your workouts. You can target specific muscle groups or do full-body workouts, depending on your goals. This makes weightlifting an excellent option for people who want to switch up their routine or who have specific areas they want to target.

The Benefits of Cardio for Weight Loss

While lifting weights has its benefits, cardio is also an effective way to burn calories and lose weight. Cardio exercises are typically high-intensity, meaning they get your heart rate up and keep it there for an extended period. This helps you burn a lot of calories in a short amount of time.

Additionally, cardio exercises are generally easy to do and require little to no equipment. Running, cycling, or even walking can all be effective cardio exercises, making them accessible to people of all fitness levels.

How to Get the Most Out of Your Workouts

Regardless of whether you choose to lift weights or do cardio, there are a few things you can do to get the most out of your workouts:

  • Set realistic goals: Having a specific goal in mind can help keep you motivated and on track.
  • Mix it up: Doing the same workout every day can get boring, so switch it up and try new exercises or routines.
  • Be consistent: Consistency is key when it comes to weight loss, so make sure you're sticking to a regular workout routine.
  • Eat a healthy diet: Exercise alone isn't enough to lose weight. You also need to eat a healthy, balanced diet to achieve your goals.

Question and Answer

Q. Can you lose weight by lifting weights?

A. Yes, lifting weights can help you lose weight by building lean muscle mass, which increases your metabolism and helps you burn more calories throughout the day.

Q. How many calories does lifting weights burn?

A. The number of calories burned during a weightlifting session depends on a variety of factors, such as the intensity of the workout and the person's weight. On average, lifting weights can burn anywhere from 100-300 calories per hour.

Q. Is it better to do cardio or lift weights to lose belly fat?

A. Both cardio and weightlifting can help you lose belly fat, but weightlifting may be more effective in the long run. This is because weightlifting helps build lean muscle mass, which increases your metabolism and helps you burn more calories throughout the day.

Q. How often should you lift weights or do cardio to lose weight?

A. It's recommended that adults get at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week, along with two or more days of strength training per week.

Conclusion

So, what burns the most calories: lifting weights or cardio? The truth is, both exercises can be effective for weight loss, depending on your goals and preferences. While cardio burns more calories during the workout itself, lifting weights has a lasting effect on your metabolism and can help you burn more calories throughout the day. Ultimately, the best exercise for weight loss is the one that you enjoy and can stick to consistently.

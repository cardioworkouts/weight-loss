Is It Better To Do Cardio After A Workout

Written by Daniel Jul 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who hits the gym regularly and often wonders if it is better to do cardio after a workout? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this question, and for good reason. After all, the order in which you perform your exercises can impact your overall fitness goals. In this article, we'll explore the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio after a workout to help you make an informed decision.

It's no secret that exercise is essential for optimal health. But what's often debated is the order in which you should perform your workouts. While some experts argue that cardio before weightlifting is the best approach, others believe that it's better to do cardio after a workout. Ultimately, the answer depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences.

If your main focus is building muscle and strength, weightlifting should be your priority. Doing cardio before weightlifting can deplete your energy levels, making it harder to lift heavy weights and perform exercises that require a lot of strength. However, if your goal is to lose weight and improve your cardiovascular health, doing cardio after a workout can help you achieve your objectives more effectively.

So, is it better to do cardio after a workout? The answer is yes, if your primary goal is to burn fat and improve your cardiovascular health. By doing cardio after weightlifting, your body can use stored fats as fuel, helping you burn more calories and lose weight faster. Additionally, doing cardio after a workout can help flush out lactic acid from your muscles, reducing soreness and fatigue.

In summary, the order in which you perform your workouts depends on your fitness goals. If you're looking to build muscle and strength, weightlifting should be your priority. However, if fat loss and cardiovascular health are your primary objectives, doing cardio after a workout can be the best approach.

My Personal Experience with Doing Cardio After a Workout

I have been following a workout routine for the past year, and I always do cardio after weightlifting. In my experience, this approach has helped me burn fat and lose weight effectively. I have also noticed that my muscles are less sore and fatigued after doing cardio, which makes it easier to stick to my workout routine.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio After a Workout

There are several benefits of doing cardio after a workout, including:

1. Increased fat burning: When you do cardio after weightlifting, your body uses stored fats as fuel, helping you burn more calories and lose weight faster.

2. Reduced soreness and fatigue: Doing cardio after a workout can help flush out lactic acid from your muscles, reducing soreness and fatigue.

3. Improved cardiovascular health: Regular cardio after weightlifting can improve your cardiovascular health, reducing your risk of heart disease and other chronic illnesses.

How to Incorporate Cardio After a Workout

If you decide to do cardio after weightlifting, it's essential to do it correctly. Here are some tips to help you incorporate cardio after a workout:

1. Start slow: Begin with short cardio sessions and gradually increase the duration and intensity over time.

2. Mix it up: To prevent boredom and improve results, try different types of cardio, such as running, cycling, or swimming.

3. Fuel up: Eat a small snack before your workout to give you the energy you need to perform cardio after weightlifting.

FAQs about Doing Cardio After a Workout

1. Is it better to do cardio before or after weightlifting?

It depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle and strength, weightlifting should be your priority. However, if fat loss and cardiovascular health are your primary objectives, doing cardio after a workout can be the best approach.

2. How long should I wait to do cardio after weightlifting?

It's best to wait at least 20-30 minutes after weightlifting before doing cardio. This will give your muscles time to recover and reduce the risk of injury.

3. Can I do cardio and weightlifting on the same day?

Yes, you can do cardio and weightlifting on the same day. However, it's best to do them at separate times, such as in the morning and evening, to give your body time to recover.

4. How often should I do cardio after a workout?

It depends on your fitness goals and schedule. Ideally, you should aim to do cardio after weightlifting at least 2-3 times a week to see results.

Conclusion of Is It Better to Do Cardio After a Workout

In conclusion, the order in which you perform your workouts can impact your fitness goals. If your primary objective is to build muscle and strength, weightlifting should be your priority. However, if you want to burn fat and improve cardiovascular health, doing cardio after a workout can be the best approach. Whatever your fitness goals, it's essential to listen to your body and do what feels right for you.

How Long Should An Aerobic Workout Be

Written by Luffy Jul 31, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you often wonder how long should an aerobic workout be? Do you feel like you're not getting the results you want from your workouts, and you're not sure if it's because you're not working out long enough? If so, this post is for you.

Aerobic workouts are a great way to improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and get in shape. However, if you're not working out for the right amount of time, you may not be reaping all the benefits of your efforts.

The general recommendation is to aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity per week. This can be broken down into 30 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity, five times per week, or 25 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity, three times per week.

In summary, how long should an aerobic workout be? It depends on the intensity of your workout. For moderate-intensity workouts, aim for at least 30 minutes, five times per week. For vigorous-intensity workouts, aim for at least 25 minutes, three times per week.

Personal Experience: Finding the Right Duration

Personally, I struggled with finding the right duration for my aerobic workouts. I was working out for an hour every day, but I wasn't seeing the results I wanted. It wasn't until I started doing shorter, more intense workouts that I started to see a difference.

When I started doing 30-minute HIIT workouts three times per week, I noticed a significant improvement in my endurance and overall fitness level. I also found that I had more time in my day to focus on other things, rather than spending an hour at the gym every day.

Breaking It Down

If you're new to exercise or have a health condition, it's important to start slow and gradually work your way up to longer durations. You can start with 10-15 minutes of moderate-intensity activity per day and gradually increase it by five minutes each week until you reach 30 minutes per day.

It's also important to mix up your workouts and try different types of aerobic activity. This can include walking, jogging, cycling, swimming, dancing, or any other activity that gets your heart rate up and makes you breathe harder.

Benefits of Aerobic Workouts

Aerobic workouts have numerous benefits, including:

- Improving cardiovascular health

- Burning calories for weight loss

- Reducing the risk of chronic diseases

- Boosting mood and reducing stress

- Improving sleep quality

Tips for Sticking with Your Workouts

To make sure you stick with your workouts and reach your goals, try these tips:

- Schedule your workouts into your calendar

- Find a workout buddy for accountability

- Mix up your workouts to prevent boredom

- Reward yourself for reaching milestones

Personal Experience: Finding Motivation

Personally, I find it hard to stay motivated with my workouts. To combat this, I've started tracking my progress and setting small goals for myself. For example, I'll try to run a little further or faster each week, or do a few extra reps of an exercise.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do all my aerobic activity in one day?

A: While it's better to spread out your aerobic activity throughout the week, you can do it all in one day if needed. Just make sure you're getting the recommended amount of activity per week.

Q: How do I know if I'm doing moderate or vigorous-intensity activity?

A: You can use the "talk test" to determine your intensity level. During moderate-intensity activity, you should be able to talk but not sing. During vigorous-intensity activity, you should not be able to say more than a few words without pausing for breath.

Q: Can I still do other forms of exercise if I'm doing aerobic activity?

A: Yes, it's important to include strength training and flexibility exercises in your fitness routine. Aim for at least two days of strength training per week and include stretching or yoga on a regular basis.

Q: Can I break up my workouts into smaller intervals throughout the day?

A: Yes, you can break up your workouts into 10-minute intervals throughout the day if needed. Just make sure you're getting the recommended amount of activity per week.

Conclusion

In summary, how long should an aerobic workout be? It depends on the intensity of your workout, but generally aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity per week. Remember to start slow, mix up your workouts, and find motivation to stick with your routine. With consistency and dedication, you'll be on your way to a healthier, happier you.

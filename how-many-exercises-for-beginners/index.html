How Many Exercises For Beginners A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a beginner in the world of fitness and wondering how many exercises you should do? You're not alone. Starting a fitness routine can be overwhelming, especially when it comes to figuring out how many exercises to include in your workout.

Many beginners struggle with finding the right balance between doing enough exercises to see results and not overdoing it and risking injury. It's important to understand that there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. The number of exercises you should do depends on various factors such as your fitness level, goals, and time availability.

What Is the Target of How Many Exercises for Beginners?

As a beginner, it's important to start with a few simple exercises and gradually increase the number and intensity as you progress. The target of how many exercises for beginners is to help you build a solid foundation and prevent injury while achieving your fitness goals.

When starting, it's recommended that you do 5-6 exercises per workout session, focusing on different muscle groups. This will help you get a full-body workout while avoiding fatigue and overtraining.

The Importance of Starting Slow

When I started my fitness journey, I made the mistake of trying to do too many exercises too soon. I ended up feeling burnt out and discouraged, which slowed down my progress. It's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.

As a beginner, it's also important to focus on proper form and technique rather than the number of exercises you're doing. Doing an exercise with incorrect form can lead to injury and hinder your progress. Start with lighter weights and focus on getting the movement right before increasing the weight or intensity.

Factors to Consider When Deciding How Many Exercises to Do

When deciding how many exercises to include in your workout, there are a few factors to consider:

  • Your fitness level
  • Your goals
  • Your time availability
  • Your recovery time

These factors will help you determine the right number of exercises to include in your workout routine. For example, if you're looking to build muscle, you may need to do more exercises and sets per muscle group than if you're just looking to maintain your current fitness level.

Tips for Choosing the Right Exercises

Choosing the right exercises is just as important as deciding how many exercises to do. As a beginner, it's important to focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once. Some examples of compound exercises include squats, lunges, push-ups, and pull-ups.

You should also choose exercises that you enjoy and that fit your goals. If you hate running, don't force yourself to run just because it's a popular exercise. There are plenty of other cardio exercises you can do, such as cycling, swimming, or dancing.

How Many Exercises for Beginners: FAQs

1. Can I do more than 6 exercises per workout session?

While it's possible to do more than 6 exercises per workout session, it's not recommended for beginners. Doing too many exercises can lead to fatigue, injury, and burnout. It's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and number of exercises as you progress.

2. How many workout sessions should I do per week?

As a beginner, it's recommended that you start with 2-3 workout sessions per week and gradually increase as you progress. It's important to give your body enough time to recover between workouts to prevent injury and burnout.

3. Should I do cardio or strength training?

Both cardio and strength training are important for overall health and fitness. As a beginner, it's recommended that you focus on building a foundation with strength training before adding in cardio. Once you've built a solid foundation, you can start incorporating cardio into your workouts.

4. Can I do the same exercises every workout session?

While it's possible to do the same exercises every workout session, it's not recommended. Doing the same exercises can lead to boredom and plateauing. It's important to switch up your exercises and vary your routine to keep your body challenged and prevent plateauing.

Conclusion of How Many Exercises for Beginners

As a beginner, it's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and number of exercises as you progress. Focus on proper form and technique, and choose exercises that fit your goals and preferences. Remember that there is no one-size-fits-all answer to how many exercises you should do, and it depends on various factors such as your fitness level, goals, and time availability. By following these tips, you can build a solid foundation and achieve your fitness goals safely and effectively.

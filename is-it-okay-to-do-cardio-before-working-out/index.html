Is It Okay To Do Cardio Before Working Out

Written by Eveline Aug 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you trying to figure out the best way to structure your workout routine? One common question that many gym-goers have is whether it's okay to do cardio before working out. If you're here, it's likely you have the same question. In this article, we'll explore the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio before working out, and help you determine if it's the best approach for your fitness goals.

If you're someone who typically hits the gym to lift weights or do resistance training, you may be hesitant to add cardio into your routine. This is because many believe that doing cardio before working out can lead to a decrease in strength and muscle gains. Others may feel that cardio is too physically taxing, and prefer to leave it until the end of their workout. Whatever your reason for wondering about the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio before working out, we're here to help you navigate the topic.

So, is it okay to do cardio before working out? The answer is that it depends on your fitness goals. If you're trying to build muscle and strength, it's generally recommended that you do resistance training before cardio. This is because cardio can deplete your glycogen stores, which can make it harder to lift weights and build muscle. However, if your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular health or lose weight, doing cardio before resistance training can be an effective approach.

In summary, the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio before working out depend on your fitness goals. If you're trying to build muscle and strength, it's generally recommended that you do resistance training before cardio. However, if your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular health or lose weight, doing cardio before resistance training can be an effective approach.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Working Out

Personally, I find that doing cardio before working out helps me warm up and get my body ready for the more intense aspects of my workout. For example, if I'm planning to do heavy squats or deadlifts, I'll start with a quick 10-15 minute run on the treadmill to get my heart rate up and get my blood flowing. This helps me feel more energized and focused during my lifts, and reduces my risk of injury.

Drawbacks of Doing Cardio Before Working Out

One of the main drawbacks of doing cardio before working out is that it can deplete your glycogen stores. This can make it harder to lift weights and build muscle, which can be frustrating if your primary goal is to bulk up. Additionally, if you're someone who struggles with motivation, doing cardio before working out can leave you feeling too tired to complete your strength training routine.

When Should You Do Cardio Before Working Out?

If you're someone who wants to include cardio in your workout routine, it's generally recommended that you do it before resistance training if you're trying to improve your cardiovascular health or lose weight. This is because doing cardio before resistance training can help you burn more calories and improve your endurance.

How to Incorporate Cardio into Your Workout Routine

If you're looking to incorporate cardio into your workout routine, there are a few different approaches you can take. Here are some tips:

  • Start with a quick warm-up: Before you dive into your resistance training routine, take a few minutes to warm up your body with some light cardio. This can be a quick jog on the treadmill, a few minutes on the elliptical, or some jumping jacks.
  • Alternate between cardio and resistance training: If you're doing a full-body workout, consider alternating between cardio and resistance training exercises. For example, you could start with a set of squats, followed by a minute of jumping jacks, and then move onto lunges.
  • Finish with cardio: If you prefer to save cardio until the end of your workout, try doing it as a finisher. This means that after you've completed your resistance training routine, you'll finish off with 10-15 minutes of intense cardio.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after lifting weights?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to build muscle and strength, it's generally recommended that you do resistance training before cardio. However, if you're trying to improve your cardiovascular health or lose weight, doing cardio before resistance training can be an effective approach.

Q: Can doing cardio before working out lead to muscle loss?

A: It's unlikely that doing cardio before working out will lead to muscle loss, but it can make it harder to build muscle and strength if your glycogen stores are depleted. If you're trying to build muscle, it's generally recommended that you do resistance training before cardio.

Q: How much cardio should I do before working out?

A: The amount of cardio you should do before working out depends on your fitness level and goals. If you're new to exercise, start with 5-10 minutes of cardio before your workout. If you're more experienced, aim for 15-20 minutes.

Q: Is it okay to do cardio every day?

A: It's generally safe to do cardio every day, but it's important to listen to your body and take rest days when you need them. If you're doing high-intensity cardio, it's recommended that you take at least one rest day per week to allow your body to recover.

Conclusion of Is It Okay to Do Cardio Before Working Out

In summary, whether it's okay to do cardio before working out depends on your fitness goals. If you're trying to build muscle and strength, it's generally recommended that you do resistance training before cardio. However, if your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular health or lose weight, doing cardio before resistance training can be an effective approach. Remember to listen to your body and adjust your workout routine as needed. Happy sweating!

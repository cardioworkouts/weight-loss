How To Get Fitbit Heart Rate On Strava A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 17, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to track your fitness progress, there's a good chance you own a Fitbit and use Strava to log your workouts. However, you may be wondering how to get your Fitbit heart rate data onto Strava. In this tutorial, we'll cover all the steps you need to take to make this happen.

To begin with, it's essential to understand why you would want to sync your Fitbit heart rate data with Strava. By doing so, you can gain a more accurate picture of your workouts and see how your heart rate changes during different activities. This information can help you tailor your training and improve your performance.

To get your Fitbit heart rate data on Strava, you'll need to follow a few simple steps. First, you'll need to make sure you have both the Fitbit and Strava apps installed on your smartphone. Then, you'll need to connect your Fitbit account to Strava and enable heart rate tracking. Finally, you'll need to make sure your Fitbit is synced to your phone and that your heart rate data is being recorded.

In summary, here are the steps you need to follow to get Fitbit heart rate on Strava:

Step 1: Connect Your Fitbit Account to Strava

To connect your Fitbit account to Strava, open the Strava app on your smartphone and tap on the "Profile" tab. Then, tap on the "Settings" icon in the top right corner of the screen. Scroll down to "Partner Integrations" and tap on "Fitbit." Follow the prompts to sign in to your Fitbit account and authorize Strava to access your data.

Step 2: Enable Heart Rate Tracking on Strava

After connecting your Fitbit account to Strava, you'll need to enable heart rate tracking. To do this, go to the "Profile" tab in the Strava app and tap on "Settings." Then, tap on "Privacy Controls" and make sure "Heart Rate" is toggled on.

Step 3: Sync Your Fitbit and Record Your Heart Rate Data

Finally, you'll need to make sure your Fitbit is synced to your smartphone and that your heart rate data is being recorded. To do this, open the Fitbit app on your phone and make sure it is synced. Then, start a workout on Strava and make sure heart rate tracking is enabled. Your heart rate data should now be visible in your Strava activity.

My Personal Experience with Getting Fitbit Heart Rate on Strava

As an avid runner and cyclist, I rely on Strava to track my workouts and monitor my progress. However, I always felt like I was missing out on important heart rate data by using Strava alone. That's why I decided to sync my Fitbit heart rate data with Strava.

Following the steps outlined above, I was able to connect my Fitbit account to Strava and enable heart rate tracking in just a few minutes. Now, I can see how my heart rate changes during different activities and adjust my training accordingly.

Common Issues When Getting Fitbit Heart Rate on Strava

While getting your Fitbit heart rate on Strava is a relatively straightforward process, there are a few common issues you may encounter.

Issue 1: Fitbit and Strava Not Syncing

If your Fitbit and Strava accounts are not syncing, make sure both apps are up to date and that you have a stable internet connection. You may also need to disconnect and reconnect your accounts.

Issue 2: Heart Rate Data Not Showing Up in Strava

If your heart rate data is not showing up in Strava, make sure heart rate tracking is enabled in both the Fitbit and Strava apps. You may also need to restart your phone or device.

Issue 3: Inaccurate Heart Rate Readings

If you're getting inaccurate heart rate readings, make sure your Fitbit is properly fitted and that the sensors are clean. You may also need to adjust the placement of your Fitbit on your wrist.

Issue 4: Battery Life

Finally, keep in mind that using heart rate tracking on your Fitbit can drain the battery more quickly. Make sure your Fitbit is fully charged before starting a workout.

Conclusion of How to Get Fitbit Heart Rate on Strava

By following the steps outlined in this tutorial, you can easily get your Fitbit heart rate data on Strava and gain a more accurate picture of your workouts. While there may be some common issues you encounter along the way, troubleshooting these problems should be relatively straightforward.

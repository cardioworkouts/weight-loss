Can You Do Cardio On A Total Gym A Complete Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a convenient and efficient way to do cardio at home? Do you own a Total Gym but unsure if you can use it for cardio workouts? This guide is for you! In this article, we will discuss how you can do cardio on a Total Gym, its benefits, and some sample workouts you can try.

When it comes to cardio workouts, most people think of running, cycling, or swimming. However, these exercises require a lot of space, equipment, and time. Not everyone has access to a gym or the outdoors, especially during bad weather. Moreover, some people may have joint pain or injuries that prevent them from doing high-impact exercises.

Fortunately, you can still get a great cardio workout on a Total Gym. This versatile machine allows you to perform a wide range of exercises that can raise your heart rate, burn calories, and improve your cardiovascular health. By using your body weight as resistance, you can challenge your muscles and lungs while minimizing the impact on your joints.

Here are the main benefits of doing cardio on a Total Gym:

Low-Impact Exercise

Unlike running or jumping, using a Total Gym for cardio is a low-impact exercise that reduces stress on your joints. This is especially beneficial for people with knee, hip, or back problems, as well as older adults who may be more susceptible to injuries. By adjusting the incline and resistance of the Total Gym, you can control the intensity of your workout without straining your body.

Total-Body Workout

Another advantage of doing cardio on a Total Gym is that it engages multiple muscle groups at once. Most cardio machines focus on the lower body, such as treadmills, bikes, or ellipticals. However, with a Total Gym, you can work your legs, core, arms, and back in various ways. This not only burns more calories but also strengthens your entire body and improves your posture.

Sample Total Gym Cardio Workouts

Now that you know the benefits of doing cardio on a Total Gym, let's explore some sample workouts you can try. Remember to warm up and cool down before and after each session, and adjust the resistance and incline according to your fitness level.

1. Total Gym Rowing

Set the Total Gym at a low incline and attach the pulley to the glideboard. Sit facing the tower with your feet on the squat stand and hold the handles with an overhand grip. Lean back slightly and pull the handles towards your chest as you extend your legs. Return to the starting position and repeat for 10-15 reps. This exercise works your back, arms, and legs while simulating a rowing machine.

2. Total Gym Jumping Jacks

Set the Total Gym at a high incline and stand facing the tower. Hold the handles with an overhand grip and jump your feet apart and together as you raise and lower your arms. Repeat for 30-60 seconds. This exercise elevates your heart rate and works your legs, shoulders, and core.

3. Total Gym Mountain Climbers

Set the Total Gym at a medium incline and place your hands on the glideboard in a plank position. Bring one knee towards your chest and then switch legs as fast as you can while keeping your hips stable. Repeat for 30-60 seconds. This exercise targets your abs, obliques, and legs while improving your agility and coordination.

4. Total Gym Plyometric Lunges

Set the Total Gym at a low incline and stand facing away from the tower. Place one foot on the glideboard and lunge down with the other leg as you push the glideboard back. Then, explode up and switch legs mid-air, landing in a lunge with the other leg forward. Repeat for 10-15 reps. This exercise combines cardio and strength training while challenging your balance and explosiveness.

FAQs About Doing Cardio on a Total Gym

1. Can You Lose Weight by Doing Cardio on a Total Gym?

Yes, you can lose weight by doing cardio on a Total Gym, as long as you create a calorie deficit by burning more calories than you consume. Cardio workouts on a Total Gym can burn up to 500 calories per hour, depending on your intensity and duration. However, weight loss also depends on your diet, sleep, stress levels, and other lifestyle factors.

2. Is It Safe to Do Cardio on a Total Gym?

Yes, it is safe to do cardio on a Total Gym, as long as you follow the instructions and use proper form. Make sure to adjust the incline and resistance gradually, avoid sudden movements or jerks, and keep your core engaged and your breathing steady. If you have any health concerns or injuries, consult your doctor before starting any exercise program.

3. How Often Should You Do Cardio on a Total Gym?

It is recommended to do cardio workouts on a Total Gym at least 3-4 times a week, for 30-60 minutes per session. However, the frequency and duration may vary depending on your goals, fitness level, and schedule. You can also combine cardio with strength training and flexibility exercises on a Total Gym for a well-rounded workout routine.

4. What Are Some Tips for Doing Cardio on a Total Gym?

Here are some tips to help you maximize your cardio workouts on a Total Gym:

  • Start with a lower incline and resistance and gradually increase as you get stronger and more comfortable.
  • Breathe deeply and evenly throughout the workout, exhaling on the effort and inhaling on the recovery.
  • Use different variations and combinations of exercises to avoid boredom and challenge your body in new ways.
  • Track your progress and set realistic goals to stay motivated and accountable.
  • Stay hydrated and refuel with a balanced meal or snack after your workout.

Conclusion of Can You Do Cardio on a Total Gym

Cardio workouts on a Total Gym are a convenient, effective, and low-impact way to improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and tone your muscles. By using your body weight as resistance, you can challenge yourself in various ways and avoid the monotony of traditional cardio machines. Whether you are a beginner or an advanced athlete, there are plenty of exercises you can do on a Total Gym to achieve your fitness goals.

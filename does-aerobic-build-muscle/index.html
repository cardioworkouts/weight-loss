Does Aerobic Build Muscle

Written by William Nov 05, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of endless hours at the gym without seeing any muscle growth? Have you considered adding aerobic exercise to your routine, but you're not sure if it will help build muscle? If you're looking for a way to maximize your workouts, keep reading to find out if aerobic exercise can help you build muscle.

Aerobic exercise, also known as cardio, is any form of exercise that increases your heart rate and breathing rate. It includes activities such as running, cycling, swimming, and dancing. While aerobic exercise is great for improving cardiovascular health and burning calories, many people wonder if it can also build muscle.

The short answer is yes, aerobic exercise can build muscle, but it may not be as effective as resistance training. Resistance training, such as weight lifting, is the most efficient way to build muscle mass. However, aerobic exercise can still help you build muscle, especially if you're a beginner or looking to add variety to your workout routine.

Incorporating aerobic exercise into your routine can help increase blood flow and oxygen to your muscles, which can improve endurance and strength. It can also help reduce body fat, which can make your muscles more visible. So while it may not be the most efficient way to build muscle, aerobic exercise can still be a beneficial addition to your workout routine.

My Experience with Aerobic Exercise and Muscle Building

When I first started working out, I focused solely on weight lifting to build muscle. However, I quickly realized that I was neglecting my cardiovascular health and endurance. I decided to incorporate running into my routine, and I was surprised to see that it actually helped me build muscle.

While I didn't see the same level of muscle growth as I did with weight lifting, I did notice an improvement in my overall physique. My muscles were more defined, and I had more endurance during my weight lifting workouts. Incorporating aerobic exercise also helped me reduce my body fat, which made my muscles more visible.

The Benefits of Aerobic Exercise for Muscle Building

While resistance training is the most efficient way to build muscle mass, there are still many benefits to incorporating aerobic exercise into your routine:

1. Improved Endurance:

Aerobic exercise can help improve your endurance, which can help you perform better during weight lifting workouts. By improving your endurance, you'll be able to lift heavier weights for longer periods of time, which can help you build more muscle.

2. Reduced Body Fat:

Aerobic exercise can help reduce body fat, which can make your muscles more visible. By reducing your body fat, you'll be able to see the muscle you're building more clearly, which can be a great motivator.

3. Increased Blood Flow and Oxygen:

Aerobic exercise can help increase blood flow and oxygen to your muscles, which can improve strength and endurance. By improving blood flow and oxygen delivery, you'll be able to perform better during weight lifting workouts, which can help you build more muscle.

4. Variety:

Incorporating aerobic exercise into your routine can help add variety and prevent boredom. Doing the same workout routine day after day can lead to burnout, so adding variety can help keep you motivated and engaged.

Tips for Incorporating Aerobic Exercise into Your Routine

If you're looking to add aerobic exercise to your routine, here are some tips to help you get started:

1. Start Slow:

If you're new to aerobic exercise, start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. This can help prevent injury and ensure that you're able to stick with it long-term.

2. Mix it Up:

Try different types of aerobic exercise to keep things interesting. Running, cycling, swimming, and dancing are all great options.

3. Combine with Resistance Training:

While aerobic exercise can help build muscle, it's still important to incorporate resistance training into your routine for maximum muscle growth.

4. Fuel Your Workouts:

Make sure to fuel your workouts with proper nutrition. Eating a balanced diet with plenty of protein can help support muscle growth.

Question and Answer

Q: Can aerobic exercise replace resistance training for muscle building?

A: While aerobic exercise can help build muscle, it's not as efficient as resistance training for muscle growth. It's still important to incorporate resistance training into your routine for maximum muscle growth.

Q: How often should I incorporate aerobic exercise into my routine?

A: It's recommended to incorporate at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week for overall health benefits. However, the frequency and duration of your aerobic exercise will depend on your individual goals and fitness level.

Q: Can I build muscle with low-intensity aerobic exercise?

A: Low-intensity aerobic exercise, such as walking, may not be as effective for muscle building as high-intensity exercise. However, it can still be beneficial for overall health and can help improve endurance.

Q: Should I do aerobic exercise before or after resistance training?

A: It's generally recommended to do resistance training before aerobic exercise, as this can help prevent fatigue during weight lifting workouts. However, the order of your workouts will depend on your individual goals and preferences.

Conclusion of Does Aerobic Build Muscle

While resistance training is the most efficient way to build muscle mass, aerobic exercise can still be a beneficial addition to your workout routine. By improving endurance, reducing body fat, and increasing blood flow and oxygen to your muscles, aerobic exercise can help support muscle growth. So if you're looking to add variety to your routine or improve your overall fitness, consider incorporating some form of aerobic exercise into your workouts.

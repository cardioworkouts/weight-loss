Good Starter Cardio Workout A Beginner s Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to start your fitness journey with a good cardio workout but don't know where to begin? It can be overwhelming to navigate the world of cardio exercises, especially if you're new to working out. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll guide you through a good starter cardio workout that will help you improve your cardiovascular health and kickstart your fitness journey.

Starting a new workout routine can be challenging, especially if you're not sure where to start. Maybe you're feeling intimidated by the gym, or you're not sure which exercises are best for you. Whatever your concerns may be, just know that you're not alone. Many people struggle with finding the right workout routine that works for them. But with a little bit of guidance, you can find a good starter cardio workout that suits your needs and helps you achieve your fitness goals.

So, what exactly is a good starter cardio workout? In simple terms, it's a workout that gets your heart rate up and improves your cardiovascular health. This type of workout can help you burn calories, lose weight, and improve your overall health. Some examples of good starter cardio workouts include jogging, cycling, swimming, and dancing.

In this article, we'll explore some of the best cardio exercises for beginners and provide you with a step-by-step guide to help you get started. We'll also cover some tips on how to stay motivated and stick to your workout routine.

Jogging

Jogging is a great way to get started with cardio exercise. It's a low-impact exercise that's easy on your joints and can be done anywhere, at any time. To get started, all you need is a good pair of running shoes and a flat surface to run on.

When starting out, it's important to take it slow and gradually build up your endurance. Start with a brisk walk and gradually transition into a jog. Aim to jog for at least 20-30 minutes per session, three to four times per week. As you build up your endurance, you can increase your speed and distance.

Cycling

Cycling is another great option for beginners. It's a low-impact exercise that's easy on your joints and can be done indoors or outdoors. To get started, you'll need a stationary bike or a regular bike if you prefer to cycle outdoors.

Start with a 10-15 minute warm-up to get your heart rate up, then gradually increase your speed and resistance. Aim to cycle for at least 30 minutes per session, three to four times per week. As you build up your endurance, you can increase your speed and resistance.

Jumping Jacks

If you're looking for a cardio workout that can be done anywhere, jumping jacks are a great option. They're easy to do and require no equipment. To get started, stand with your feet together and your arms at your sides. Jump up and spread your legs apart while raising your arms above your head. Jump back to the starting position and repeat.

Aim to do three sets of 20 jumping jacks, three to four times per week. As you build up your endurance, you can increase the number of sets and reps.

Dancing

Dancing is a fun and effective way to get your heart rate up and improve your cardiovascular health. It's a low-impact exercise that can be done alone or with a partner. To get started, find a dance style that you enjoy, such as salsa, hip hop, or ballroom dancing.

Start with a 10-15 minute warm-up to get your heart rate up, then gradually increase the intensity of your dancing. Aim to dance for at least 30 minutes per session, three to four times per week. As you build up your endurance, you can increase the length and intensity of your dance sessions.

Question and Answer

Q. How often should I do cardio?

A. Aim to do cardio exercise for at least 30 minutes per session, three to four times per week.

Q. What should I eat before a cardio workout?

A. Eat a light meal or snack that's high in carbohydrates and low in fat and protein. Good options include a banana, a granola bar, or a smoothie.

Q. How do I stay motivated to do cardio?

A. Set goals for yourself, find a workout buddy, listen to music, and switch up your workout routine to keep things interesting.

Q. Can I do cardio if I have joint pain?

A. Yes, but it's important to choose low-impact exercises that are easy on your joints, such as cycling or swimming.

Conclusion of Good Starter Cardio Workout

Starting a good cardio workout routine can be challenging, but with the right guidance and motivation, you can achieve your fitness goals. Whether you choose to jog, cycle, jump, or dance, make sure to start slow and gradually build up your endurance. Aim to do cardio exercise for at least 30 minutes per session, three to four times per week. And don't forget to stay motivated and have fun!

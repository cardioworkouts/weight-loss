Is It Okay To Do Cardio After Workout

Written by Petter Aug 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who enjoys hitting the gym and doing a good workout? Do you also love doing some cardio to get your heart rate up and burn some extra calories? But have you ever wondered if doing cardio after a workout is a good idea or not? In this blog post, we will explore the topic of whether it is okay to do cardio after a workout.

Many people have different opinions when it comes to doing cardio after a workout. Some say that it is not a good idea, while others swear by it. The truth is that it depends on your fitness goals and the intensity of your workout.

If your goal is to build muscle and gain strength, then doing cardio after a workout may not be the best idea. This is because cardio can deplete your energy levels, making it harder for you to lift heavy weights and perform strength training exercises. However, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular health and burn some extra calories, then doing cardio after a workout can be beneficial.

In conclusion, whether it is okay to do cardio after a workout depends on your fitness goals and the intensity of your workout. If you are unsure, it is always best to consult with a certified personal trainer who can help you create a workout plan that is tailored to your needs.

Benefits of Doing Cardio after a Workout

Personally, I have found that doing cardio after a workout has helped me improve my endurance and cardiovascular health. I used to struggle with running and other cardio exercises, but after incorporating them into my post-workout routine, I have noticed a significant improvement in my overall fitness level.

When you do cardio after a workout, your body is already warmed up and ready to go. This means that you can push yourself harder and get more out of your cardio session. It also helps to increase blood flow to your muscles, which aids in recovery and reduces soreness.

Drawbacks of Doing Cardio after a Workout

On the other hand, if you are someone who is trying to build muscle and gain strength, doing cardio after a workout may not be the best idea. This is because cardio can deplete your energy levels, making it harder for you to lift heavy weights and perform strength training exercises. It can also lead to overtraining and burnout if you are not careful.

How to Incorporate Cardio after a Workout

If you do decide to incorporate cardio after a workout, it is important to do it in a way that complements your fitness goals. This means choosing the right type of cardio and controlling the intensity and duration of your workout.

For example, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular health and burn some extra calories, you can try doing low to moderate intensity cardio exercises such as jogging, cycling or using the elliptical machine for 20-30 minutes after your strength training session. This will help you maintain your energy levels and burn extra calories without compromising your strength training workout.

Tips for Doing Cardio after a Workout

Here are some tips to keep in mind if you decide to incorporate cardio after a workout:

  • Choose a low to moderate intensity cardio exercise
  • Control the intensity and duration of your workout
  • Make sure to cool down and stretch properly after your workout to avoid injury
  • Stay hydrated and refuel with a post-workout snack or meal

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after a workout?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to build muscle and gain strength, it is best to do strength training exercises first and then do cardio afterwards. If your goal is to improve your cardiovascular health and burn some extra calories, you can do cardio before or after your workout.

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio after a workout?

A: It is recommended to wait at least 30 minutes after your strength training workout before doing cardio. This allows your body to recover and avoid overtraining.

Q: Can doing cardio after a workout help with weight loss?

A: Yes, doing cardio after a workout can help you burn extra calories and aid in weight loss. However, it is important to control the intensity and duration of your workout to avoid overtraining and burnout.

Q: Can doing cardio after a workout be harmful?

A: It can be harmful if you overdo it or do not allow your body to properly recover. It is important to listen to your body and consult with a certified personal trainer if you are unsure about the intensity and duration of your workout.

Conclusion of Is it Okay to Do Cardio after Workout

Whether it is okay to do cardio after a workout depends on your fitness goals and the intensity of your workout. If you are someone who is trying to build muscle and gain strength, doing cardio after a workout may not be the best idea. However, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular health and burn some extra calories, doing cardio after a workout can be beneficial. It is important to listen to your body and consult with a certified personal trainer if you are unsure about the intensity and duration of your workout.

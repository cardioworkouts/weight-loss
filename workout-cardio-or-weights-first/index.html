Workout Cardio Or Weights First Which Is Better

Written by Christine Jun 06, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you confused about whether to do cardio or weights first during your workout? This question has been a topic of debate among fitness enthusiasts for years. Some people believe that cardio should be done before weights, while others think that weights should always come first. In this article, we will explore the benefits of both cardio and weights and help you decide which one to prioritize during your workout.

When it comes to workout cardio or weights first, the decision ultimately depends on your fitness goals. Both cardio and weights have their own unique benefits, and which one you prioritize will depend on what you're hoping to achieve.

If you're looking to improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and lose weight, then cardio should be your top priority. Cardio exercises such as running, cycling, or swimming are excellent for strengthening your heart and lungs, and burning a lot of calories in the process. By doing cardio before weights, you will ensure that your body is warmed up and ready to perform at its best during your weightlifting session.

On the other hand, if you're looking to build muscle, increase strength, and improve your overall body composition, then weights should be your top priority. Weightlifting exercises such as squats, deadlifts, and bench presses are excellent for building muscle and increasing strength. By doing weights before cardio, you will ensure that your body has all the energy it needs to perform at its best during your weightlifting session.

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio or weights first during your workout depends on your fitness goals. If you're looking to improve your cardiovascular health and burn calories, then cardio should be your top priority. If you're looking to build muscle and increase strength, then weights should be your top priority.

Why Cardio Should Come First

Personally, I always start my workout with cardio. I find that doing cardio first helps me to warm up and get my heart rate up, which prepares me for my weightlifting session. After a good cardio session, I feel energized and ready to tackle my weightlifting exercises.

Cardio is also an excellent way to burn calories and lose weight. By doing cardio before weights, you will burn more calories during your workout, which can help you to achieve your weight loss goals faster.

Why Weights Should Come First

While I prefer to start with cardio, some people prefer to start with weights. The main reason for this is that weightlifting exercises require a lot of energy and focus. By doing weights first, you will ensure that your body has all the energy it needs to perform at its best.

Weightlifting is also an excellent way to build muscle and increase strength. By doing weights before cardio, you will be able to focus all your energy and attention on your weightlifting exercises, which can help you to achieve your muscle-building goals faster.

How to Incorporate Both Cardio and Weights into Your Workout

If you're looking to incorporate both cardio and weights into your workout, the best way to do this is to alternate between the two. For example, you could start with 20 minutes of cardio, followed by 20 minutes of weights, and then finish with another 20 minutes of cardio. This will ensure that you get the benefits of both cardio and weights, without over-exerting yourself.

How to Choose the Right Cardio and Weightlifting Exercises

When it comes to choosing the right cardio and weightlifting exercises, it's important to choose exercises that are appropriate for your fitness level and goals. If you're new to exercise, start with low-impact cardio exercises such as walking or cycling, and light weightlifting exercises such as bodyweight squats or lunges. As you progress, you can gradually increase the intensity and difficulty of your workouts.

Conclusion of Workout Cardio or Weights First

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio or weights first during your workout depends on your fitness goals. If you're looking to improve your cardiovascular health and burn calories, then cardio should be your top priority. If you're looking to build muscle and increase strength, then weights should be your top priority. Ultimately, the most important thing is to listen to your body and do what feels right for you. By incorporating both cardio and weights into your workout, you can achieve a balanced and effective fitness routine that will help you to achieve your goals.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it okay to do cardio and weights on the same day?

A: Yes, it's perfectly fine to do cardio and weights on the same day. Just make sure to alternate between the two and give your body enough time to rest and recover between workouts.

Q: How often should I do cardio and weights?

A: This depends on your fitness goals and schedule. Generally, it's recommended to do cardio and weights at least 3-4 times per week. However, if you're short on time, you can also do a combination of cardio and weights in the same workout.

Q: Should I do cardio before or after weights?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If you're looking to improve your cardiovascular health and burn calories, then cardio should come first. If you're looking to build muscle and increase strength, then weights should come first.

Q: How long should my cardio and weightlifting sessions be?

A: This depends on your fitness level and goals. Generally, it's recommended to do at least 20-30 minutes of cardio and 20-30 minutes of weightlifting per session. However, if you're short on time, you can also do shorter workouts and still get the benefits.

Is Cardio After Weight Lifting Beneficial

Written by Eveline Sep 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who hits the gym regularly or just starting out on your fitness journey? Have you ever wondered if doing cardio after weight lifting is beneficial or not? Well, you are not alone! There is a lot of confusion around this topic, and in this article, we will answer all your questions about cardio after weight lifting in a simple and straightforward manner.

The Pain Points of Cardio After Weight Lifting

One of the biggest concerns people have is whether doing cardio after weight lifting will hinder their muscle gains. Many people believe that doing cardio after lifting weights will lead to muscle loss, while others think it will affect their performance during weight lifting. Another concern is whether it is necessary to do cardio after weight lifting, or if it can be done on separate days.

The Target of Cardio After Weight Lifting

The target of doing cardio after weight lifting is primarily to burn fat and improve cardiovascular health. Weight lifting alone may not be enough to help you achieve your weight loss goals, and incorporating cardio can help you burn more calories, leading to weight loss. Cardio also helps improve heart health, endurance, and overall fitness.

My Personal Experience with Cardio After Weight Lifting

I have been weight lifting for a few years now and have always incorporated cardio after my weight lifting sessions. I have found that doing cardio after weight lifting has helped me burn more fat and improve my cardiovascular health. Initially, I was worried that doing cardio after weight lifting would affect my performance during weight lifting, but I found that it didn't make much of a difference. In fact, doing cardio after weight lifting helped me feel more energized and less fatigued.

Benefits of Cardio After Weight Lifting

Doing cardio after weight lifting has several benefits, including:

  • Increased fat burning
  • Improved cardiovascular health
  • Increased endurance
  • Improved recovery time

Increased Fat Burning

Doing cardio after weight lifting can help you burn more fat. When you lift weights, you burn calories, but doing cardio after weight lifting can help you burn even more calories, leading to increased fat loss.

Improved Cardiovascular Health

Cardio after weight lifting can help improve your heart health and overall fitness by increasing your heart rate and improving blood circulation. It can also help lower blood pressure and reduce the risk of heart disease.

Additional Tips for Cardio after Weight Lifting

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when doing cardio after weight lifting:

  • Start with a low-intensity cardio workout
  • Gradually increase the intensity of your cardio workout
  • Avoid doing too much cardio after weight lifting, as it can lead to fatigue and muscle loss
  • Make sure you are properly hydrated before and after your workout

Question and Answer Section

Q: Will cardio after weight lifting affect my muscle gains?

A: No, doing cardio after weight lifting will not affect your muscle gains, as long as you don't overdo it. In fact, doing cardio after weight lifting can help improve your overall fitness and help you burn more fat.

Q: Is it necessary to do cardio after weight lifting?

A: It is not necessary to do cardio after weight lifting, but it can be beneficial for weight loss and overall fitness. If you prefer to do cardio on separate days, that is also fine.

Q: How long should I do cardio after weight lifting?

A: The duration of your cardio workout will depend on your fitness level and goals. It is generally recommended to start with 10-15 minutes and gradually increase the duration over time.

Q: Can I do cardio before weight lifting?

A: Yes, you can do cardio before weight lifting, but it is recommended to start with a low-intensity cardio workout and gradually increase the intensity to avoid fatigue during weight lifting.

Conclusion of Cardio After Weight Lifting

Cardio after weight lifting can be beneficial for weight loss, improving cardiovascular health, and overall fitness. It is important to start with a low-intensity cardio workout and gradually increase the intensity over time. Doing cardio after weight lifting will not affect your muscle gains as long as you don't overdo it. Incorporate cardio into your fitness routine and see the benefits for yourself!

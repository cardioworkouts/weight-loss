Is Cardio Better Before Or After Weight Training

Written by Robby Oct 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you confused about whether to do cardio before or after weight training? Do you want to know which one is better for your fitness goals? This blog post will help you understand the benefits of both and which one is suitable for you.

Cardio and weight training are two popular exercises that offer various health benefits. Cardio exercises help in improving cardiovascular health, increasing endurance, and burning calories, while weight training helps in building muscle mass and increasing strength. But the question is, which one should you do first?

The answer to this question depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to build muscle and increase strength, then weight training should be your priority. On the other hand, if your primary goal is to lose weight and improve endurance, then cardio should come first.

In summary, both cardio and weight training are essential for overall fitness. The priority of doing one before the other depends on your fitness goals.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Weight Training

As mentioned earlier, doing cardio before weight training is suitable for those whose primary goal is to lose weight and improve endurance. When you do cardio before weight training, your body uses stored fat as fuel, which helps in burning calories and losing weight. Additionally, doing cardio before weight training helps in warming up your muscles, increasing blood flow, and reducing the risk of injury during weight training.

Personally, I have experienced the benefits of doing cardio before weight training. When I used to do weight training first, I felt fatigued and lacked energy for cardio. However, when I started doing cardio first, I felt more energized and focused during weight training.

Benefits of Doing Weight Training Before Cardio

If your primary goal is to build muscle and increase strength, then doing weight training before cardio is better. When you do weight training before cardio, your muscles are fresh, and you can lift heavier weights, which results in more muscle growth and strength gains. Additionally, doing weight training before cardio helps in depleting glycogen stores, which means that your body will use stored fat as fuel during cardio, resulting in more weight loss.

Personally, I have also experienced the benefits of doing weight training before cardio. When I used to do cardio first, I felt tired and lacked energy for weight training. However, when I started doing weight training first, I felt stronger and could lift heavier weights.

Combining Cardio and Weight Training

Another option is to combine cardio and weight training in the same workout. This method is suitable for those who have limited time for exercise or want to maximize their results. When you combine cardio and weight training, you can burn calories, build muscle, and improve endurance in one workout.

Tips for Combining Cardio and Weight Training

If you want to combine cardio and weight training, here are some tips to follow:

  1. Start with a warm-up: Begin with five to ten minutes of light cardio, such as walking or jogging, to warm up your muscles.
  2. Do weight training first: If your primary goal is to build muscle, then do weight training first.
  3. Choose the right cardio: Choose a cardio exercise that complements your weight training. For example, if you are doing upper body weight training, then choose a lower body cardio exercise, such as cycling or running.
  4. Limit cardio time: Keep cardio time limited to 20-30 minutes, so you do not interfere with your weight training.
  5. Cool down: End your workout with a five to ten minutes cool-down, such as stretching, to help your muscles recover.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it necessary to do cardio and weight training on the same day?

A: No, it is not necessary to do both exercises on the same day. You can do them on separate days, depending on your fitness goals and schedule.

Q: How long should I wait between cardio and weight training?

A: It is recommended to wait for at least 30 minutes between cardio and weight training. This will give your muscles time to recover and avoid any interference between the two exercises.

Q: How often should I do cardio and weight training?

A: It depends on your fitness goals and schedule. If you want to build muscle, then you can do weight training 2-3 times a week and cardio 1-2 times a week. If you want to lose weight, then you can do cardio 3-4 times a week and weight training 1-2 times a week.

Q: Can I do cardio and weight training on an empty stomach?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weight training on an empty stomach. However, it is recommended to have a small snack before exercise to avoid fatigue and improve performance.

Conclusion of Is Cardio Better Before or After Weight Training

Both cardio and weight training are essential for overall fitness, and the priority of doing one before the other depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight and improve endurance, then do cardio before weight training. If you want to build muscle and increase strength, then do weight training before cardio. Alternatively, you can combine both exercises in the same workout. Remember to start with a warm-up, choose the right cardio, limit cardio time, and cool down. By following these tips, you can maximize your results and achieve your fitness goals.

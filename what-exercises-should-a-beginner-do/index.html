What Exercises Should A Beginner Do

Written by Christine Jul 01, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you considering starting a fitness routine but don't know where to begin? Starting an exercise program can be overwhelming, especially if you're a beginner. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this tutorial blog post, we'll guide you through what exercises a beginner should do to kickstart their fitness journey.

Starting a fitness routine can be challenging, and it's common to feel unsure about what exercises to do. You may be worried about injuring yourself or not seeing results. That's why it's essential to start with exercises that are safe and effective for beginners.

Cardiovascular Exercises

Cardiovascular exercises, also known as cardio, are essential for beginners. These exercises will help improve your heart and lung health, increase endurance, and burn calories. Walking, jogging, swimming, cycling, and dancing are excellent examples of cardio exercises that are safe and effective for beginners.

When I started my fitness journey, I began with walking for 30 minutes a day, three times a week. As I built my endurance, I increased my time and frequency. In just a few weeks, I was able to walk for an hour a day, five times a week.

Strength Training

Strength training is another important exercise for beginners. It helps build muscle, increases bone density, and improves posture. Bodyweight exercises, such as push-ups, squats, lunges, and planks, are great for beginners. Resistance bands and dumbbells can also be used for strength training.

When I started strength training, I began with bodyweight exercises, such as squats and lunges. As I built strength, I added resistance bands to my routine. Now, I use dumbbells for my strength training workouts.

Flexibility Exercises

Flexibility exercises are essential for beginners to improve range of motion, reduce the risk of injury, and relieve muscle tension. Examples of flexibility exercises include stretching, yoga, and Pilates. These exercises can be done at home or in a class setting.

Circuit Training

Circuit training is an excellent way for beginners to get a full-body workout. Circuit training involves performing a series of exercises, one after the other, with little rest in between. This type of training improves cardiovascular fitness, builds strength, and burns calories.

When I started circuit training, I began with simple exercises, such as jumping jacks, lunges, and squats. As I built strength and endurance, I added more challenging exercises to my routine, such as burpees and mountain climbers.

Conclusion of What Exercises Should a Beginner Do

Starting a fitness routine can be challenging, but it's essential to start with exercises that are safe and effective for beginners. Cardiovascular exercises, strength training, flexibility exercises, and circuit training are excellent options for beginners. Remember to start slow and gradually increase intensity and frequency. With consistency and dedication, you'll reach your fitness goals in no time!

Question and Answer

Q: How often should a beginner exercise?

A: It's recommended that beginners exercise at least three days a week, with a rest day in between each workout. As you build endurance and strength, you can increase the frequency and duration of your workouts.

Q: Should beginners use weights for strength training?

A: Bodyweight exercises are an excellent place to start for beginners. As you build strength, you can gradually add weights to your routine. It's important to use proper form and start with light weights to avoid injury.

Q: Can beginners do high-intensity interval training (HIIT)?

A: HIIT can be challenging for beginners, and it's essential to start with low to moderate intensity exercises. As you build endurance and strength, you can gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.

Q: Is it necessary to stretch before and after exercise?

A: Yes, stretching is essential before and after exercise to prevent injury and improve flexibility. Dynamic stretching before exercise and static stretching after exercise are recommended.

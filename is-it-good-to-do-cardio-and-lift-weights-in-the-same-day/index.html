Is It Good To Do Cardio And Lift Weights In The Same Day

Written by Oliver Oct 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who loves to hit the gym and get a good workout in? Maybe you’re wondering if it’s a good idea to do cardio and lift weights in the same day. This is a common question that many fitness enthusiasts ask. In this blog post, we’ll discuss whether or not it’s good to do cardio and lift weights in the same day.

When it comes to working out, everyone has their own preferences and goals. However, many people find it challenging to balance cardio and weightlifting in their routines. Doing both in the same day can be tough, but it’s not impossible. It’s important to consider your fitness level, goals, and overall health before deciding on a workout routine.

The short answer is yes, it’s possible to do cardio and lift weights in the same day. In fact, combining both types of exercise can lead to better overall fitness and health. Cardio and weightlifting both offer unique benefits, and incorporating them both into your routine can help you achieve a well-rounded workout.

Cardio is great for improving your cardiovascular health, which can reduce your risk of heart disease, stroke, and other illnesses. It also helps you burn calories and lose weight. Weightlifting, on the other hand, is great for building muscle mass and increasing strength. It can also help you burn calories and lose weight, as muscle burns more calories than fat.

So, how can you combine both types of exercise in the same day? One option is to split your workout into two separate sessions, with cardio in the morning and weightlifting in the afternoon or evening. Another option is to do a combination of both types of exercise in one session. For example, you could start with cardio to warm up, then move on to weightlifting, and finish with a cool-down cardio session.

My Personal Experience

As a personal trainer, I often recommend my clients to combine cardio and weightlifting in their workout routines. One of my clients, Sarah, was hesitant to try this approach at first. She was worried that she wouldn’t have enough energy to complete both types of exercise in the same day. However, after trying it out for a few weeks, she noticed a significant improvement in her fitness level and overall health. She was able to lose weight, build muscle, and improve her cardiovascular health all at the same time.

The Benefits of Combining Cardio and Weightlifting

There are many benefits to combining cardio and weightlifting in your workout routine. Here are a few:

Burn More Calories

Combining cardio and weightlifting can help you burn more calories than doing either type of exercise alone. Cardio burns calories during the workout, while weightlifting burns calories after the workout as your body repairs and builds muscle.

Improve Overall Fitness

Doing both cardio and weightlifting can help you achieve better overall fitness, as you’ll be working different muscle groups and improving your cardiovascular health at the same time.

Save Time

Combining both types of exercise in the same day can save you time, as you won’t have to schedule separate workouts for each type of exercise.

Tips for Doing Cardio and Weightlifting in the Same Day

If you’re planning on combining cardio and weightlifting in the same day, here are a few tips:

- Start with a good warm-up to prevent injury

- Focus on your form during weightlifting exercises

- Stay hydrated throughout your workout

- Give your body time to recover between workouts

Common Questions About Doing Cardio and Weightlifting in the Same Day

Q: Is it better to do cardio or weightlifting first?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If you’re looking to build muscle, you may want to do weightlifting first. If you’re looking to improve your cardiovascular health, you may want to do cardio first.

Q: How long should I wait between cardio and weightlifting?

A: It’s recommended that you wait at least 30 minutes between cardio and weightlifting, to give your body time to recover.

Q: How often should I do cardio and weightlifting in the same day?

A: It’s recommended that you do cardio and weightlifting in the same day 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between.

Q: Can I do cardio and weightlifting on the same day if I’m a beginner?

A: Yes, but it’s important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. It’s also important to consult with a doctor or personal trainer before starting a new workout routine.

Conclusion

Overall, combining cardio and weightlifting in the same day can be a great way to achieve better overall fitness and health. It’s important to consider your fitness level, goals, and overall health before deciding on a workout routine. With the right approach and mindset, you can successfully combine both types of exercise in your workout routine.

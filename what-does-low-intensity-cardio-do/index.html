What Does Low Intensity Cardio Do

Written by Oliver Aug 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who is looking to improve your overall health and fitness? Perhaps you are wondering what type of exercise would be best for you. If you are looking for a low-impact form of exercise that can still provide great health benefits, then low intensity cardio might be just what you need.

Many people struggle with finding the right exercise routine that fits their lifestyle and physical abilities. Some people may have joint pain, while others may not have enough time to commit to lengthy workouts. Low intensity cardio is a great option for people who want to improve their health without putting too much strain on their body.

Low intensity cardio is a form of exercise that involves performing physical activity at a lower intensity level, typically at a heart rate of 50-70% of your maximum heart rate. This type of exercise can include activities such as walking, cycling, swimming, or using an elliptical machine.

In summary, low intensity cardio is a gentle form of exercise that can provide numerous health benefits, such as improved cardiovascular health, increased endurance, and weight loss. This type of exercise is great for people who are just starting out on their fitness journey, or for those who want to maintain their current level of fitness without causing too much strain on their body.

Benefits of Low Intensity Cardio

As someone who has struggled with joint pain and physical limitations, I have found that low intensity cardio has been a great way to improve my health and fitness without causing too much strain on my body. This type of exercise has allowed me to build my endurance and strength gradually, all while improving my cardiovascular health and mental well-being.

When you engage in low intensity cardio, your body uses fat as its primary source of fuel. This means that you can burn more calories during your workout, which can lead to weight loss over time. Additionally, low intensity cardio can help to improve your mood and mental health, as it releases endorphins that can help to reduce stress and anxiety.

How to Incorporate Low Intensity Cardio into Your Routine

If you are interested in incorporating low intensity cardio into your exercise routine, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it is important to start slow and gradually build up your endurance and strength over time. You can start by walking for 15-20 minutes a day, and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workouts as your fitness level improves.

It is also important to choose activities that you enjoy and that fit your physical abilities. For example, if you have joint pain, you may want to choose low-impact activities such as swimming or using an elliptical machine. Additionally, it is important to incorporate strength training into your routine to help build muscle and improve your overall fitness level.

FAQs About Low Intensity Cardio

Q: Is low intensity cardio effective for weight loss?

A: Yes, low intensity cardio can be an effective way to lose weight over time, as it helps to burn calories and improve your metabolic rate.

Q: Can low intensity cardio improve my cardiovascular health?

A: Yes, low intensity cardio can help to improve your cardiovascular health by strengthening your heart and lungs, and improving your overall endurance.

Q: How often should I engage in low intensity cardio?

A: It is recommended that adults engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week. You can break this up into shorter sessions throughout the week to make it more manageable.

Q: Can low intensity cardio be done at home?

A: Yes, there are many low intensity cardio exercises that can be done at home, such as walking, cycling on a stationary bike, or using an elliptical machine.

Conclusion of What Does Low Intensity Cardio Do

Overall, low intensity cardio is a great way to improve your health and fitness without putting too much strain on your body. Whether you are just starting out on your fitness journey or are looking to maintain your current level of fitness, low intensity cardio can provide numerous benefits such as improved cardiovascular health, increased endurance, and weight loss. By incorporating low intensity cardio into your exercise routine, you can improve your overall health and well-being in a safe and sustainable way.

Simple Beginner Workouts A Guide For Getting Started

Written by Robby Jun 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you new to working out and not sure where to start? Do you find the idea of exercising overwhelming and intimidating? Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this guide, we will walk you through some simple beginner workouts that can help you get started on your fitness journey.

Starting a new workout routine can be challenging, especially if you haven't exercised in a while. It's easy to get discouraged when you don't see results right away or when you feel like you're not making progress. But don't give up! With the right attitude and approach, you can build a sustainable workout habit that will help you achieve your fitness goals.

The first step in any workout routine is to determine your goals. Do you want to lose weight? Build muscle? Improve your overall health and fitness? Once you have a clear idea of what you want to achieve, you can start to choose the right exercises to help you get there.

In this guide, we will cover some simple beginner workouts that target different areas of the body and can help you achieve a variety of fitness goals. These workouts are designed to be easy to follow and require little to no equipment, making them perfect for beginners.

Bodyweight Exercises

Bodyweight exercises are a great way to build strength and endurance without the need for any equipment. These exercises use your own body weight as resistance, making them easy to do at home or on the go. Some examples of bodyweight exercises include:

Push-ups: This classic exercise targets your chest, shoulders, and triceps. Start in a plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart. Lower your body until your chest touches the ground, then push back up to the starting position.

Squats: Squats target your legs, glutes, and core. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, then bend your knees and lower your body as if you're sitting in a chair. Keep your chest up and your weight in your heels.

Plank: Planks are a great way to build core strength. Start in a push-up position with your hands directly under your shoulders. Lower your forearms to the ground and hold for as long as you can.

Cardio Workouts

Cardiovascular exercise is important for improving your overall health and fitness. These workouts get your heart rate up and help you burn calories. Some examples of cardio workouts include:

Walking: Walking is a low-impact exercise that is easy to do anywhere. Start with a 10-15 minute walk and gradually increase your time as you get more comfortable.

Jogging: Jogging is a higher-intensity form of cardio that can help you burn more calories. Start with short intervals of jogging and walking, then gradually increase your jogging time as your fitness improves.

Jumping jacks: Jumping jacks are a fun and effective way to get your heart rate up. Stand with your feet together, then jump your feet out and raise your arms overhead. Jump your feet back together and lower your arms to your sides.

Strength Training

Strength training is important for building muscle and improving your overall strength and fitness. These workouts use weights or other forms of resistance to challenge your muscles. Some examples of strength training exercises include:

Dumbbell curls: Dumbbell curls target your biceps. Hold a dumbbell in each hand and curl the weights up towards your shoulders, then lower them back down.

Lunges: Lunges target your legs and glutes. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, then take a big step forward with one foot. Lower your body until your back knee almost touches the ground, then push back up to the starting position.

Shoulder press: Shoulder presses target your shoulders and triceps. Hold a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height, then press the weights overhead. Lower them back down to shoulder height to complete one rep.

Conclusion of Simple Beginner Workouts

Starting a new workout routine can be intimidating, but it doesn't have to be. By starting with simple beginner workouts, you can build a foundation of strength and fitness that will help you achieve your goals in the long run. Remember to start slow, listen to your body, and be patient with yourself. With time and consistency, you'll be amazed at what you can achieve!

Question and Answer

Q: Do I need any equipment for these workouts?

A: Most of these workouts can be done with little to no equipment. Bodyweight exercises require no equipment, while strength training exercises can be done with dumbbells or other forms of resistance.

Q: How often should I do these workouts?

A: Aim to do these workouts at least three times a week, with rest days in between. As you get more comfortable, you can increase the frequency and intensity of your workouts.

Q: How long should I do each workout?

A: Aim to do each workout for 20-30 minutes, or until you feel fatigued. As your fitness improves, you can increase the duration and intensity of your workouts.

Q: Can I combine these workouts with other forms of exercise?

A: Absolutely! These workouts can be combined with other forms of exercise, such as yoga or Pilates, to create a well-rounded fitness routine.

Exercise Cardio Or Strength First Which Is Better

Written by Daniel Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you confused about whether you should do cardio or strength training first during your workout? If yes, then you are not alone. Many people have a hard time deciding which type of exercise they should start with. In this blog post, we will discuss the benefits and drawbacks of both cardio and strength training and help you decide which one is best for you.

Pain Points of Cardio or Strength First

One of the main pain points of deciding between cardio or strength training first is that it can be overwhelming to choose one. Both types of exercise have their own benefits, and it's hard to determine which type of exercise will benefit your body more. Additionally, some people may be more comfortable with one type of exercise over the other, making it hard for them to switch it up.

What is Cardio or Strength First?

Cardio and strength training are both essential components of a well-rounded exercise routine. Cardio exercises are primarily focused on increasing your heart rate, while strength training exercises are focused on building muscle. When it comes to deciding which type of exercise to do first, it ultimately depends on your fitness goals.

Benefits and Drawbacks of Cardio or Strength First

Cardio exercises, such as running, cycling, or swimming, are great for improving your cardiovascular health, burning calories, and reducing stress. Doing cardio before strength training can help you warm up your muscles and prepare your body for more intense exercises. However, if you do too much cardio before strength training, you may become too fatigued to perform your strength exercises effectively.

Strength training exercises, such as weightlifting or bodyweight exercises, are great for building muscle, improving your bone density, and increasing your metabolism. Doing strength training exercises before cardio can help you burn more calories during your cardio workout. However, if you do too much strength training before cardio, you may not have enough energy to perform your cardio exercises effectively.

My Personal Experience with Cardio or Strength First

Personally, I prefer to do strength training exercises before cardio. I find that doing strength training exercises first helps me build up my energy levels and prepare my body for cardio exercises. Additionally, I feel like I can push myself more during my cardio workout if I have already completed my strength training exercises.

My Personal Experience with Cardio or Strength First

On the other hand, some people prefer to do cardio exercises before strength training. They feel like doing cardio first helps them warm up their muscles and get their heart rate up, making it easier for them to perform their strength training exercises. Additionally, doing cardio first can help people burn more calories during their workout.

Tips for Cardio or Strength First

If you're still unsure about whether you should do cardio or strength training first, try experimenting with both and see which one works best for you. Additionally, make sure to warm up your muscles before starting your workout, regardless of which type of exercise you decide to do first.

How to Incorporate Cardio or Strength First into Your Workout Routine

If you want to incorporate both cardio and strength training into your workout routine, try doing them on separate days. This will give your body time to recover between workouts and help you avoid overtraining. Alternatively, you can do one type of exercise in the morning and the other in the evening.

FAQs About Cardio or Strength First

Q: Is it better to do cardio or strength training first?

A: It ultimately depends on your fitness goals. If you want to improve your cardiovascular health, it's best to do cardio first. If you want to build muscle, it's best to do strength training first.

Q: How long should I do cardio or strength training for?

A: It's best to aim for at least 30 minutes of exercise per day. However, the amount of time you should do cardio or strength training for depends on your fitness level and goals.

Q: Can I do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and strength training on the same day. Just make sure to warm up your muscles before starting your workout and give your body time to recover between exercises.

Q: How often should I do cardio or strength training?

A: It's best to aim for at least 3-4 days of cardio or strength training per week. However, the amount of exercise you should do depends on your fitness level and goals.

Conclusion of Exercise Cardio or Strength First

Deciding whether to do cardio or strength training first can be challenging. Ultimately, it depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. Try experimenting with both and see which one works best for you. Remember to warm up your muscles before starting your workout and give your body time to recover between exercises.

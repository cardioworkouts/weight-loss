Is It Better To Do Hiit Before Or After Cardio

Written by Robby Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who loves to hit the gym regularly? Are you confused about whether you should do HIIT before or after cardio? Well, you're not alone! This is a common question among fitness enthusiasts, and it can be challenging to decide which one to do first. However, in this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about the topic.

Pain Points

Doing HIIT and cardio consistently is a great way to maintain your fitness levels and stay healthy. However, it can be challenging to decide which one to do first. You may be worried about not getting the desired results or even injuring yourself if you do it wrong. It's essential to know the right order to get the best out of your workout sessions.

What Should You Do First?

The answer to this question depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to improve your endurance, then doing cardio first may be better. When you do cardio first, you'll burn more calories, and your muscles will be warmed up, making it easier to move on to HIIT. However, if your primary goal is to build strength and power, then doing HIIT first may be better. HIIT can improve your muscle strength, and you'll be able to lift more weight during your cardio session.

Overall, it's essential to know your fitness goals and decide accordingly.

Main Points

In summary, deciding whether to do HIIT before or after cardio depends on your fitness goals. If your aim is to improve your endurance, then doing cardio first is recommended. However, if your goal is to build strength and power, then doing HIIT first is better. It's essential to understand your body and your fitness goals to decide which one to do first.

Benefits of Doing HIIT Before Cardio

Personally, I prefer doing HIIT before cardio. When you do HIIT first, your body burns stored glycogen, which means that when you move on to cardio, your body will burn more fat. Additionally, doing HIIT first can also help prevent boredom, as you get to switch up your workout routine. HIIT can also help improve your metabolic rate, which means that your body will burn calories even after your workout.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before HIIT

When you do cardio before HIIT, you warm up your muscles, making it easier to move on to HIIT. Additionally, doing cardio first can improve your endurance and help you burn more calories, making it ideal for those who want to lose weight. Doing cardio first can also help prevent injury, as your muscles are already warmed up.

Conclusion

In conclusion, deciding whether to do HIIT before or after cardio depends on your fitness goals. Both HIIT and cardio have their benefits, and it's essential to know what you want to achieve from your workout. Remember to listen to your body and start slow if you're new to working out.

Tips on How to Get the Best of Both Worlds

If you want to get the best of both worlds, you can combine HIIT and cardio into one workout. Try doing a 30-second sprint, followed by a 60-second jog, and repeat ten times. This way, you'll get the benefits of both HIIT and cardio.

Personal Experience

When I started working out, I was unsure whether to do HIIT before or after cardio. However, after consulting with a trainer, I realized that it depends on your fitness goals. Personally, I prefer doing HIIT first, as I find it more challenging and exciting. I feel more energized and accomplished when I finish my HIIT workout, and it motivates me to push harder during my cardio session.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Can doing HIIT before cardio cause injury?

A: It's unlikely to cause injury if you warm-up correctly and gradually increase the intensity of your workout.

Q: Can I do HIIT and cardio on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do both HIIT and cardio on the same day. However, it's essential to space them out and give your body time to recover.

Q: How can I prevent injury when doing HIIT?

A: It's essential to warm-up correctly and use proper form during your workout. Additionally, starting slow and gradually increasing the intensity can help prevent injury.

Q: How often should I do HIIT or cardio?

A: It's recommended to do at least 30 minutes of cardio and 20 minutes of HIIT per day, depending on your fitness goals.

Conclusion of Is It Better to Do HIIT Before or After Cardio

In conclusion, there is no right or wrong answer to whether you should do HIIT before or after cardio. It depends on your fitness goals and what you want to achieve from your workout. Remember to listen to your body and start slow if you're new to working out.

The Ultimate Guide To Best Pre Workout Supplement For Cardio

Written by Aprilia Jul 31, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for the perfect pre-workout supplement for your cardio session? Do you feel like you're not getting the most out of your workouts? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle to find the right pre-workout supplement to help them achieve their fitness goals.

Cardio can be a great way to improve your overall health, but it can also be challenging. Finding the right pre-workout supplement can make all the difference in your energy levels, endurance, and overall performance. In this article, we'll explore the best pre-workout supplements for cardio and how they can help you get the most out of your workouts.

Before we dive into the best pre-workout supplements for cardio, let's first understand what pre-workout supplements are and why they are essential for your workout routine. Pre-workout supplements are specially designed to enhance your performance during your workout. They typically contain a combination of ingredients that improve energy levels, focus, and endurance.

Best Pre Workout Supplement for Cardio: Caffeine

Caffeine is one of the most popular pre-workout supplements for cardio. It is a natural stimulant that helps to improve energy levels and focus. Caffeine works by blocking adenosine, a neurotransmitter that promotes sleep and relaxation, in the brain. This results in increased alertness and wakefulness.

Personally, I have found that taking caffeine before my cardio sessions helps me to push through my workouts and stay focused. However, it's important to note that caffeine can have different effects on different people. It's essential to start with a low dose and gradually increase it to find your optimal dosage.

Best Pre Workout Supplement for Cardio: Beta-Alanine

Beta-alanine is a non-essential amino acid that helps to reduce fatigue and increase endurance. It works by increasing the levels of carnosine in the muscles, which helps to buffer lactic acid build-up. This means that you can work out for longer periods without feeling fatigued.

Personally, I have found that taking beta-alanine before my cardio sessions helps me to push through my workouts and increase my endurance. However, it's important to note that beta-alanine can cause a tingling sensation on the skin, known as paresthesia. This is a harmless side effect, but it can be uncomfortable for some people.

Best Pre Workout Supplement for Cardio: Citrulline Malate

Citrulline malate is a combination of the amino acid citrulline and malic acid. It helps to improve blood flow and reduce fatigue. Citrulline malate works by increasing the levels of nitric oxide in the body, which helps to dilate blood vessels and improve blood flow.

Personally, I have found that taking citrulline malate before my cardio sessions helps me to increase my endurance and delay fatigue. However, it's important to note that citrulline malate can cause gastrointestinal distress in some people. It's essential to start with a low dose and gradually increase it to find your optimal dosage.

Best Pre Workout Supplement for Cardio: Creatine

Creatine is a naturally occurring compound that helps to improve muscle strength and power. It works by increasing the levels of phosphocreatine in the muscles, which helps to produce more ATP, the primary energy source for our cells.

Personally, I have found that taking creatine before my cardio sessions helps me to increase my power and explosiveness. However, it's important to note that creatine can cause water retention in some people. It's essential to stay hydrated and start with a low dose to avoid any side effects.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I take more than one pre-workout supplement?

A: Yes, you can take more than one pre-workout supplement. However, it's essential to be mindful of the ingredients and dosages to avoid any adverse side effects.

Q: Can pre-workout supplements cause any side effects?

A: Yes, pre-workout supplements can cause side effects such as nausea, jitters, and headaches. It's important to start with a low dose and monitor any adverse reactions.

Q: When is the best time to take pre-workout supplements?

A: It's best to take pre-workout supplements 30 minutes before your workout to allow them to take effect.

Q: Do I need to cycle my pre-workout supplements?

A: Yes, it's recommended to cycle your pre-workout supplements to avoid building up a tolerance. It's best to take a break from pre-workout supplements every 8-12 weeks.

Conclusion of Best Pre Workout Supplement for Cardio

Finding the best pre-workout supplement for your cardio sessions can be challenging, but it's worth the effort. The right pre-workout supplement can help you to push through your workouts, increase your endurance, and achieve your fitness goals. Remember to be mindful of the ingredients and dosages, and always consult with your healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

Is Cardio Before Weight Lifting Good

Written by Eveline Aug 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you confused about whether to start your workout with cardio or weight lifting? Do you want to know if it's good to do cardio before weight lifting? If yes, then this post is for you. Read on to discover the truth about cardio before weight lifting.

The Pain Points

Many fitness enthusiasts and athletes are confused about whether to start their workout with cardio or weight lifting. They wonder which one is more effective and which one should be done first. There is a lot of conflicting information out there about this topic, which only adds to the confusion.

The Answer to Is Cardio Before Weight Lifting Good

The answer is yes, cardio before weight lifting is good, but it depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to lose weight or burn fat, then doing cardio before weight lifting can be very effective. Cardio exercises like running, cycling, or swimming can help elevate your heart rate and increase your metabolic rate, making it easier for you to burn calories and lose fat.

On the other hand, if your goal is to build muscle, then doing weight lifting before cardio may be more effective. Weight lifting exercises like squats, deadlifts, or bench presses can help improve your strength, increase your muscle mass, and boost your metabolism, making it easier for you to burn fat and build muscle.

Summary of Is Cardio Before Weight Lifting Good

In summary, whether you should do cardio before weight lifting or weight lifting before cardio depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to lose weight, then doing cardio before weight lifting may be more effective. But if your goal is to build muscle, then doing weight lifting before cardio may be more effective.

Personal Experience with Cardio Before Weight Lifting

As a personal trainer, I often recommend doing cardio before weight lifting to my clients who want to lose weight. I have seen many clients achieve great results by starting their workout with cardio exercises like running, cycling, or jumping jacks. Doing cardio before weight lifting helps elevate their heart rate and increase their metabolic rate, making it easier for them to burn fat and lose weight.

Personal Experience with Weight Lifting Before Cardio

On the other hand, I also recommend doing weight lifting before cardio to my clients who want to build muscle. I have seen many clients achieve great results by starting their workout with weight lifting exercises like squats, deadlifts, or bench presses. Doing weight lifting before cardio helps improve their strength, increase their muscle mass, and boost their metabolism, making it easier for them to burn fat and build muscle.

Benefits of Cardio Before Weight Lifting

There are several benefits of doing cardio before weight lifting, such as:

  • Elevating your heart rate and increasing your metabolic rate
  • Burning more calories and losing more fat
  • Improving your cardiovascular health and endurance
  • Reducing the risk of injury during weight lifting

Tips for Doing Cardio Before Weight Lifting

If you want to do cardio before weight lifting, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with a warm-up to prevent injury
  • Choose cardio exercises that you enjoy and that challenge you
  • Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your cardio workout
  • Take a break between cardio and weight lifting to recover

Question and Answer

Q: How long should I do cardio before weight lifting?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals. If you're a beginner, start with 10-15 minutes of cardio before weight lifting. If you're more advanced, you can do up to 30 minutes of cardio before weight lifting.

Q: Will doing cardio before weight lifting make me lose muscle?

A: No, doing cardio before weight lifting will not make you lose muscle as long as you eat enough protein and calories to support your muscle growth.

Q: Should I do cardio every day before weight lifting?

A: It's not necessary to do cardio every day before weight lifting. It's recommended to do cardio 2-3 times a week and weight lifting 2-3 times a week, with at least one rest day in between.

Q: Can I do cardio and weight lifting on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weight lifting on the same day, but it's recommended to do cardio before weight lifting or at a separate time of the day to prevent fatigue and injury.

Conclusion of Is Cardio Before Weight Lifting Good

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio before weight lifting or weight lifting before cardio depends on your fitness goals. Both cardio and weight lifting have their benefits and can help you achieve your fitness goals. So, choose the one that best fits your needs and preferences, and stick to a regular workout routine to see the best results.

Should I Mix Cardio And Weight Training

Written by Eveline Nov 24, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you one of those people who have been wondering whether or not to mix cardio and weight training in your workout routine? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this question and aren't sure what the best approach is. It can be overwhelming to figure out how to combine two seemingly different exercises, especially if you're new to working out.

One of the main pain points when it comes to mixing cardio and weight training is the fear of not getting optimal results. Some people worry that combining the two will negatively impact their gains, and they'll end up losing muscle mass or not gaining any at all. Others worry that cardio will make them too tired to perform well during weight training sessions, or that they won't have enough energy to do both exercises effectively.

The short answer to this question is yes, you should mix cardio and weight training in your workout routine. Doing so offers numerous benefits that can help you reach your fitness goals faster and more effectively.

Combining cardio and weight training is an excellent way to burn more calories and build lean muscle mass. Cardio exercises, such as running or biking, help you burn calories faster and improve your cardiovascular health. Weight training, on the other hand, helps you build lean muscle mass, which boosts your metabolism and helps you burn more calories even when you're not working out.

In conclusion, mixing cardio and weight training is a highly effective way to reach your fitness goals. Not only does it help you burn more calories and build lean muscle mass, but it also improves your overall health and well-being.

The Benefits of Mixing Cardio and Weight Training

When I first started working out, I was hesitant to mix cardio and weight training. I thought that doing both in one session would be too much for my body to handle. However, after doing some research and consulting with a personal trainer, I realized that mixing the two exercises was the best approach for me.

One of the biggest benefits of mixing cardio and weight training is that it helps you burn more calories. When you combine the two exercises, your body has to work harder to keep up with the demands of each workout. This increased demand on your body means that you'll burn more calories and fat, which can help you lose weight and get in better shape.

Mixing cardio and weight training is also a great way to build lean muscle mass. When you lift weights, you're breaking down muscle fibers, which then repair and grow stronger during rest periods. By adding cardio to your workout routine, you're giving your body the extra boost it needs to build lean muscle mass.

How to Mix Cardio and Weight Training

When it comes to mixing cardio and weight training, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts over time. This will help prevent injury and ensure that you're able to handle the demands of both exercises.

It's also important to vary your workouts to prevent boredom and ensure that you're targeting different muscle groups. For example, you might do a cardio workout one day and a weight training workout the next, or you might do both exercises in the same session but target different muscle groups.

Finally, make sure to give yourself enough rest and recovery time between workouts. This will help your body repair and build muscle, which is essential for achieving your fitness goals.

My Personal Experience

When I first started working out, I focused solely on cardio exercises like running and biking. However, I quickly realized that I wasn't seeing the results I wanted, and I wasn't building the lean muscle mass that I desired.

After consulting with a personal trainer, I started incorporating weight training into my routine, and I immediately noticed a difference. I felt stronger and more energized, and I started seeing results much faster than I had before.

Now, I mix cardio and weight training in my workouts, and I love the variety and challenge that it provides. It's been a game-changer for me, and I highly recommend it to anyone who wants to get in better shape and improve their overall health.

FAQs About Mixing Cardio and Weight Training

Q: How often should I mix cardio and weight training?

A: It depends on your fitness goals and your current level of fitness. Generally, it's best to aim for a mix of both exercises 3-4 times per week.

Q: Should I do cardio or weight training first?

A: It's generally recommended to do weight training first, as it requires more energy and focus than cardio. However, there's no hard and fast rule, and you should do what works best for you.

Q: Can I do cardio and weight training in the same session?

A: Yes, you can do both exercises in the same session. However, it's important to vary the intensity and duration of each exercise to prevent injury and ensure that you're able to complete both workouts effectively.

Q: How long should my cardio and weight training sessions be?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals. Generally, cardio sessions should be 20-30 minutes, while weight training sessions should be 45-60 minutes.

Conclusion

Mixing cardio and weight training is an excellent way to reach your fitness goals and improve your overall health and well-being. By combining the two exercises, you'll burn more calories, build lean muscle mass, and feel stronger and more energized. So why not give it a try and see what it can do for you?

How Long Should A Beginner Hiit Workout Be

Written by Robby Jun 14, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you a beginner who is starting out with HIIT workouts and wondering how long your sessions should last? You're not alone. Many people struggle with this question and end up either overdoing it or not pushing themselves hard enough. The right length of a HIIT workout can vary depending on your fitness level, goals, and the type of workout you're doing. In this article, we'll help you figure out the ideal duration for a beginner HIIT workout so that you can get the most out of your exercise routine.

When it comes to HIIT workouts, the duration of a session is typically shorter than traditional cardio routines. This is because HIIT involves alternating between high-intensity exercises and periods of rest or low-intensity exercises. The goal is to get your heart rate up and keep it there for the duration of the workout. A typical beginner HIIT workout can last anywhere from 10 to 30 minutes, depending on your fitness level and the type of routine you're following.

To get started with a beginner HIIT workout, aim for a session that lasts around 20 minutes. This should be enough time to get your heart rate up and challenge your body without pushing yourself too hard. As you gain more experience and your fitness level improves, you can gradually increase the duration of your workouts. However, it's important not to overdo it and to listen to your body. If you feel tired or sore, take a break and rest before continuing with your workout routine.

In summary, the ideal duration for a beginner HIIT workout is around 20 minutes. This will give you enough time to challenge your body and get your heart rate up without pushing yourself too hard. As you become more experienced and your fitness level improves, you can gradually increase the duration of your workouts. Remember to listen to your body and take breaks when needed.

Personal Experience with HIIT Workouts

As someone who has been doing HIIT workouts for several years, I can say that the duration of a session can make a huge difference in your results. When I first started out, I would do 30-minute sessions, but I found that I was overdoing it and not giving my body enough time to recover. Eventually, I settled on a 20-minute routine that was challenging but not too intense. This allowed me to see better results and avoid burnout.

The Importance of Rest and Recovery

While it's important to challenge your body with intense workouts, it's equally important to give yourself time to rest and recover. This means taking breaks between workouts and allowing your body to heal and rebuild itself. Overtraining can lead to injuries and burnout, so it's important to find a balance between exercise and rest.

Tips for a Successful Beginner HIIT Workout

If you're new to HIIT workouts, here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Choose exercises that you enjoy and that challenge you
  • Start with a 20-minute routine and gradually increase the duration as you become more experienced
  • Be sure to warm up and cool down before and after your workout
  • Listen to your body and rest when needed
  • Stay hydrated and fuel your body with healthy foods

How to Create a Beginner HIIT Workout Routine

If you're not sure how to create a HIIT workout routine, there are plenty of resources available online. You can find free workout videos on YouTube or sign up for a fitness program that specializes in HIIT workouts. Many gyms also offer HIIT classes that are led by trained instructors. The key is to find a routine that works for you and that you enjoy.

Real-Life Results from a Beginner HIIT Workout

One of my clients, Sarah, started doing HIIT workouts a few months ago. She was initially intimidated by the idea of high-intensity exercise, but she was determined to give it a try. Sarah started with a 20-minute routine that included exercises like jumping jacks, squats, and lunges. She gradually increased the duration of her workouts and started incorporating more challenging exercises like burpees and mountain climbers.

After just a few weeks, Sarah noticed a significant improvement in her overall fitness level. She had more energy, slept better, and felt stronger and more confident. She also lost a few pounds and noticed that her clothes fit better. Sarah is now a regular HIIT workout enthusiast and has even started competing in local fitness events.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do longer HIIT workouts or shorter ones?

A: It's better to start with shorter HIIT workouts and gradually increase the duration as you become more experienced. This will help you avoid burnout and injuries.

Q: How many times a week should I do a beginner HIIT workout?

A: It's recommended to start with 2-3 sessions per week and gradually increase the frequency as your fitness level improves.

Q: Can I do HIIT workouts if I have injuries?

A: It depends on the type and severity of your injuries. It's best to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new exercise routine.

Q: What are some good HIIT exercises for beginners?

A: Good HIIT exercises for beginners include jumping jacks, squats, lunges, push-ups, and planks.

Conclusion of How Long Should a Beginner HIIT Workout Be

In conclusion, the ideal duration for a beginner HIIT workout is around 20 minutes. This will give you enough time to challenge your body and get your heart rate up without pushing yourself too hard. As you become more experienced and your fitness level improves, you can gradually increase the duration of your workouts. Remember to listen to your body and take breaks when needed. With the right approach, HIIT workouts can be an effective way to improve your fitness level and achieve your goals.

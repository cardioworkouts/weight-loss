60 Tips What To Eat After Cardio Workout For Beginner

Written by Bardi Sep 12, 2023 · 3 min read

What you eat after a cardio workout can be just as important as the workout itself. Your body needs certain nutrients to recover and replenish after a workout. In this tutorial, we'll discuss what to eat after cardio workout and why it's important.

After a cardio workout, you may feel tired, hungry, and thirsty. These are all common pain points that can be alleviated by eating the right foods. It's important to refuel your body with the right nutrients to aid in muscle recovery and prevent muscle breakdown.

What to Eat After Cardio Workout

Protein and carbohydrates are essential after a cardio workout. Protein helps repair and build muscle while carbohydrates replenish glycogen stores that were depleted during the workout. Good sources of protein include chicken, fish, lean beef, eggs, and plant-based options such as tofu and beans. Good sources of carbohydrates include fruits, vegetables, rice, pasta, and bread.

In addition to protein and carbohydrates, it's important to stay hydrated after a cardio workout. Water is the best option, but sports drinks can also be beneficial as they contain electrolytes that were lost during the workout.

Personal Experience

After a long run, I always make sure to refuel with a protein shake and a banana. The protein shake provides the necessary protein to aid in muscle recovery while the banana replenishes my glycogen stores. I also make sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Meal Ideas

Here are some meal ideas that are perfect after a cardio workout:

  • Grilled chicken with roasted vegetables and brown rice
  • Salmon with sweet potato and asparagus
  • Tofu stir-fry with brown rice and mixed vegetables
  • Whole wheat pasta with lean ground beef and tomato sauce

Importance of Timing

The timing of your post-workout meal is also important. It's best to eat within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout to maximize muscle recovery and replenish glycogen stores.

Avoiding Overeating

While it's important to refuel after a cardio workout, it's also important to avoid overeating. It's easy to overestimate the number of calories burned during a workout and consume more calories than necessary. Stick to a balanced meal with the right amount of protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats.

Personal Experience

After a spin class, I like to refuel with a smoothie bowl. I blend together frozen fruit, protein powder, almond milk, and spinach. I then top it with granola and sliced almonds for some healthy fats. It's the perfect balance of protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats.

Question and Answer

Q:

What if I don't have time to eat after my workout?

A:

If you don't have time to eat a meal after your workout, try a protein shake or a protein bar. These are quick and easy options that can be consumed on-the-go.

Q:

Can I eat junk food after a cardio workout?

A:

While it's tempting to indulge in junk food after a workout, it's not the best option. Junk food doesn't provide the necessary nutrients for muscle recovery and can actually hinder progress.

Q:

Can I drink alcohol after a cardio workout?

A:

Alcohol should be avoided after a cardio workout. It can cause dehydration and hinder muscle recovery.

Q:

What if I'm not hungry after my workout?

A:

If you're not hungry after your workout, try a protein shake or a small snack such as a piece of fruit. It's important to refuel your body even if you don't feel hungry.

Conclusion of What to Eat After Cardio Workout

Eating the right foods after a cardio workout is essential for muscle recovery and replenishing glycogen stores. Stick to a balanced meal with the right amount of protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats. Remember to stay hydrated and refuel within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout. Avoid junk food and alcohol and don't skip your post-workout meal even if you're not hungry.

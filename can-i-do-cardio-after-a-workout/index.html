Can I Do Cardio After A Workout

Written by Eveline Aug 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who hits the gym regularly and is confused about whether you can do cardio after a workout? Many people have this question, and it's understandable because there are a lot of myths surrounding this topic. In this blog post, we'll clear up the confusion and give you a definitive answer to whether you can do cardio after a workout or not.

Pain Points Related to Can I Do Cardio After a Workout

Most people who hit the gym regularly have this question in their minds. They are concerned about whether doing cardio after a workout will affect their gains or not. Some are even worried about overexerting themselves and causing injuries. Others are confused about the optimal time to do cardio.

Answer to Can I Do Cardio After a Workout

The answer to whether you can do cardio after a workout is a resounding yes! In fact, doing cardio after your strength training can be beneficial in many ways. It can help boost your metabolism, burn more calories, and improve your cardiovascular health. However, the timing of your cardio session is crucial.

You should aim to do your cardio after your strength training session or on a separate day entirely. Doing cardio before strength training can lead to fatigue, which can negatively impact your performance during your workout. It's crucial to ensure that you have enough energy to perform your strength training exercises effectively.

Summary of Main Points Related to Can I Do Cardio After a Workout

In summary, doing cardio after a workout is perfectly fine, and it can even be beneficial. However, it's essential to time your cardio session correctly. Aim to do your cardio after your strength training session or on a separate day entirely. Doing cardio before strength training can lead to fatigue, which can negatively impact your performance during your workout.

My Personal Experience with Can I Do Cardio After a Workout

As someone who has been hitting the gym regularly for a few years now, I've experimented with different workout routines. Initially, I used to do cardio before my strength training, thinking it would help me warm up and prevent injuries. However, I noticed that I would often feel fatigued during my strength training, which affected my performance.

After some research and experimentation, I switched to doing cardio after my strength training sessions. It made a significant difference in my energy levels and helped me burn more calories. I found that I was also less prone to injuries and could perform my exercises better.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio After a Workout

As mentioned earlier, doing cardio after a workout can have several benefits. Here are some of the most significant benefits:

Improved Cardiovascular Health

Cardiovascular exercises like running, cycling, and swimming can improve your heart health and help prevent various diseases like heart disease, stroke, and high blood pressure. Doing cardio after your strength training session can help you achieve your fitness goals while also improving your cardiovascular health.

Burn More Calories

Doing cardio after your strength training session can help you burn more calories. Strength training helps build muscle, which can help increase your metabolism. Doing cardio after your strength training session can help you burn more calories and achieve your weight loss goals.

Tips for Doing Cardio After a Workout

Here are some tips to keep in mind when doing cardio after a workout:

Stay Hydrated

It's crucial to stay hydrated during your workout, especially when doing cardio after a strength training session. Make sure to drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout.

Don't Overexert Yourself

It's essential to listen to your body and not overexert yourself. Doing too much cardio can lead to injuries and negatively impact your performance during your strength training session.

Question and Answer

Here are some common questions related to whether you can do cardio after a workout:

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after strength training?

A: It's better to do cardio after strength training to avoid fatigue and ensure optimal performance during your workout.

Q: How long should I wait after strength training to do cardio?

A: It's best to wait for at least 30 minutes after your strength training session to do cardio. This will allow your body to recover and replenish its energy stores.

Q: Can doing cardio after a workout affect my gains?

A: No, doing cardio after a workout won't affect your gains. In fact, it can even help you burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular health.

Q: How much cardio should I do after a workout?

A: The amount of cardio you should do after a workout depends on your fitness goals. It's best to consult a fitness professional to determine the right amount of cardio for your needs.

Conclusion

Doing cardio after a workout is perfectly fine and can even be beneficial. However, it's crucial to time your cardio session correctly. Aim to do your cardio after your strength training session or on a separate day entirely. Doing cardio before strength training can lead to fatigue, which can negatively impact your performance during your workout. By following the tips outlined in this blog post, you can ensure that you get the most out of your workout routine.

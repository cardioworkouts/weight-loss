Which Is Better To Do First Cardio Or Strength Training

Written by Luffy Nov 23, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you confused about which is better to do first: cardio or strength training? If you're looking to improve your overall fitness, it's essential to know the answer to this question. Many people have different opinions on the topic, but it's crucial to understand the science behind it. In this post, we'll explore the benefits and drawbacks of both cardio and strength training and help you decide which you should prioritize.

When it comes to fitness, everyone has different goals, needs, and preferences. Some people want to lose weight, while others want to build muscle, improve their endurance, or just feel healthier. However, one common pain point for many people is time management. If you're like most people, you don't have hours to spend at the gym every day. You want to get the most out of your workouts, and you want to know which is better to do first: cardio or strength training.

The answer to this question depends on your fitness goals. If you're looking to build muscle and strength, you should prioritize strength training. However, if you want to improve your endurance and cardiovascular health, you should prioritize cardio. Both types of exercise are essential for overall fitness, but the order in which you do them can affect how effective they are.

Personal Experience with Strength Training First

Personally, I prefer to do strength training before cardio. When I start my workout with weights, I feel energized and ready to tackle my cardio. I also find that I have more endurance and can push myself harder during my cardio session. It's important to note that everyone is different, and what works for me may not work for you.

Personal Experience with Cardio First

On the other hand, some people prefer to do cardio before strength training. They feel that cardio gets their heart pumping and warms up their muscles, making it easier to lift weights. They also find that they have more energy and can lift heavier weights when they start with cardio. Again, it's essential to find what works best for you and your fitness goals.

The Benefits of Strength Training First

When you prioritize strength training first, you're able to lift heavier weights and build more muscle. When you lift weights, you're breaking down muscle fibers, and when you rest, your body repairs and rebuilds them stronger than before. By doing strength training first, you have more energy and can lift heavier weights, which leads to more muscle growth.

The Benefits of Cardio First

On the other hand, when you prioritize cardio first, you're able to improve your cardiovascular health and endurance. Cardiovascular exercise gets your heart pumping and improves blood flow, which can reduce your risk of heart disease, stroke, and other health problems. When you start with cardio, you're also able to warm up your muscles, which can reduce the risk of injury during strength training.

Personal Experience with Both

Personally, I've tried both approaches, and I've found that both have their benefits. When I prioritize strength training, I feel stronger and more muscular. When I prioritize cardio, I feel healthier and more energetic. That's why I like to switch up my routine and do both types of exercise regularly.

Question and Answer

Q: How long should I do cardio before strength training?

A: It's recommended to do 5-10 minutes of cardio to warm up your muscles before strength training.

Q: How long should I wait after strength training to do cardio?

A: You should wait at least 10-15 minutes after strength training to do cardio to allow your muscles to recover.

Q: Can I do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do both types of exercise on the same day, but it's important to prioritize one over the other depending on your fitness goals.

Q: Should I do cardio or strength training first if I want to lose weight?

A: If you want to lose weight, you should prioritize strength training first. Building muscle increases your metabolism, which leads to more calories burned at rest.

Conclusion of Which is Better to Do First: Cardio or Strength Training

There's no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of which is better to do first: cardio or strength training. It depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. If you want to build muscle and strength, prioritize strength training first. If you want to improve your endurance and cardiovascular health, prioritize cardio. However, both types of exercise are essential for overall fitness, so it's important to find what works best for you and your body.

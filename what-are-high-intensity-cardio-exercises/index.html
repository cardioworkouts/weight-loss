What Are High Intensity Cardio Exercises

Written by Christine Sep 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of doing monotonous cardio exercises that don't seem to make a difference in your fitness level? Do you want to burn more calories in less time? If so, high intensity cardio exercises might be exactly what you need. In this article, we'll explore what high intensity cardio exercises are, their benefits, and some examples of exercises you can try.

What Are High Intensity Cardio Exercises?

High intensity cardio exercises are short bursts of intense exercise that get your heart rate up and challenge your body. They are designed to push you out of your comfort zone and help you burn more calories in less time. Unlike traditional steady-state cardio exercises like jogging or cycling, high intensity cardio exercises require you to work at a near-maximum effort level for a short period of time.

High intensity cardio exercises are challenging but effective. They have been shown to improve cardiovascular health, increase endurance, and burn more calories than traditional cardio exercises. They can also help you break through a fitness plateau and keep your workouts interesting and engaging.

How to Do High Intensity Cardio Exercises

High intensity cardio exercises can be done in many different ways. Here are some examples:

Interval Training

Interval training involves alternating periods of high intensity exercise with periods of rest or lower intensity exercise. For example, you might sprint for 30 seconds, then walk for 60 seconds, and repeat the cycle for 20-30 minutes. You can do interval training with any type of exercise, including running, cycling, or swimming.

HIIT Workouts

HIIT (High Intensity Interval Training) workouts are a type of interval training that involves short bursts of maximum effort exercise followed by periods of rest. These workouts typically last 20-30 minutes and can be done with bodyweight exercises, cardio machines, or weights.

Circuit Training

Circuit training involves doing a series of exercises in a row with little to no rest in between. This type of workout can be high intensity if you choose challenging exercises and move quickly between them. You can do circuit training with bodyweight exercises, weights, or a combination of both.

Sprinting

Sprinting is a high intensity exercise that can be done on a track or treadmill. It involves running as fast as you can for a short distance (usually 20-30 seconds) and then resting for a period of time before repeating. Sprinting can be challenging but effective for improving cardiovascular health and burning calories.

Benefits of High Intensity Cardio Exercises

High intensity cardio exercises have many benefits, including:

  • Improved cardiovascular health
  • Increased endurance
  • Burn more calories in less time
  • Break through fitness plateaus
  • Keep workouts interesting and engaging

Questions and Answers

Q: How often should I do high intensity cardio exercises?

A: It's best to start slowly and gradually increase the frequency and intensity of your workouts. Aim to do high intensity cardio exercises 2-3 times per week and build up from there.

Q: Can high intensity cardio exercises be dangerous?

A: Like any exercise, high intensity cardio exercises can be dangerous if not done properly. Make sure to warm up properly, start slowly, and listen to your body. If you have any health concerns, talk to your doctor before starting a new exercise program.

Q: What are some other benefits of high intensity cardio exercises?

A: In addition to the benefits listed above, high intensity cardio exercises can also help improve insulin sensitivity, reduce blood pressure, and improve overall fitness level.

Q: Do I need any special equipment to do high intensity cardio exercises?

A: No, you can do high intensity cardio exercises with little to no equipment. Bodyweight exercises like burpees, jumping jacks, and mountain climbers can all be done with no equipment. If you want to use weights or cardio machines, however, you will need access to a gym or equipment at home.

Conclusion of What Are High Intensity Cardio Exercises

High intensity cardio exercises are a great way to challenge your body, burn more calories, and improve your fitness level. By incorporating high intensity cardio exercises into your workout routine, you can break through plateaus and see results faster. Just remember to start slowly, listen to your body, and have fun!

Should You Do Strength Training Or Cardio First

Written by Christine Oct 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you confused about whether you should start your workout routine with strength training or cardio? You're not alone. It's a common question among fitness enthusiasts. In this blog post, we will explore the benefits of both and help you decide which one you should do first.

Pain Points of Should You Do Strength Training or Cardio First

One of the main pain points associated with this question is the fear of losing out on the benefits of one exercise over the other. Many people worry that if they do cardio before strength training, they will have less energy for lifting weights. On the other hand, some people believe that strength training before cardio will tire them out for their cardio workout.

Answer to Should You Do Strength Training or Cardio First

The answer to this question is not straightforward, as it depends on your fitness goals. However, studies have shown that if your main goal is to build muscle, you should do strength training first. This is because you will have more energy to lift weights when your muscles are fresh. Additionally, strength training has been shown to increase your metabolism, which will help you burn more calories during your cardio workout.

Conversely, if your main goal is to improve your cardiovascular endurance, you should do cardio first. This is because cardio can help you warm up and get your heart rate up, making it easier to perform strength training exercises. Additionally, doing cardio first can help you burn more calories during your workout, as your body will be in a fat-burning mode.

Personal Experience: Starting with Strength Training

When I first started working out, I used to do cardio before strength training. However, I noticed that I was always too tired to lift weights, and my strength gains were minimal. After doing some research, I switched my routine to start with strength training. I noticed a significant improvement in my strength gains, and I was able to lift heavier weights. Additionally, I found that I had more energy for my cardio workouts, and I was able to burn more calories.

Personal Experience: Starting with Cardio

One of my friends, who is a long-distance runner, always starts her workout with cardio. She says that doing cardio first helps her warm up and get her heart rate up, making it easier to perform her strength training exercises. Additionally, she finds that doing cardio first helps her burn more calories during her workout, as her body is in a fat-burning mode.

Explaining Should You Do Strength Training or Cardio First in More Detail

Strength training and cardio are both essential components of a well-rounded workout routine. Strength training helps build muscle and increase metabolism, while cardio helps improve cardiovascular endurance and burn calories. However, the order in which you perform these exercises can have a significant impact on your results.

If your main goal is to build muscle, you should focus on strength training first. This is because strength training requires a lot of energy and focus, and you want to make sure that your muscles are fresh and ready to go. Additionally, strength training has been shown to increase your metabolism, which will help you burn more calories during your cardio workout.

If your main goal is to improve your cardiovascular endurance, you should focus on cardio first. This is because cardio can help you warm up and get your heart rate up, making it easier to perform strength training exercises. Additionally, doing cardio first can help you burn more calories during your workout, as your body will be in a fat-burning mode.

How to Decide Which One to Do First

Deciding which one to do first depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle, you should start with strength training. If you want to improve your cardiovascular endurance, you should start with cardio. However, if your goal is to lose weight, you should consider doing both in the same workout. This will help you burn more calories and build muscle at the same time.

Personal Experience: Doing Both in the Same Workout

Personally, I like to do both strength training and cardio in the same workout. I start with strength training, doing exercises that target different muscle groups. After that, I move on to cardio, usually running or cycling. I find that doing both in the same workout helps me stay focused and motivated, and I see better results in terms of weight loss and muscle gain.

Question and Answer

Q: Should I always do strength training before cardio?

A: No, it depends on your fitness goals. If your main goal is to build muscle, you should start with strength training. If your main goal is to improve your cardiovascular endurance, you should start with cardio.

Q: Can I do both strength training and cardio on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do both in the same workout. In fact, doing both can help you burn more calories and build muscle at the same time.

Q: How long should I wait between strength training and cardio?

A: It's best to wait at least 30 minutes between strength training and cardio. This will give your body enough time to recover and replenish its energy stores.

Q: Should I do high-intensity interval training (HIIT) instead of traditional cardio?

A: HIIT can be a great way to improve your cardiovascular endurance and burn calories. However, it can also be very intense and put a lot of strain on your body. If you're new to exercise, it's best to start with traditional cardio and gradually work your way up to HIIT.

Conclusion of Should You Do Strength Training or Cardio First

The answer to whether you should do strength training or cardio first depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle, start with strength training. If you want to improve your cardiovascular endurance, start with cardio. However, if your goal is to lose weight, consider doing both in the same workout. Ultimately, the key to a successful workout routine is finding what works best for you and sticking to it.

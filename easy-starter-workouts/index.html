Easy Starter Workouts A Beginner s Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're new to working out, getting started can be overwhelming. You may have heard about the benefits of exercise, but you don't know where to begin. Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this post, we'll give you some easy starter workouts to help you get started on your fitness journey.

Starting a new workout routine can be tough, especially if you're not used to exercising regularly. You may feel intimidated by the gym or unsure of what exercises to do. Plus, you may be worried about not being fit enough or not knowing the proper form. These are all common concerns, but they shouldn't stop you from getting started.

The target of easy starter workouts is to help beginners get comfortable with exercising and build a foundation for more advanced workouts. These workouts are designed to be simple, low-impact, and easy to follow. They will help you develop strength, flexibility, and endurance without overwhelming your body.

In this post, we'll cover four easy starter workouts: walking, bodyweight exercises, resistance band exercises, and yoga. These workouts require minimal equipment and can be done anywhere, making them perfect for beginners.

Walking

Walking is one of the easiest and most accessible workouts for beginners. All you need is a good pair of shoes and some open space. Walking is a low-impact exercise that can help you build endurance, burn calories, and improve your cardiovascular health.

When I started my fitness journey, walking was my go-to workout. I would walk for 30 minutes every day, gradually increasing my speed and distance. It was a great way to get my body moving without overexerting myself. Plus, I loved being outdoors and getting some fresh air.

To get started with walking, all you need to do is put on your shoes and start moving. You can walk around your neighborhood, in a park, or on a treadmill. Start with a 10-15 minute walk and gradually increase your time and distance as you get more comfortable.

Bodyweight Exercises

Bodyweight exercises are another great option for beginners because they don't require any equipment. These exercises use your own body weight as resistance, helping you build strength and endurance. Plus, they can be done anywhere, making them perfect for at-home workouts.

Some examples of bodyweight exercises include squats, lunges, push-ups, and planks. When I first started doing bodyweight exercises, I would follow along with workout videos on YouTube. They were a great way to learn proper form and get a full-body workout in a short amount of time.

To get started with bodyweight exercises, choose a few exercises that target different muscle groups and do them in a circuit. For example, you could do 10 squats, 10 lunges, and 10 push-ups, rest for 30 seconds, and then repeat the circuit. Start with 1-2 circuits and gradually increase as you get stronger.

Resistance Band Exercises

Resistance bands are another great option for beginners because they're inexpensive, portable, and versatile. These bands provide resistance, helping you build strength and tone your muscles. Plus, they're easy to use and can be modified to meet your fitness level.

When I first started using resistance bands, I followed along with workout videos on YouTube. They were a great way to learn new exercises and get a full-body workout. Some examples of resistance band exercises include bicep curls, tricep extensions, and standing rows.

To get started with resistance band exercises, choose a few exercises that target different muscle groups and do them in a circuit. For example, you could do 10 bicep curls, 10 tricep extensions, and 10 standing rows, rest for 30 seconds, and then repeat the circuit. Start with 1-2 circuits and gradually increase as you get stronger.

Yoga

Yoga is a great workout for beginners because it focuses on flexibility, balance, and mindfulness. It can help you reduce stress, improve your posture, and increase your overall well-being. Plus, it can be done anywhere, making it perfect for at-home workouts.

When I first started doing yoga, I followed along with videos on YouTube. They were a great way to learn proper form and get a full-body workout. Some examples of yoga poses include downward dog, Warrior I, and child's pose.

To get started with yoga, find a quiet space in your home and grab a yoga mat. Choose a few poses that feel good for your body and hold them for 30 seconds to 1 minute. You can follow along with a video or create your own flow. Start with 10-15 minutes and gradually increase as you get more comfortable.

Conclusion of Easy Starter Workouts

Starting a new workout routine can be tough, but it's worth it. Easy starter workouts are designed to help beginners build a foundation for more advanced workouts. Walking, bodyweight exercises, resistance band exercises, and yoga are all great options for beginners. Start with one or two workouts and gradually increase as you get stronger. Remember to listen to your body and take rest days when needed. With time and consistency, you'll be on your way to a healthier, happier you!

Question and Answer

Q: How many days a week should I do these workouts?

A: It's recommended to do at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise most days of the week. However, it's important to listen to your body and not overdo it. Start with 2-3 days a week and gradually increase as you get stronger.

Q: Do I need any equipment for these workouts?

A: Walking and yoga require minimal equipment. Bodyweight exercises and resistance band exercises require a mat and resistance bands. These items can be purchased inexpensively online or at a sporting goods store.

Q: How long should I hold each yoga pose?

A: It's recommended to hold each yoga pose for 30 seconds to 1 minute. However, you can modify as needed and hold for less time if it feels uncomfortable.

Q: Can I combine these workouts?

A: Yes! You can combine walking with bodyweight exercises or resistance band exercises for a full-body workout. You can also add yoga as a cool-down or on rest days.

Should You Do Cardio Before Or After Weights To Lose Fat

Written by Frank Sep 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who is trying to lose fat but is confused about whether you should do cardio before or after weights? If yes, then you are not alone. Many people have this confusion, and it is essential to understand what is right for you to achieve your goals effectively.

Pain Points Related to Should You Do Cardio Before or After Weights to Lose Fat

People who want to lose fat often have a hard time figuring out what type of exercise should they do. They don't know whether they should do cardio or weight training. Moreover, if they decide to do both, they get confused about what they should do first, cardio or weight training. This confusion can lead to incorrect exercise routines, which may not be helpful in achieving their goals.

Answering the Target of Should You Do Cardio Before or After Weights to Lose Fat

To answer the question of whether you should do cardio before or after weights to lose fat, the answer is that it depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to lose fat, then it is best to do cardio before weights. Doing cardio first helps to burn more calories, and it also warms up your muscles. This warm-up helps to prevent injury during weight training.

On the other hand, if your primary goal is to build muscle, then it is best to do weights before cardio. Doing weights first helps to build strength, and it also burns calories during and after the workout. This post-workout calorie burn is called the after-burn effect, which helps to burn more calories even after the workout is over.

Summary of Main Points Related to Should You Do Cardio Before or After Weights to Lose Fat

In summary, whether you should do cardio before or after weights to lose fat depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose fat, then it is best to do cardio before weights, and if you want to build muscle, then it is best to do weights before cardio.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Weights

Personally, I have found that doing cardio before weights has helped me achieve my fat loss goals more effectively. When I do cardio first, I feel more energized, and my body is warmed up, which helps me lift weights with better form and no injuries. Moreover, doing cardio first helps to increase my heart rate, which helps me burn more calories during my weight training session.

Benefits of Doing Weights Before Cardio

On the other hand, if your primary goal is to build muscle, then it is best to do weights before cardio. Doing weights first helps to build strength, and it also burns calories during and after the workout. This post-workout calorie burn is called the after-burn effect, which helps to burn more calories even after the workout is over.

Factors to Consider When Choosing Your Workout Order

When choosing your workout order, there are a few factors that you should consider:

  • Your fitness goals
  • Your fitness level
  • Your schedule
  • Your body type

All these factors play an essential role in determining what type of exercise you should do and when you should do it.

Tips on How to Choose Your Workout Order

To choose your workout order, you can follow these tips:

  • Figure out your fitness goals
  • Determine your fitness level
  • Plan your workout schedule
  • Listen to your body
  • Consult a fitness professional

Question and Answer

Q. Can I do cardio and weights on the same day?

A. Yes, you can do cardio and weights on the same day. However, it is recommended to do cardio before weights if your primary goal is to lose fat and weights before cardio if your primary goal is to build muscle.

Q. How long should I do cardio and weights?

A. The duration of your cardio and weight training session depends on your fitness level and goals. It is recommended to do at least 30 minutes of cardio and 30 minutes of weight training for a total of 60 minutes of exercise.

Q. What type of cardio is best for fat loss?

A. Any type of cardio that increases your heart rate is beneficial for fat loss. However, high-intensity interval training (HIIT) has been found to be more effective in burning fat than steady-state cardio.

Q. How many days a week should I do cardio and weights?

A. It is recommended to do at least 3-4 days of cardio and 2-3 days of weight training per week. However, the frequency of your workouts depends on your fitness level and goals.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio Before or After Weights to Lose Fat

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio before or after weights to lose fat depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to lose fat, then it is best to do cardio before weights, and if your primary goal is to build muscle, then it is best to do weights before cardio. It is essential to listen to your body, consult a fitness professional, and choose a workout order that works best for you.

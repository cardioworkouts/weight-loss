Should We Do Cardio Before Or After Workout

Written by Bardi Sep 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who loves to hit the gym and workout but is confused about when to do cardio? You're not alone. Many people struggle with whether they should do cardio before or after their workout. In this blog post, we'll explore the benefits and drawbacks of both options to help you make an informed decision.

The Pain Points of Should We Do Cardio Before or After Workout

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to deciding whether to do cardio before or after your workout is that there is no one-size-fits-all answer. Different people have different goals, and their bodies may react differently to cardio depending on when they do it. Additionally, there are many myths and misconceptions about cardio that can make it difficult to know what to do.

The Answer to Should We Do Cardio Before or After Workout

The answer to whether you should do cardio before or after your workout depends on your goals. If your goal is to build muscle, then doing cardio after your workout is generally better. This is because cardio can deplete your glycogen stores, which can make it harder for your body to recover and build muscle. However, if your goal is to improve your endurance, then doing cardio before your workout may be more beneficial.

Ultimately, the best approach is to experiment and see what works best for you. Try doing cardio before and after your workout for a few weeks each and see how your body responds. You may find that one approach works better than the other, or that a combination of the two is best for you.

Main Points of Should We Do Cardio Before or After Workout

In summary, the main points to consider when deciding whether to do cardio before or after your workout are:

  • Your goals
  • Your body's response to cardio
  • Experimentation to find what works best for you

Personal Experience with Doing Cardio Before or After Workout

Personally, I found that doing cardio before my workout helped me to warm up and get my heart rate up. However, I also noticed that doing cardio after my workout helped me to recover faster and feel less sore the next day. I now do a combination of both, starting with a short cardio warm-up before my workout and then doing a longer cardio session after my strength training.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Your Workout

There are several benefits to doing cardio before your workout. These include:

  • Warming up your body and preparing your muscles for exercise
  • Increasing your heart rate, which can help you to burn more calories during your workout
  • Improving your endurance and stamina

However, it's important to note that doing too much cardio before your workout can deplete your glycogen stores, which can make it harder to build muscle.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio After Your Workout

There are also several benefits to doing cardio after your workout. These include:

  • Helping your body to recover and reduce soreness
  • Reducing stress and promoting relaxation
  • Burning additional calories after your workout

Additionally, doing cardio after your workout can help to prevent injury by helping your body to cool down and gradually return to its resting state.

How to Incorporate Cardio Before or After Your Workout

If you're looking to incorporate cardio into your workout routine, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Start with a short warm-up before your workout to get your heart rate up and prepare your muscles for exercise
  • Consider doing high-intensity interval training (HIIT) or circuit training to combine cardio and strength training
  • Gradually increase the length and intensity of your cardio sessions over time
  • Listen to your body and adjust your routine as needed

The Bottom Line on Should We Do Cardio Before or After Workout

When it comes to deciding whether to do cardio before or after your workout, there is no one-size-fits-all answer. It depends on your goals, your body's response to cardio, and your personal preferences. Experiment with both approaches to find what works best for you, and don't be afraid to mix it up and try new things.

Question and Answer

Q: Can doing cardio before your workout help with weight loss?

A: Yes, doing cardio before your workout can help you to burn more calories and promote weight loss. However, keep in mind that doing too much cardio can also lead to overtraining and injury.

Q: Is it better to do cardio on an empty stomach?

A: There is some evidence to suggest that doing cardio on an empty stomach can help to burn more fat. However, this approach may not be suitable for everyone, especially if you have low blood sugar or are prone to dizziness or lightheadedness.

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio after my workout?

A: The amount of time you should wait to do cardio after your workout depends on your goals and how intense your workout was. If you did a high-intensity workout, you may want to wait a few hours to do cardio to allow your body to recover. However, if you did a lower intensity workout, you may be able to do cardio immediately after.

Q: Can doing cardio after your workout harm your gains?

A: It's unlikely that doing cardio after your workout will harm your gains, as long as you're not doing too much cardio and depleting your glycogen stores. In fact, doing cardio after your workout can help to promote recovery and reduce soreness.

Conclusion of Should We Do Cardio Before or After Workout

Choosing whether to do cardio before or after your workout can be a difficult decision, but ultimately it comes down to your goals and personal preferences. Experiment with both approaches to find what works best for you, and don't be afraid to mix it up and try new things. Remember to listen to your body and adjust your routine as needed, and above all, have fun!

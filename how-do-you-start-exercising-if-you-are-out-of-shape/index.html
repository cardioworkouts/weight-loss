How To Start Exercising If You Are Out Of Shape

Written by Aprilia Oct 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you feeling out of shape and don't know how to start exercising? Do you feel overwhelmed by the thought of getting back in shape? You are not alone. Starting to exercise when you are out of shape can seem like a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. In this article, we will discuss how you can start exercising if you are out of shape and make it a part of your lifestyle.

Starting an exercise routine can be challenging, especially if you haven't exercised in a while. Some common pain points associated with starting to exercise include a lack of motivation, fear of failure, not knowing where to start, and feeling uncomfortable in your own body. These pain points can make it difficult to take the first step and start exercising.

The first step to start exercising if you are out of shape is to set realistic goals. You don't need to run a marathon or lift heavy weights on your first day. Start with small, achievable goals that you can build on. For example, you can start with a 10-minute walk around your neighborhood and gradually increase the time and intensity of the exercise.

In addition to setting realistic goals, find an exercise that you enjoy. It could be dancing, swimming, cycling, or any other activity that gets your heart rate up. When you enjoy the exercise, you are more likely to stick to it and make it a habit.

To make exercising a habit, schedule it into your daily routine. It could be as simple as waking up 30 minutes earlier to do some yoga or going for a walk during your lunch break. By scheduling exercise into your routine, you are more likely to make it a part of your lifestyle.

My Personal Experience

When I first started exercising, I was intimidated by the gym and didn't know where to start. Instead of forcing myself to go to the gym, I started with some at-home workouts using online videos. I found a workout that I enjoyed and started doing it regularly. Eventually, I built up the confidence to go to the gym and try out new exercises.

Find a Buddy

Exercising with a friend or family member can make it more enjoyable and keep you accountable. Find someone who has similar fitness goals as you and make exercise dates. You can go for a walk together or try a new fitness class. Having a workout buddy can make exercising more fun and help you stay on track.

Start Slowly

When you are out of shape, it's important to start slowly and listen to your body. Don't push yourself too hard or you risk injury or burnout. Start with low-impact exercises, such as walking or swimming, and gradually increase the intensity as your fitness level improves.

Set Realistic Expectations

It's important to set realistic expectations when starting to exercise if you are out of shape. Don't expect to see results overnight, and don't beat yourself up if you miss a workout. Consistency is key when it comes to exercise, so focus on making exercise a habit and the results will come.

The Importance of Rest

Rest is just as important as exercise when it comes to getting in shape. Your body needs time to recover and repair after a workout, so make sure to take rest days. Overtraining can lead to injury and burnout, so listen to your body and take a break when you need it.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I exercise if I am out of shape?

A: It's important to start slowly and gradually build up your fitness level. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate exercise most days of the week.

Q: What if I don't like exercise?

A: Find an activity that you enjoy, such as dancing or swimming. Exercise doesn't have to be boring or a chore if you find an activity that you love.

Q: How do I stay motivated to exercise?

A: Set realistic goals, find an exercise that you enjoy, and exercise with a friend. By making exercise a habit and finding ways to make it fun, you are more likely to stay motivated.

Q: Can I exercise if I have a health condition?

A: It's important to talk to your doctor before starting any exercise program, especially if you have a health condition. Your doctor can help you determine what exercises are safe for you and how to modify them to meet your needs.

Conclusion

Starting to exercise when you are out of shape can be challenging, but it doesn't have to be. By setting realistic goals, finding an exercise that you enjoy, and making exercise a habit, you can improve your fitness level and make exercise a part of your lifestyle. Remember to start slowly, listen to your body, and have fun!

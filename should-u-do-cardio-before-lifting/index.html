Should You Do Cardio Before Lifting

Written by Christine Nov 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you trying to figure out if you should do cardio before lifting weights? This is a common question among those looking to improve their fitness and health. Cardio and weightlifting are two of the most popular forms of exercise, and they offer a range of benefits. However, it can be challenging to decide which one to do first. In this post, we will explore the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio before lifting and vice versa. So, let's dive in!

Pain Points Related to Cardio Before Lifting

It's important to understand that both cardio and weightlifting are excellent forms of exercise, but they serve different purposes. Cardio is great for improving overall cardiovascular health, burning calories, and reducing stress. On the other hand, weightlifting is great for building muscle, improving strength, and boosting metabolism. So, it's understandable why people want to know which one to do first. However, doing cardio before lifting can be problematic for some people.

Answer to Should You Do Cardio Before Lifting?

The short answer is that it depends on your goals. If your primary goal is to build muscle and strength, then it's best to do weightlifting first. When you do cardio before lifting, you deplete your energy stores, which can make it challenging to lift weights effectively. Your muscles will be fatigued, and you'll have less energy to perform your lifts. However, if your primary goal is to improve cardiovascular health or burn calories, then it's best to do cardio first.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, whether you should do cardio before lifting depends on your goals. If you want to build muscle and strength, do weightlifting first. If you want to improve cardiovascular health or burn calories, do cardio first. It's essential to understand the benefits and drawbacks of each form of exercise and how they can complement each other.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Lifting

Personally, I like to do cardio before lifting because it helps me warm up and get my heart rate up. I find that it helps me perform better during my lifts because my muscles are warmed up, and I have more energy. Additionally, doing cardio first can help me burn more calories during my workout, which is great for weight loss.

Drawbacks of Doing Cardio Before Lifting

However, there are some drawbacks to doing cardio before lifting. As I mentioned earlier, doing cardio first can deplete your energy stores, making it challenging to lift weights effectively. You may also find that you're too tired to perform your lifts correctly, which can lead to injury. So, it's essential to listen to your body and adjust your workout accordingly.

The Science Behind Cardio Before Lifting

When you do cardio before lifting, your body uses glycogen as its primary energy source. Glycogen is stored in your muscles and liver and is used during high-intensity exercise, such as weightlifting. When you deplete your glycogen stores during cardio, you'll have less energy to perform your lifts effectively. Additionally, doing cardio first can increase cortisol levels, which can lead to muscle breakdown and hinder muscle growth.

Tips for Doing Cardio Before Lifting

If you decide to do cardio before lifting, there are a few things you can do to make sure you're still getting the most out of your lifts. First, make sure you're fueling your body with the right nutrients. Eat a balanced meal that includes complex carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats. This will give you the energy you need to perform your lifts effectively. Second, make sure you're warming up properly. Spend at least ten minutes doing light cardio to get your heart rate up and your muscles warmed up. Finally, listen to your body. If you're feeling too tired or fatigued, adjust your workout accordingly.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after lifting?

A: It depends on your goals. If you want to build muscle and strength, do weightlifting first. If you want to improve cardiovascular health or burn calories, do cardio first.

Q: How long should I do cardio before lifting?

A: It's best to do cardio for 10-15 minutes to warm up your muscles and get your heart rate up.

Q: Can I do cardio and weightlifting on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weightlifting on the same day. However, it's essential to give your muscles time to recover between workouts.

Q: What should I eat before doing cardio before lifting?

A: Eat a balanced meal that includes complex carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats. This will give you the energy you need to perform your lifts effectively.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio Before Lifting

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio before lifting depends on your goals. If you want to build muscle and strength, do weightlifting first. If you want to improve cardiovascular health or burn calories, do cardio first. It's essential to listen to your body and adjust your workout accordingly. Remember, both forms of exercise are great for your health, and they can complement each other when done correctly.

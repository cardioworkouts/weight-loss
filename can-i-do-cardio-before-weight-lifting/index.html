Can I Do Cardio Before Weight Lifting

Written by Petter Sep 17, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you someone who is wondering if you can do cardio before weight lifting? Do you want to know the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio before weight lifting? If yes, then you are at the right place.

Many people are confused about whether they should do cardio before weight lifting or not. They worry that cardio will tire them out and make it harder for them to lift weights. They also wonder if doing cardio before weight lifting will negatively impact their muscle gains.

The answer to the question "can I do cardio before weight lifting?" is yes, you can. However, there are some things you should keep in mind before doing so.

Cardio before weight lifting can help you warm up and get your heart rate up, which can help you perform better during your weight lifting workout. Additionally, doing cardio before weight lifting can help you burn more calories and fat, which can help you lose weight and get in better shape.

However, doing cardio before weight lifting can also tire you out, which can make it harder for you to lift weights. Additionally, doing too much cardio before weight lifting can negatively impact your muscle gains, as your body will use the energy it would have used to build muscle to power your cardio workout instead.

My Personal Experience

In my personal experience, I find that doing a short cardio warm-up before weight lifting helps me perform better during my weight lifting workout. I usually do 5-10 minutes on the treadmill or elliptical to get my heart rate up and get my muscles warmed up.

However, I make sure not to do too much cardio before weight lifting, as I find that doing too much cardio makes me too tired to lift weights effectively. Additionally, I usually do my cardio workout after my weight lifting workout, as I find that doing cardio after weight lifting helps me burn more fat and calories.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Weight Lifting

As mentioned earlier, doing cardio before weight lifting can help you warm up and get your heart rate up, which can help you perform better during your weight lifting workout. Additionally, doing cardio before weight lifting can help you burn more calories and fat, which can help you lose weight and get in better shape.

Another benefit of doing cardio before weight lifting is that it can help you improve your cardiovascular health. Cardiovascular exercise can help strengthen your heart and lungs, which can help you live a longer and healthier life.

How Much Cardio Should You Do Before Weight Lifting?

The amount of cardio you should do before weight lifting depends on your fitness level and goals. If you are new to working out, you may want to start with just a few minutes of cardio before weight lifting to warm up your muscles.

However, if you are an experienced athlete or bodybuilder, you may be able to do more cardio before weight lifting without negatively impacting your muscle gains. Some athletes and bodybuilders do up to an hour of cardio before weight lifting to burn more fat and calories.

Tips for Doing Cardio Before Weight Lifting

If you want to do cardio before weight lifting, here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Don't do too much cardio before weight lifting, as it can negatively impact your muscle gains.

2. Make sure to warm up your muscles before doing cardio or weight lifting.

3. Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout to stay hydrated.

4. Don't skip your weight lifting workout after doing cardio, as weight lifting is essential for building muscle.

Conclusion of Can I Do Cardio Before Weight Lifting

So, can you do cardio before weight lifting? The answer is yes, you can. However, you should be careful not to do too much cardio before weight lifting, as it can negatively impact your muscle gains. Additionally, you should make sure to warm up your muscles before doing cardio or weight lifting and stay hydrated throughout your workout.

A. Yes, doing cardio before weight lifting can help you burn more calories and fat, which can help you lose weight faster.

Q. Should I do cardio before or after weight lifting?

A. It depends on your goals. If your goal is to burn more fat and calories, you may want to do cardio after weight lifting. However, if your goal is to warm up your muscles and get your heart rate up, you may want to do cardio before weight lifting.

Q. Can doing too much cardio before weight lifting hurt my muscle gains?

A. Yes, doing too much cardio before weight lifting can negatively impact your muscle gains, as your body will use the energy it would have used to build muscle to power your cardio workout instead.

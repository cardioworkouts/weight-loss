Songs To Exercise Too A Guide To Finding The Perfect Workout Playlist

Written by Eveline Nov 10, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of working out to the same old boring music? Do you find that your motivation to exercise is dwindling? Look no further than songs to exercise too! A great playlist can be the difference between a mediocre workout and a fantastic one. In this article, we'll explore the benefits of using music to enhance your workouts and provide some suggestions for the best songs to exercise too.

Working out can be a challenging and monotonous activity, especially if you're doing it alone. One way to combat this is by incorporating music into your routine. Music has been shown to increase endurance, improve mood, and distract from feelings of fatigue. Choosing the right songs to exercise too can make all the difference in your workout experience.

So, what makes a good workout playlist? The answer may vary from person to person, but generally, you want to choose songs that have a consistent beat and high energy. This will help you maintain a steady pace and stay motivated throughout your workout. Additionally, you may want to choose songs that have a personal meaning to you or remind you of a positive experience to boost your mood.

In summary, the benefits of using songs to exercise too are numerous, including increased endurance and improved mood. To create the perfect workout playlist, choose songs with a consistent beat and high energy that also hold personal meaning to you.

Top 5 Songs to Exercise Too

1. "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor
This classic 80s rock anthem is a staple in any workout playlist. Its upbeat tempo and motivational lyrics will have you feeling like a champion in no time.

2. "Can't Hold Us" by Macklemore & Ryan Lewis
With its fast-paced beat and catchy chorus, this song is perfect for high-intensity workouts like running or cycling.

3. "Lose Control" by Missy Elliott
This early 2000s hit is the ultimate pump-up song. Its infectious beat and empowering lyrics will have you feeling unstoppable.

4. "Jump" by Van Halen
Another classic rock song, "Jump" is perfect for getting you in the mood to exercise. Its upbeat tempo and iconic keyboard riff will have you jumping up and down in no time.

5. "Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson ft. Bruno Mars
This funky hit from 2014 is a great addition to any workout playlist. Its catchy chorus and fun vibe will keep you energized throughout your workout.

How to Use Songs to Exercise Too for Different Workouts

Depending on the type of workout you're doing, you may want to choose different songs to exercise too. For example:

- Cardio workouts: For activities like running or cycling, you'll want to choose songs with a fast tempo and consistent beat to help you maintain your pace. Songs with a motivational message or catchy chorus can also be helpful for staying motivated.

- Strength training: When lifting weights, you may want to choose songs with a slower tempo to help you focus on your movements. Additionally, songs with a heavy bassline or powerful lyrics can help you feel empowered and ready to tackle your workout.

- Yoga or stretching: For these more relaxing activities, you'll want to choose songs with a slower tempo and calming vibe. Instrumental music or songs with nature sounds can be particularly helpful for creating a relaxing atmosphere.

The Science Behind Songs to Exercise Too

The benefits of using music during exercise have been studied extensively. One study found that listening to music during a workout can increase endurance by up to 15%. Another study found that music can help distract from feelings of fatigue, allowing individuals to exercise for longer periods of time.

Additionally, music has been shown to have a positive effect on mood, reducing feelings of stress and anxiety. This can be especially helpful for those who struggle with motivation to exercise.

How to Create the Perfect Playlist for Your Workouts

When creating a playlist for your workouts, there are a few things to keep in mind:

- Choose songs that have a consistent beat and high energy
- Mix up genres and styles to keep things interesting
- Include songs that have personal meaning to you
- Don't be afraid to switch up your playlist every once in a while to keep things fresh

My Personal Experience with Songs to Exercise Too

As someone who has struggled with motivation to exercise in the past, incorporating music into my workouts has been a game-changer. Having a playlist of songs that I love and that help me stay focused and energized has made all the difference in my workout routine. Whether I'm running, lifting weights, or doing yoga, I always make sure to have some great tunes to keep me going.

FAQs About Songs to Exercise Too

Q: Can listening to music during exercise be distracting?
A: While it's true that music can be distracting, it can also be helpful in blocking out external distractions and helping you stay focused on your workout.

Q: Should I listen to the same songs every time I work out?
A: It's up to you! Some people prefer to have a consistent playlist for their workouts, while others like to mix things up to keep them interesting.

Q: Can I use songs to exercise too for any type of workout?
A: Yes! Whether you're running, lifting weights, or doing yoga, there is a perfect playlist out there for you.

Q: How can I find new songs to add to my workout playlist?
A: There are a variety of ways to discover new music, including browsing streaming services like Spotify or Apple Music, asking friends for recommendations, or attending live music events.

Conclusion of Songs to Exercise Too

In conclusion, incorporating songs to exercise too into your workout routine can have numerous benefits, including increased endurance and improved mood. By choosing songs with a consistent beat and high energy that also hold personal meaning to you, you can create the perfect playlist to take your workouts to the next level. So turn up the volume, and let the music move you!

Should You Do Cardio Or Weightlifting First

Written by Bardi Jun 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you confused about whether you should do cardio or weightlifting first during your workout session? You are not alone. Many people struggle with this question, and it can be difficult to determine which one to prioritize. In this blog post, we will go over the pros and cons of both options, and help you make an informed decision about what works best for your fitness goals.

Pain Points

One of the main pain points related to this topic is that there is no one-size-fits-all answer. The best option for you depends on your fitness goals, current fitness level, and personal preferences. Another issue is that doing both cardio and weightlifting in one session can be time-consuming, and it can be challenging to figure out how to fit everything in.

Should You Do Cardio or Weightlifting First?

If your main goal is to build muscle, then weightlifting should be your priority. Doing cardio first can tire you out, making it harder to lift weights with proper form and intensity. Additionally, weightlifting requires more energy and effort than cardio, so you want to make sure you have enough strength and focus for your lifts. However, if your primary goal is to lose weight, then cardio should be your priority. Doing cardio first can help you burn more calories and fat, making your weightlifting session more effective in building muscle.

Ultimately, the choice is up to you, and you need to determine what works best for your fitness goals and preferences. Some people prefer to alternate between cardio and weightlifting days, while others like to do both in one session. It is essential to experiment and find what works best for you.

Main Points

In summary, when deciding whether to do cardio or weightlifting first, you need to consider your fitness goals and level. If you want to build muscle, prioritize weightlifting, and if you want to lose weight, prioritize cardio. The decision ultimately depends on your personal preferences and fitness goals.

Personal Experience: Weightlifting First

As someone who wants to build muscle, I always prioritize weightlifting during my workouts. I find that if I do cardio first, I am too tired to lift weights with proper form and intensity. Additionally, weightlifting requires more energy and effort, so I prefer to do it when I am fresh and focused.

Personal Experience: Cardio First

On the other hand, my friend Sarah's primary goal is to lose weight. She always starts her workout with cardio, as she finds it helps her burn more calories and fat. She says that doing weightlifting after cardio makes her feel more energized and ready to tackle her lifts.

More Explanation About Cardio or Weightlifting First

Another factor to consider is the duration and intensity of your workout. If you have a lot of time to spare, you can do both cardio and weightlifting in one session. However, if you are short on time, you may want to prioritize one over the other. Additionally, if you are doing high-intensity interval training (HIIT), you may want to do cardio first to warm up your body and get your heart rate up before moving on to weightlifting.

How To Determine What Works Best for You

The best way to determine whether to do cardio or weightlifting first is to experiment. Try doing cardio first for a few weeks and see how it affects your weightlifting performance. Then, switch it up and prioritize weightlifting first and see if you notice any differences in your progress. It is essential to listen to your body and do what feels best for you.

Personal Experience: Alternating Cardio and Weightlifting Days

Personally, I like to alternate between cardio and weightlifting days. On cardio days, I focus on running or cycling, and on weightlifting days, I prioritize lifting heavy weights. I find that this approach helps me stay motivated and engaged, as I get to switch things up and challenge my body in different ways.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio and weightlifting on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do both cardio and weightlifting on the same day. However, you need to determine what works best for your fitness goals and level. If you are short on time, you may want to prioritize one over the other, or you can alternate between cardio and weightlifting days.

Q: Does doing cardio first burn more fat?

A: Yes, doing cardio first can help you burn more fat, as your body uses stored fat for fuel during cardio exercises. However, if your primary goal is to build muscle, you may want to do weightlifting first, as it requires more energy and effort.

Q: How long should I wait between cardio and weightlifting?

A: Ideally, you should wait at least 30 minutes between cardio and weightlifting to allow your body to recover and replenish energy stores. However, this can vary depending on your fitness level and the intensity of your workout.

Q: Should I warm up before doing cardio or weightlifting?

A: Yes, it is essential to warm up before doing any exercise, as it helps prevent injury and prepares your body for the workout. You can do a few minutes of light cardio or dynamic stretching to warm up your body.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio or Weightlifting First

When it comes to the question of whether to do cardio or weightlifting first, the answer depends on your fitness goals and level. If you want to build muscle, prioritize weightlifting, and if you want to lose weight, prioritize cardio. Ultimately, the decision is up to you, and you need to experiment to find what works best for your body.

How Many Calories Does Working Out For 2 Hours Burn

Written by Oliver Oct 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who has been working out for a while but still can't seem to figure out how many calories you're burning? Do you want to know exactly how many calories you can burn in a 2-hour workout session? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle to determine how many calories they're burning during their workouts, and it can be frustrating when you're not seeing the results you want. But don't worry, we're here to help.

Before we get into how many calories you can burn during a 2-hour workout session, let's talk about why this is such an important question. For many people, weight loss is a primary goal of exercise, and understanding how many calories you're burning is essential to achieving that goal. Additionally, knowing how many calories you're burning can help you adjust your diet and make sure you're eating enough to fuel your workouts.

The number of calories you burn during a workout depends on several factors, including your weight, age, gender, and the type of exercise you're doing. On average, a person can burn between 500-900 calories in a 2-hour workout session. However, this number can vary depending on the intensity of your workout and the types of exercises you're doing.

In summary, working out for 2 hours can burn between 500-900 calories depending on your weight, age, gender, and the types of exercises you're doing.

My Personal Experience

As someone who loves to exercise, I've spent a lot of time figuring out how many calories I'm burning during my workouts. When I do a 2-hour workout session, I typically burn around 700-800 calories, depending on the types of exercises I'm doing. I've found that high-intensity workouts like HIIT and cycling burn the most calories for me, while lower-intensity exercises like yoga and Pilates burn fewer calories.

Factors That Affect Calorie Burn

As mentioned earlier, several factors can affect how many calories you burn during a workout. Let's take a closer look at some of these factors:

Weight

Your weight plays a significant role in how many calories you burn during a workout. Generally speaking, the more you weigh, the more calories you'll burn. For example, a person who weighs 200 pounds will burn more calories during a workout than someone who weighs 150 pounds.

Intensity

The intensity of your workout also affects how many calories you burn. High-intensity exercises like running and jumping jacks burn more calories than low-intensity exercises like walking and stretching.

Types of Exercises

The types of exercises you do during your workout can also affect how many calories you burn. Cardio exercises like running and cycling burn more calories than strength training exercises like weightlifting. However, strength training can help increase your metabolism, which can lead to more calories burned throughout the day.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I burn 1000 calories in a 2-hour workout session?
A: It's possible, but it depends on several factors, including your weight, age, gender, and the types of exercises you're doing.

Q: How can I accurately track how many calories I'm burning during my workouts?
A: There are several apps and fitness trackers available that can help you track your calorie burn during workouts.

Q: Does the time of day I work out affect how many calories I burn?
A: There is some evidence to suggest that working out in the morning may lead to more calories burned throughout the day. However, the most important thing is to find a time that works for you and stick with it.

Q: Can I lose weight by working out for 2 hours a day?
A: It's possible, but it depends on several factors, including your diet and the types of exercises you're doing.

Conclusion of How Many Calories Does Working Out for 2 Hours Burn

In conclusion, a 2-hour workout session can burn between 500-900 calories, depending on your weight, age, gender, and the types of exercises you're doing. By understanding how many calories you're burning during your workouts, you can adjust your diet and exercise routine to help you achieve your fitness goals.

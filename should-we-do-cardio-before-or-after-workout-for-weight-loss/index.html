Should We Do Cardio Before Or After Workout For Weight Loss

Written by William Jul 29, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you struggling to lose weight even after working out regularly? Are you unsure whether to do cardio before or after weight training? If so, you're not alone. There's a lot of conflicting information out there about the best way to structure your workout routine for weight loss. In this post, we'll explore the question of whether you should do cardio before or after weight training to help you achieve your weight loss goals.

When it comes to weight loss, there are a few common pain points that people face. First, it can be difficult to find the time and motivation to exercise regularly. Second, it can be confusing to know which exercises are most effective for weight loss. Finally, it can be frustrating when you're not seeing the results you want, even after putting in a lot of effort. By understanding whether to do cardio before or after weight training, you can address all of these pain points and optimize your workout routine for weight loss.

The short answer to the question of whether to do cardio before or after weight training is that it depends on your goals. If your primary goal is to build muscle, then you should do weight training first, followed by cardio. However, if your primary goal is weight loss, then you should do cardio first, followed by weight training.

To understand why this is the case, it's helpful to know a bit about how the body uses energy during exercise. When you do cardio, your body primarily uses carbohydrates as fuel. However, when you do weight training, your body primarily uses stored glycogen in your muscles as fuel. By doing cardio first, you deplete your body's glycogen stores, which means that when you move on to weight training, your body will have to burn fat for energy instead. This can lead to greater weight loss over time.

In summary, the best way to structure your workout routine for weight loss is to do cardio before weight training. This will help you burn more fat and achieve your weight loss goals more quickly. However, it's important to keep in mind that everyone's body is different, and what works for one person may not work for another. It's always a good idea to consult with a personal trainer or other fitness professional to help you design a workout routine that is tailored to your individual needs and goals.

Personal Experience with Cardio Before Weight Training

As someone who has struggled with weight loss for many years, I've tried a lot of different workout routines over the years. However, I've found that doing cardio before weight training has been the most effective for me in terms of achieving my weight loss goals. By depleting my body's glycogen stores with cardio, I'm able to burn more fat during weight training and see greater results overall.

Tips for Doing Cardio Before Weight Training

If you're new to working out or haven't tried doing cardio before weight training before, here are a few tips to help you get started:

1. Start Slowly

If you're new to working out, it's important to start slowly and gradually build up your endurance. Start with just 10-15 minutes of cardio before weight training and gradually increase the time as you get stronger.

2. Mix It Up

Doing the same type of cardio every day can get boring quickly. Mix it up by trying different types of cardio, such as running, cycling, or swimming, to keep things interesting.

3. Stay Hydrated

Cardio can be dehydrating, so it's important to drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout. This will help you stay hydrated and avoid cramping or other issues.

Question and Answer

Q: How long should I do cardio before weight training?

A: This depends on your fitness level and goals. If you're new to working out, start with just 10-15 minutes of cardio before weight training and gradually increase the time as you get stronger.

Q: Is it safe to do cardio before weight training?

A: Yes, it's safe to do cardio before weight training as long as you warm up properly and listen to your body. If you experience any pain or discomfort, stop the workout immediately and consult with a fitness professional.

Q: Can I still build muscle if I do cardio before weight training?

A: Yes, you can still build muscle if you do cardio before weight training. However, you may not see the same gains in muscle mass as you would if you did weight training first.

Q: Do I need to do cardio and weight training on the same day?

A: It's not necessary to do cardio and weight training on the same day, but it can be beneficial for weight loss. If you do choose to do both on the same day, make sure to space them out by at least a few hours to allow your body to recover.

Conclusion of Should We Do Cardio Before or After Workout for Weight Loss

In conclusion, if your primary goal is weight loss, it's best to do cardio before weight training. This will help you burn more fat and achieve your weight loss goals more quickly. However, it's important to listen to your body and consult with a fitness professional to design a workout routine that is tailored to your individual needs and goals.

Should You Do Cardio Before Working Out Legs A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 10, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you wondering whether you should do cardio before working out legs? This is a common question among fitness enthusiasts. While some people swear by doing cardio before leg workouts, others believe that it's better to do strength training first. In this post, we'll explore the pros and cons of doing cardio before working out legs, and help you make an informed decision.

Pain Points

One of the main concerns people have when it comes to doing cardio before working out legs is fatigue. Cardio exercises such as running or cycling can be quite taxing on the body, and if you do them before leg workouts, you may not have enough energy to lift heavy weights or perform squats and lunges effectively. Additionally, some people worry that doing cardio first may cause them to lose muscle mass, since cardio exercises are typically associated with weight loss.

The Answer

The answer to whether you should do cardio before working out legs depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to build muscle mass and increase strength, it's generally better to do strength training first. This is because strength training requires a lot of energy and focus, and you want to make sure that you're not too fatigued to perform your exercises effectively. However, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular fitness or lose weight, doing cardio before leg workouts may be more beneficial.

Ultimately, the best approach is to experiment and see what works for you. Some people find that doing cardio before leg workouts helps them warm up and get in the zone, while others prefer to do it after their strength training session. It's important to listen to your body and adjust your workout routine accordingly.

Main Points

In summary, whether you should do cardio before working out legs depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle mass and increase strength, it's generally better to do strength training first. However, if your goal is to improve cardiovascular fitness or lose weight, doing cardio before leg workouts may be more beneficial. Ultimately, the best approach is to experiment and see what works for you.

Personal Experience: Cardio Before Leg Workouts

As a personal trainer, I've worked with clients who prefer doing cardio before leg workouts and others who prefer doing it after. Personally, I find that doing cardio first helps me warm up and get in the zone. I typically start with a 10-15 minute jog on the treadmill, followed by some dynamic stretching. After that, I move on to my leg workout, which includes squats, lunges, and deadlifts. I find that I have enough energy to perform my exercises effectively, and I'm able to maintain good form throughout my workout.

Personal Experience: Strength Training Before Cardio

On the other hand, some of my clients swear by doing strength training first. They feel that it helps them build more muscle mass and increase their overall strength. They typically start with heavy compound exercises such as squats and deadlifts, and then move on to isolation exercises such as leg curls and leg extensions. After their strength training session, they do some cardio to improve their cardiovascular fitness.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Leg Workouts

There are several benefits to doing cardio before leg workouts. Firstly, it can help you warm up and get your blood flowing, which can reduce the risk of injury. Secondly, it can improve your cardiovascular fitness and endurance, which can help you perform better during your leg workout. Finally, it can help you burn more calories and lose weight, which is great if weight loss is one of your fitness goals.

Tips for Doing Cardio Before Leg Workouts

If you decide to do cardio before leg workouts, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with a light warm-up such as jogging or cycling for 5-10 minutes
  • Do some dynamic stretching to loosen up your muscles
  • Focus on low-intensity cardio such as steady-state running or cycling
  • Avoid high-intensity cardio such as HIIT or sprinting, as this can tire you out too quickly
  • Stay hydrated and fuel your body with the right nutrients before and after your workout

Personal Experience: Strength Training Before Cardio

As a personal trainer, I've also worked with clients who prefer doing strength training first. They feel that it helps them build more muscle mass and increase their overall strength. They typically start with heavy compound exercises such as squats and deadlifts, and then move on to isolation exercises such as leg curls and leg extensions. After their strength training session, they do some cardio to improve their cardiovascular fitness.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it bad to do cardio before leg day?

A: It's not necessarily bad to do cardio before leg day, but it may affect your performance during your leg workout. If you're too fatigued from cardio, you may not be able to lift heavy weights or perform your exercises effectively. However, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular fitness or lose weight, doing cardio before leg day may be more beneficial.

Q: Can you lose muscle by doing cardio before lifting?

A: It's unlikely that you'll lose muscle by doing cardio before lifting, as long as you're fueling your body with the right nutrients and eating enough protein. However, if you're doing too much cardio and not enough strength training, you may experience muscle loss over time.

Q: How long should you wait to do cardio after leg day?

A: It's generally recommended to wait at least 24-48 hours before doing cardio after leg day, to allow your muscles time to recover. However, if you're feeling up to it, you can do some low-intensity cardio such as walking or light cycling to help with recovery.

Q: Is it better to do cardio or strength training first?

A: Whether you should do cardio or strength training first depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to build muscle mass and increase strength, it's generally better to do strength training first. However, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular fitness or lose weight, doing cardio first may be more beneficial.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio Before Working Out Legs

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio before working out legs depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle mass and increase strength, it's generally better to do strength training first. However, if your goal is to improve cardiovascular fitness or lose weight, doing cardio before leg workouts may be more beneficial. It's important to experiment and see what works for you, and to listen to your body and adjust your workout routine accordingly.

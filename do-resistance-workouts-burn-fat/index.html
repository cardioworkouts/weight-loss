Do Resistance Workouts Burn Fat A Simple Guide

Written by Petter Nov 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who has been trying to lose weight but is not seeing any result? Have you been considering resistance workouts to burn fat but are not sure if it’s worth it? If you have been dealing with these questions, then this article is for you.

Before we dive into the details of resistance workouts, let’s first understand what resistance workouts are and how they can help in burning fat.

Resistance workouts are a type of exercise that involves using external resistance to work against the muscles, such as weights or resistance bands. These workouts help in building strength and muscle mass, which can ultimately lead to burning fat. Resistance workouts also have many other benefits, such as improving bone density, reducing the risk of injury and improving posture.

In this article, we will discuss the benefits of resistance workouts in burning fat, the different types of resistance workouts, and some tips for incorporating resistance workouts into your routine.

How Do Resistance Workouts Help Burn Fat?

When you perform resistance workouts, you are working against an external resistance, which causes damage to the muscle fibers. This damage triggers a process called muscle protein synthesis, where the body rebuilds and repairs the damaged muscle fibers. This process requires energy, which means that your body will burn calories even after you have finished your workout.

Resistance workouts also help in building muscle mass, which is important in burning fat. Muscles are metabolically active, which means that they burn more calories at rest than fat. This means that the more muscle mass you have, the more calories you will burn at rest, ultimately leading to burning fat.

Types of Resistance Workouts

There are many different types of resistance workouts that you can incorporate into your routine. Some popular ones include:

  • Weightlifting
  • Bodyweight exercises
  • Resistance band workouts
  • Kettlebell workouts
  • TRX suspension training

It’s important to choose a type of resistance workout that you enjoy and that fits into your lifestyle. Consistency is key when it comes to burning fat and achieving your fitness goals.

Tips for Incorporating Resistance Workouts into Your Routine

If you are new to resistance workouts, it’s important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and frequency of your workouts. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Start with bodyweight exercises and gradually add weights
  • Incorporate resistance workouts into your existing routine
  • Work with a personal trainer to develop a customized workout plan
  • Make sure to rest and recover between workouts

FAQs About Resistance Workouts and Burning Fat

Q: How often should I do resistance workouts to burn fat?

A: It is recommended to do resistance workouts at least two to three times per week, with at least 48 hours of rest between workouts to allow for muscle recovery.

Q: Can I lose weight just by doing resistance workouts?

A: While resistance workouts can help in burning fat, it’s important to combine them with a healthy diet and other forms of exercise, such as cardiovascular exercise, for optimal weight loss results.

Q: Should I do cardio before or after resistance workouts?

A: It’s recommended to do cardio after resistance workouts as it can help in burning more fat. Resistance workouts deplete the body’s glycogen stores, which means that the body will use fat as a source of energy during cardio.

Q: Do I need to lift heavy weights to burn fat?

A: No, lifting heavy weights is not necessary to burn fat. You can still achieve fat loss by performing resistance workouts with lighter weights and higher repetitions.

Conclusion of Do Resistance Workouts Burn Fat

Resistance workouts can be an effective way to burn fat and achieve your fitness goals. By incorporating resistance workouts into your routine and combining them with a healthy diet and other forms of exercise, you can see significant improvements in your body composition and overall health. Remember to start slow, choose a type of resistance workout that you enjoy and work with a personal trainer to develop a customized workout plan for optimal results.

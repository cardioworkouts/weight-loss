What Is The Best Workout Split For Beginners

Written by Frank Nov 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Starting a fitness journey can be overwhelming, especially when it comes to figuring out the best workout split for beginners. With so many options available, it can be challenging to determine which one will work best for you. In this blog post, we will explore the topic of what is the best workout split for beginners and provide some helpful tips to get you started on the right track.

Many beginners struggle with finding a workout split that will help them achieve their fitness goals while also being manageable and sustainable. It can be tempting to jump right into a rigorous, six-day workout routine, but this can lead to burnout and injury. On the other hand, some beginners may feel intimidated by the gym and opt for a less challenging workout split, which may not be effective in achieving their goals.

The best workout split for beginners is one that is well-rounded and customizable to your fitness level and goals. It should include a combination of cardio, strength training, and flexibility exercises to improve overall fitness, prevent injury, and increase endurance.

In summary, the best workout split for beginners is one that includes a variety of exercises to improve overall fitness and is customizable to your goals and fitness level. Now, let's dive deeper into what makes a good workout split and provide some examples to get you started.

The Push-Pull-Legs Workout Split

The push-pull-legs workout split is a great option for beginners as it focuses on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once. This split involves working your pushing muscles, pulling muscles, and leg muscles separately over three different workouts. You can do this split three to six times a week, depending on your fitness level and goals.

For example, on push day, you'll do exercises like bench press, shoulder press, and tricep dips. On pull day, you'll do exercises like pull-ups, rows, and bicep curls. On leg day, you'll do exercises like squats, deadlifts, and lunges.

The Full-Body Workout Split

The full-body workout split is another great option for beginners as it works all of your major muscle groups in one workout. This split can be done two to three times a week, depending on your fitness level and goals.

For example, a full-body workout could include exercises like squats, lunges, push-ups, pull-ups, and planks. You can also add in some cardio at the end of the workout, such as running or cycling.

The Importance of Rest Days

Regardless of which workout split you choose, it is essential to incorporate rest days into your routine. Rest days allow your muscles to recover and prevent injury. Aim for at least one to two rest days per week, depending on your fitness level and goals.

Tips for Creating Your Own Workout Split

If you're not sure which workout split to choose, or if you want to customize your own, here are some tips to get you started:

1. Determine your fitness goals.

2. Choose exercises that target your specific goals.

3. Incorporate a variety of exercises, including cardio, strength training, and flexibility exercises.

4. Consider your fitness level and adjust the intensity as needed.

5. Don't forget to incorporate rest days into your routine.

Question and Answer

Q: How many days a week should I work out as a beginner?

A: It's recommended to start with two to three days a week and gradually increase as you become more comfortable and experienced.

Q: Should I focus on cardio or strength training?

A: It's important to incorporate both cardio and strength training into your workout routine for overall fitness and health.

Q: Can I create my own workout split?

A: Yes, you can customize your own workout split based on your fitness goals and level.

Q: How long should I rest between sets?

A: Rest times can vary, but typically 30 seconds to two minutes is recommended, depending on the exercise and your fitness level.

Conclusion of What is the Best Workout Split for Beginners

The best workout split for beginners is one that is well-rounded, customizable, and sustainable. It should include a combination of cardio, strength training, and flexibility exercises to improve overall fitness and prevent injury. By choosing a workout split that fits your goals and fitness level, you'll be on your way to a healthier, happier you.
